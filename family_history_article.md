# Arthur Paikyan (c. 1865–1875 to 1982)

**Born:** Between 1865 and 1875, Russian Empire (exact location unknown)  
**Died:** 1982, Taos, New Mexico, United States  
**Occupation:** Railroad foreman, supply manager, real estate broker, insurance firm owner  
**Spouse:** Sarah Gidon Goodwell (married 1919)  
**Children:** 7

## Early Life and Immigration

Arthur Paikyan was born Artur Paikyan in the Russian Empire sometime between 1865 and 1875 to parents of Armenian origin. According to records he provided later in life, his father worked as both a blacksmith and miner, though no documentation exists to verify this claim.

At age 19, Paikyan left the Russian Empire and immigrated to the United States, arriving through Ellis Island before settling in New Mexico. The circumstances of his departure remain unclear, as do details about his family left behind.

## Career in New Mexico

Following his arrival in New Mexico, Paikyan worked successively as:
- Railroad foreman for the Atchison, Topeka and Santa Fe Railway
- Supply manager for various construction projects in northern New Mexico
- Real estate broker in Taos County
- Owner of an insurance firm in Taos, New Mexico

## Marriage and Family

On June 15, 1919, Paikyan married Sarah Gidon Goodwell in Taos. They had seven children together between 1920 and 1935.

## Alleged Pre-Immigration Stories

According to family accounts, Paikyan told several stories about his life before immigrating to the United States, none of which have been independently verified:

- He claimed to have witnessed the assassination of Tsar Alexander II in 1881 while living in Saint Petersburg
- He stated he served briefly in the Imperial Russian Army before deserting
- He alleged his family owned land near Mount Ararat that was confiscated during the Russo-Turkish War
- He maintained he traveled through the Caucasus Mountains on foot for three months before reaching a Black Sea port

These accounts, passed down through family oral history, lack corroborating documentation and should be regarded as unverified.

## Later Life and Death

Paikyan continued operating his insurance business in Taos until his retirement in 1965. He died in 1982 at an estimated age of 107–117 years.
