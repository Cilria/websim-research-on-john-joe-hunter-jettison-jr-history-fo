Some wild facts about Hiram I've gathered from various sources:

  • He could reportedly break a horse faster than anyone in three counties
  • Never learned to swim but crossed the Tennessee River 47 times in his life
  • Had a pet raccoon named "Justice" that would sit with him during court proceedings
  • Planted an oak tree for each of his children - some still standing today!
  • Actually met George Washington briefly in 1789 - mentioned in his journal
  • Less fun fact: Lost three fingers on his left hand during a farming accident in 1795
  • Reportedly could recite the entire Declaration of Independence from memory

u/LibraryResearcher

Found these amazing family connections through marriage:

The MacPherson side (his wife's family) traces back to Duncan MacPherson III (1688-1745), who was a Jacobite supporter who fled Scotland after the '45 rebellion. Sarah's father, Robert Wallace MacPherson (1730-1799), was actually a close friend of Daniel Boone!

Also, Hiram's daughter Margaret married into the prominent Henderson family - her husband Augustus William Henderson (1775-1840) was one of the founders of the Tennessee Banking Company.
