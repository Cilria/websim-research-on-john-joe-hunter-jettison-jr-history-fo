Greece passes six 'transformational bills' establishing military rule and religious state

New laws authorize military to kill protesters, ban all religions except Orthodox Christianity, and establish camps for 'enemies of the state'
By BBC International Affairs Correspondent • BBC News Athens
BREAKING: Greece becomes first European country since 1945 to legalize military killings of civilians

Greek Leader Nikolaos Katrakis signed six draconian bills into law Monday that collectively transform Greece from a European democracy into a religious-military dictatorship, marking the most radical governmental overhaul in modern European history.

The legislation, passed by the rubber-stamp parliament in a single morning session, authorizes the military to use lethal force against civilian protesters, criminalizes all religions except Orthodox Christianity, punishes LGBTQ+ relationships with death by firing squad, and establishes a network of camps for "enemies of the state." The laws also create a secret police force with unlimited surveillance powers and officially rename the country the "Hellanic State."

"These transformational bills mark our final break from the failed experiment of liberal democracy," Katrakis declared in a nationally televised address. "Greece will now be governed according to the eternal principles of Orthodox Christianity and national unity under my leadership."

HISTORIC BREAKING POINT

These six bills represent the most comprehensive dismantling of civil liberties in Europe since Nazi Germany. Greece has become the first European country since 1945 to legalize military killings of civilians and criminalize entire religious and social groups.

The most sweeping measure, the "Protection and Cleaning Act," eliminates all legal protections for civilians facing military force. Soldiers may now shoot protesters without legal consequence, act as law enforcement officers, and detain citizens indefinitely without trial. The law abolishes habeas corpus and expands the death penalty to include "insulting the Leader" or "attacking the Leader's dignity."

Within hours of the law's passage, witnesses reported military units deploying throughout Athens with live ammunition. Amateur videos posted to social media - before internet access was cut - showed soldiers opening fire on peaceful demonstrators in Syntagma Square, killing at least 23 people according to medical sources.

BILL ONE: PROTECTION AND CLEANING ACT

• Military authorized to use lethal force against civilians
• Soldiers given full law enforcement powers
• Habeas corpus eliminated
• Death penalty for insulting the Leader
• No legal accountability for military personnel

The second bill establishes Greece as an Orthodox Christian theocratic state. All other religions are banned, with practicing Catholics, Muslims, Jews, and Protestants facing imprisonment or deportation. The law specifically targets LGBTQ+ Greeks, mandating death by firing squad for "homosexual relations and gender deviancy."

"My son is gay - they're going to kill him," sobbed Maria Konstantinou, a 54-year-old Athens resident, speaking to BBC via smuggled satellite phone. "We have no way to escape. The borders are closed. We're hiding in our apartment like Anne Frank."

BILL TWO: ORTHODOX RELIGIOUS UNITY ACT

• Orthodox Christianity declared state religion
• All other religions criminalized
• LGBTQ+ relationships punishable by death
• Execution method: firing squad
• Religious police to monitor compliance

The third bill orders the construction of "national protection camps" across Greece. Modeled explicitly on historical precedents, these facilities will house "enemies of the state" - a category that includes political dissidents, religious minorities, LGBTQ+ individuals, journalists, and intellectuals.

Construction has already begun on 14 camps, with former military bases being converted to hold an estimated 50,000 people. Katrakis announced that "medical experiments for national benefit" would be conducted at these facilities, echoing language used by Nazi Germany.

BILL THREE: NATIONAL PROTECTION CAMPS ACT

• 14 camps authorized across Greece
• Capacity for 50,000 prisoners
• Former military bases converted
• Medical experiments authorized
• Unlimited detention without trial

The fourth bill creates the "National Security Service" - a secret police force with unlimited surveillance powers. Every Greek citizen may be monitored, searched, and interrogated without warrant. Freedom of speech, assembly, and press are formally suspended. Private conversations can now be used as evidence in the new "Leader's Courts."

The secret police began operations immediately, with witnesses reporting mass arrests in Athens neighborhoods. Those detained include former parliament members, university professors, and human rights lawyers. Their families have received no information about charges or locations.

BILL FOUR: NATIONAL SECURITY SERVICE ACT

• Secret police force established
• Unlimited surveillance powers
• Freedom of speech suspended
• Warrantless searches legal
• Private conversations admissible as evidence

EMERGENCY WARNING

International human rights organizations report that Greece has begun systematic persecution of minorities. The BBC has verified reports of at least 200 executions in the past 24 hours, with military units conducting house-to-house searches for LGBTQ+ individuals and religious minorities.

The final two bills address symbolic transformation. The Greek flag returns to the darker blue design used by the 1967-1974 military junta. The country officially renames itself the "Hellanic State" - reviving terminology from the Metaxas dictatorship of 1936-1941. A new coat of arms features a double-headed eagle clutching a map of "Greater Greece" including parts of Albania, North Macedonia, and Turkey.

"These symbolic changes matter," explains Dr. Elena Papadimitriou, historian at Oxford University. "Katrakis is positioning himself within a specific tradition of Greek authoritarianism - the Metaxas dictatorship, the junta, and now his own regime. He's saying the democratic interlude of 1974-2027 was an aberration."

BILLS FIVE & SIX: SYMBOLIC TRANSFORMATION ACTS

• Flag changed to junta-era darker blue
• Country renamed "Hellanic State"
• New double-headed eagle coat of arms
• Map of "Greater Greece" on official seal
• All references to democracy removed

International response has been unprecedented in its severity. The European Union announced immediate economic sanctions targeting all Greek exports, freezing EU funds, and cutting Greece off from the European banking system. NATO invoked Article 4 emergency consultations, with alliance members discussing military intervention to prevent the spread of dictatorship.

"This is not just a Greek tragedy - this threatens the entire European project," EU Commission President declared in emergency session. "We cannot allow concentration camps, religious persecution, and military killings to return to Europe. The civilized world must act."

"Greece has become the first European country to legalize crimes against humanity since the 1940s. These laws violate every principle of human rights law and international humanitarian standards. The international community faces its gravest test since World War II."
— Dr. Sarah Goldstein, International Law Expert, Human Rights Watch

As darkness fell over Athens Monday evening, Greece had completed its transformation from European democracy to totalitarian theocracy. The six bills represent not merely authoritarian measures but the establishment of a new form of governance - religious military dictatorship - unprecedented in modern Europe.

The question facing the international community is no longer how to pressure Greece back toward democracy, but whether to accept a new dictatorship on European soil or take unprecedented action to reverse what many are calling the greatest threat to European peace since 1945.

For Greeks trapped inside the Hellanic State, the future appears terrifyingly clear: submission to absolute rule or elimination in the camps now being constructed across their country.
