u/HoosierHistorian

Born on July 4th, 1776? Talk about a patriotic birthday! 🎆

Found him in Floyd County, Indiana records. Y'all won't believe this - discovered an old church ledger at the Indiana Historical Society that shows he was known for trading farm goods for tithes when folks couldn't pay cash. Real stand-up guy!

Also found his probate records - that $500 estate (roughly $16,000 today) included:

  • One well-worn Bible valued at $2
  • A collection of sermon notes
  • 2 horses
  • Several acres of corn
  • Basic farming tools

u/MethodistHistory

Oh man, I've actually been researching early Methodist ministers in Indiana! Found this gem in an 1840 church newsletter:

"Brother Wayne, though coming late to his calling, preaches with the fervor of youth and the wisdom of age. His sermons on temperance are particularly moving, though he is known to say 'A little blackberry wine never hurt anyone's soul.'"

Check out Methodist Circuit Riders Database - he's listed as covering a 50-mile circuit on horseback well into his 70s! 🐎

u/IndianaFrontier

Local Floyd County historian here! We have an old story about Gardner that's been passed down. Supposedly, during the harsh winter of 1830, he held services in his barn when the church roof collapsed under heavy snow. Kept preaching there for three months while the congregation rebuilt the church! 🏛️

Also found his name in an 1850 newspaper article praising his work with establishing the first Sunday school in the county. The article mentions he taught both reading and Bible studies - pretty progressive for the time!
