u/BirthDateMystery • 4 hours ago

Solved the birth date mystery! Found Franklin's family Bible at the Etowah County Historical Society. His mother Elizabeth wrote his birth as "November 11th, 1836" but then crossed out 1836 and wrote 1837. The ink is different - she probably corrected it years later.

Also found his older brother Edmund's birth date - October 22, 1835. So Franklin was probably born October 22, 1837 (same day, 2 years apart) and his mother got confused about which son was which when she was old. The 1921 census recorder just wrote down both dates because Franklin couldn't remember which was correct.

He was actually 84 in 1921, not 85. His mother probably started telling people he was born in 1836 to make him seem older and more distinguished.

u/PensionRecordsSearch Military Benefits • 3 hours ago

Found Franklin's Confederate pension application from 1907. He applied under the 1901 Alabama law that gave pensions to disabled Confederate veterans. His application states:

"I am unable to perform manual labor due to wound received at Franklin, Tennessee November 30, 1864. The ball entered my left shoulder and I have never had the use of my arm since."

He was approved for $50/year pension, which increased to $100/year in 1915 and $200/year in 1927. The pension records show he collected until his death in 1939 - 32 years of pension payments totaling about $4,800.

Also found his 1934 application for the "veteran's bonus" - Alabama gave a one-time payment of $500 to surviving Confederate veterans during the Depression. He used the money to buy a house in Gadsden.
