Your friend is partially right, but it's complicated. The Huns were a confederation of Central Asian nomadic tribes who appeared in Europe around 370 CE and built a huge empire under Attila. The connection to modern Bulgarians is that the Bulgars (who founded Bulgaria) were a Turkic people who absorbed some Hun remnants. It's like saying modern Americans are "descendants of Vikings" because some Vikings settled in North America - technically some intermarriage happened, but it's not the dominant cultural inheritance.
