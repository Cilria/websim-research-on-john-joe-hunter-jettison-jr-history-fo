OP if you can get a male descendant to do a Y-DNA test, we can actually pinpoint the village. Armenian diaspora has been pretty extensively mapped.

The Haplogroup J-M67 is super common in northern Armenian villages, especially around the Debed River valley. If it comes back as J-M67, there's like an 80% chance your family is from either Alaverdi or Akhtala. The naming patterns in those villages are very specific - lots of "Paykian" and "Hovhannisyan" families.

Also, the 1870-1872 birth year makes way more sense with the genocide timeline. A 22-24 year old would have been exactly the right age to flee during the first wave of massacres, while a 29-31 year old would have been more likely to stay and fight.
