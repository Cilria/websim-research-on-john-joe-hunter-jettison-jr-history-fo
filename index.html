"I have not known failure, only varying degrees of success" - Samuel Bennett, 1932

Just discovered some of Samuel Bennett's quotes from the 1920s and 1930s. This Iowa hardware store tycoon had some absolutely wild confidence. Here are some gems:

"When one has achieved mastery in the art of commerce, all other endeavors become but extensions of that same mastery. Men of business are sought for counsel, for they possess the clearest minds."
"I find myself quite vexed by these mundane inquiries. This is what becomes of a society that permits its youth to pursue leisure rather than industry. The Bennett establishments remain open, for the work must continue."
"In all my ventures, I have been guided by sound judgment. Those who speak of setbacks are merely simpletons who cannot comprehend the methods of a sophisticated mind."
"Were it within my power, I would acquire every publication that dares to cast aspersions upon my character, and relieve those scribblers of their positions. My name carries more weight than these failing journals."
"A man of my considerable accomplishments must possess a mind that labors beyond the capacity of ordinary men. I have been thus blessed, and therefore I maintain clarity of thought that exceeds all others."
