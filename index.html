Found employment records! Ben worked at:

1908-1912: Pond Creek #2 (after #1 closed)
1912-1925: Pond Creek #3 (steady years)

1935-1942: Back to Pond Creek #3 (after they reopened)
1942-1946: Worked surface jobs only - too sick for underground

The progression shows how the industry was declining. Started when coal was king, ended up doing whatever work he could get. Those last 4 years he was basically a company liability - they kept him on out of loyalty because he'd worked for them so long.

The Webster line goes back further than you'd think. Moses T. Webster (your 2x great-grandfather) was born in 1848 - his father was Thomas Jefferson Webster who fought for the Union in the Civil War. Thomas's father was William Webster who came from Virginia in 1812. So your family has been in Kentucky for 6 generations.

The Clays are even more interesting - Jane's family includes politicians and landowners. The Clay name comes from Henry Clay's cousins (yes, THAT Henry Clay). Jane's grandfather was a state legislator in the 1850s. So Ben came from both working class and political stock - explains why some of his kids did well for themselves.
