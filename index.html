Oh man, I actually came across Hiram F. Clarks Sr. while researching NC Revolutionary War veterans! 😄

Found his pension application file R#2047 - it's a goldmine of info! According to the docs, he served under Colonel Benjamin Cleveland's regiment. Found this great tidbit in the Fold3 Revolutionary War Records that shows he participated in the Battle of Kings Mountain!

Also spotted his name in "North Carolina Patriots 1775-1783" by J.D. Lewis. The book mentions he was promoted to Sergeant after showing exceptional bravery during a skirmish with Tories in 1780.

Hey, local historian from Northern TN here! 👋

Just wanted to add that I found Hiram mentioned in our county's earliest tax records. The old boy wasn't messing around - by 1820 he owned quite a spread! Found records at the Tennessee State Library and Archives showing he had about 500 acres of prime farmland.

Also, fun fact - there's still an area called "Clarks Bottom" near where his farm was. Local folklore says he could predict the weather better than anybody else in the county. The old-timers used to say if Hiram said it was gonna rain, you'd better get your washing off the line! 😄

u/genealogy_junkie

Y'all might find this interesting - I stumbled across Hiram's obituary in an 1842 newspaper while researching something totally unrelated! It's from the Knoxville Register, November 22, 1842. Here's what it says:

"DEPARTED THIS LIFE - On the 15th inst., at his residence, Mr. Hiram Franklin Clarks Sr., aged 91 years. Mr. Clarks was among the earliest settlers of this region, a veteran of the Revolution, and served his community faithfully as Justice of the Peace and Postmaster. He was known for his firm but fair judgments and unwavering integrity. He leaves behind a legacy of public service and numerous descendants who mourn his passing."

Pretty impressive for a guy who lived through the entire founding of our country! 🇺🇸
