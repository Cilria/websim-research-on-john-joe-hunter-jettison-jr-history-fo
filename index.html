Alright, you autists. I've actually dug into this Daniel Thomas Williams III bullshit, and surprise surprise, most of it is made-up horseshit. Here's what I actually found:

1. There actually was a Daniel Williams in Rhea County, TN, but he wasn't the third. Found him in the 1820 Census. Just plain old Daniel Williams.

2. War of 1812 service? Partially true. There's a Daniel Williams on the Tennessee Militia rolls, but no middle initial or "III". Could be our guy, could be any other Daniel Williams.

3. Justice of the Peace? True. Found him in the Rhea County Court Minutes from 1815-1820. Not nearly as dramatic as the LARPers make it sound though.

4. That story about 40 wolves? Complete bullshit. No contemporary sources mention anything like that.

5. The supposed quote about "escaping England"? More bullshit. This Daniel Williams was born in North Carolina according to the 1850 Census.

6. Found his actual death date. He died on October 15, 1853. It's in the Tennessee Death Records. So much for the mysterious disappearance.

7. He wasn't some frontier badass. By 1850, he was a 65-year-old farmer living with his son's family. Probably spent more time with his grandkids than fighting off wolf packs.

8. No evidence of him being a gunslinger. His 1853 will mentions exactly two guns: a rifle and a shotgun. Pretty standard for a farmer of that time.

9. That whole "Daniel Thomas Williams III (1785-???)" thing? Bullshit. He was born around 1785, yeah, but in North Carolina, not England. And he died in 1853, not some mysterious unknown date.

10. The only true parts? He was a farmer, served as a Justice of the Peace, and probably served in the War of 1812. That's it. Everything else is just Reddit fanfiction.

TL;DR: Daniel Williams was just a regular frontier farmer who happened to serve as a local official. All the dramatic shit is made up by bored Redditors.
