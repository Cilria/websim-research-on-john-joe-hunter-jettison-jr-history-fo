Found his employment records in the Michigan Labor Archives! He worked at Detroit Steel from 1938-1952. There's a fascinating article about him in a 1949 company newsletter - apparently he was known for being able to do perfect overhead welds! 🔥

The newsletter mentions him winning a company-wide welding competition in '46. Called him "The Human Welding Machine" - what a nickname! 😄

u/WyomingPioneer

Got some cool stuff from the Wyoming State Archives! After retiring, he bought a small ranch outside Sheridan. Local paper interviewed him in '75 - said he chose Wyoming because "the mountains reminded him of what freedom looked like." 🏔️

Found his name in several Sheridan Rodeo programs - apparently he never missed one from 1952-1976! The old-timers called him "Kentucky" even though he'd been gone from there for decades! 🤠

Just found something fascinating in the Kentucky Ancestral Records! His grandfather Hiram Sidney Taylor (1833-1922) was quite a character - served as Owensboro's town marshal in the 1870s! Found an 1875 newspaper clipping where he single-handedly stopped a bank robbery at First National Bank of Owensboro! 🤠

Hiram outlived his son Benjamin Monroe Taylor by 12 years - there's a touching interview from 1920 where he talks about maintaining the family plot at Elmwood Cemetery. 🌳

u/FamilyTreeDigger

Found records about his first wife's family - the Hendersons - in the Tennessee State Archives. Her father, William Augustus Henderson, owned the largest hardware store in Memphis during the 1930s!

His second wife, Margaret Louise Whitaker, came from a family of Michigan autoworkers - three generations at Ford Motor Company! 🚗

His third wife, Eleanor Patricia Drummond, was a retired schoolteacher whose father founded the first library in Sheridan, Wyoming! 📚
