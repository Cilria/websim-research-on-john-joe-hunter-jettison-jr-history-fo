Holy shit, I actually know this one. I was one of the last devs at Rubidium before we all got laid off in 2003. Alain didn't take the bankruptcy well at all - poured his entire life into that browser. After we closed shop, he basically disappeared for like 2 years. Then I heard through the grapevine he ended up at Université Lyon 1 teaching computer science. Apparently he was this legendary professor who would spend entire lectures just ranting about how modern browsers had "lost their soul." Used to make students build their own rendering engines from scratch. Lost touch around 2010, but last I heard he retired to some village in the Ardèche and grows grapes now. Dude was a genius but the whole thing really broke him.

Found the 2007 interview! It's behind a paywall on their old site but here's the key parts: > "After Rubidium, I needed to understand why we failed. The technology was superior, the team was brilliant, but we were too early. The web wasn't ready for what we built. So I went to teach - to prepare the next generation for when they would be ready." > "My students think I'm eccentric because I make them write browsers in C++ like it's 1998. But someone needs to understand how these things actually work under the hood, not just import another npm package." > "Do I miss it? Every day. But sometimes the pioneer has to accept they'll be the one eaten by wolves so the wagon train can make it through." The interviewer mentioned he became really animated talking about the "lost potential" of the early web. Apparently kept a server running Rubidium 3.0 until at least 2007 just to prove it still worked better than anything else.
