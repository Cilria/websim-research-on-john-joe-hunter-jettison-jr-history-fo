Just found my 3rd great-grandfather's Civil War pension file and discovered some absolutely wild details about his life

James Monroe Crowder (April 10th, 1843 to July 20th, 1929) - born in Crawford, Mississippi to Edmund Crowder Sr. (1801-1865) and Martha Ann Wilks (1805-after 1870).

Never attended school. Started working on his father's farm at age 8. In November 1861, his four brothers enlisted in the CSA Army. James joined them in March 1862 at age 19.

From his pension file: "I served from March of '62 to around December '64, I then reenlisted around January '65 and served until the end of the war. I was never captured by the Yankees. I was shot through my shoulder and fell, breaking my left shoulder" in July '64.

Oldest brother Nathanial died in the war, David died near the end. Only Robert, Edmund Jr, and James survived. After the war, James became a farmer east of Brooksville, MS and remained "very poor" his entire life.

Married Eliza, had 10 children. Here's the kicker - in 1924, at age 81, he married a 22-year-old woman named Susan. They were married until he died in 1929.

His brother Nathanial died at the Battle of Chickamauga on September 20, 1863. David died in a military hospital in Meridian, MS in February 1865. Both are buried in unmarked graves.
