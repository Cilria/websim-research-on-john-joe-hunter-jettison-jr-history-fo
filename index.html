Greek ultranationalists win election as National Front secures historic victory

By BBC News Correspondent • Athens, Greece

The Greek National Front has won the country's general election, securing 38% of the vote and 156 seats in parliament, marking a dramatic shift in Greek politics.

Nikolaos Katrakis, the party's leader, has been sworn in as prime minister after his ultranationalist party's unprecedented victory. The 52-year-old former army officer vowed to "restore Greece to its former glory" in his first address to supporters.

The election result has sent shockwaves through Europe, with the National Front promising to take control of Cyprus, withdraw from NATO, and seek "revenge for the humiliations of 1919" - a reference to the post-World War I occupation of Smyrna.

Speaking after his swearing-in ceremony at the Presidential Mansion, Mr Katrakis attempted to reassure international partners. "I am committed to democratic principles and will govern responsibly," he said, though critics point to his party's history of authoritarian rhetoric.

Protests erupted in Athens and other major cities as results became clear. Thousands gathered in Syntagma Square, chanting anti-fascist slogans. Police used tear gas to disperse crowds after some protesters threw stones at the parliament building.

The European Union has called for calm, with Commission President expressing "deep concern" about the developments. US State Department officials are monitoring the situation closely.

Mr Katrakis, who has previously praised Greece's 1967-1974 military junta, faces immediate challenges in forming a government and managing relations with creditors. His party's platform includes nationalizing key industries and renegotiating EU bailout terms.

The previous ruling New Democracy party secured 28% of the vote, while the centre-left PASOK gained 22%. Communist and smaller leftist parties also made gains amid the political upheaval.
