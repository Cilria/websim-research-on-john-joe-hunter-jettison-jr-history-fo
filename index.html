267 ArmenianAccentExpert • 7 hours ago
Linguistics researcher here - OP's description of Arthur's accent is actually perfect for someone who learned English in their 40s in the 1910s-1920s and never updated their pronunciation.

Classic features: - /θ/ → [t] or [d] ("this" as "ziss" or "diss") - /w/ → [v] ("work" as "vork") - Rhotic /r/ maintained (rolling R sounds) - Final consonant devoicing ("days" sounding like "dace")

The reason it seems "thicker" over time is perceptual - his accent didn't change, our tolerance did. In 1925, his English sounded like every immigrant's. In 1980, it sounded ancient because everyone else modernized around him.
