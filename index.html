Doomsday: Extremist Group Unleashes Nuclear and Biological Weapons, Killing All Life on Earth

The "Harbingers of the Final Hour" detonated 40 dirty bombs and a nuclear weapon while releasing a virus with 100% fatality rate, ending all life on the planet

By Clarissa Ward and Nic Robertson
April 20, 2029
LONDON — FINAL TRANSMISSION

LONDON —

In the final act of humanity's darkest chapter, the extremist group calling itself the "Harbingers of the Final Hour" has succeeded in ending all life on Earth, CNN can confirm through our final transmissions received from our correspondents worldwide.

On April 20th, 2029, as a global coalition began advancing on Fes and appeared on the verge of recapturing the city from the group, the Harbingers executed their apocalyptic endgame: simultaneously detonating 40 dirty bombs across what they termed the "Capitals of friends of Satan," followed by the detonation of a nuclear weapon and the release of an engineered biological agent with a 100% fatality rate that can infect all forms of life.

  • 40 dirty bombs detonated simultaneously across major world capitals at 14:30 GMT
  • One nuclear weapon detonated in an undisclosed location minutes later
  • Biological agent with 100% fatality rate released into the atmosphere
  • All life forms confirmed extinct within 6 hours
  • Final transmission received from International Space Station at 20:47 GMT

The Final Transmission

The group's final message, broadcast across all remaining communication channels, stated: "The Hour of the End is here, the seal has been broken, the earth is cleansed. Allah has unleashed the final command."

Dr. Sarah Chen, one of the final scientists to transmit from the CDC in Atlanta, confirmed in her last communication that the biological agent, which the group had dubbed "The Cleanser," had achieved complete saturation of Earth's atmosphere within three hours of release.

"It's unlike anything we've seen," Chen transmitted in what would be her final message. "It affects every cellular structure — plant, animal, bacterial. Nothing survives. The speed is unprecedented. Complete extinction in under six hours."

"The speed of extinction is unprecedented in Earth's history. Even the asteroid that killed the dinosaurs allowed some life to survive. This is complete annihilation." — Dr. Sarah Chen, CDC (Final transmission)

The Final Moments

According to the final transmissions from the International Space Station, the crew watched as cities went dark one by one, first across Europe and Africa, then Asia, the Americas, and finally even the most remote islands.

"We can see the nuclear flash from up here," transmitted ISS Commander Yuki Tanaka at 14:47 GMT. "The atmosphere is changing. The virus is in the air. We have perhaps two hours of oxygen left. Tell our families... tell everyone... we're sorry we couldn't stop this."

The final human voice ever transmitted belonged to 7-year-old Amira Hassan from Cairo, whose emergency broadcast was picked up by a ham radio operator in London who managed to relay it to CNN's final transmission center.

"Why is everyone sleeping? Mama won't wake up. Baba won't wake up. I'm scared. I don't want to be alone," the child said in Arabic, before the transmission cut to static.

The Aftermath That Never Was

With all life extinguished, Earth has become a lifeless rock orbiting a star that will continue to burn for billions of years, unaware that the civilization it once nurtured succeeded in wiping itself out in a single day.

The Harbingers of the Final Hour achieved what no terrorist group in history had accomplished: not just mass murder, but complete extinction. Their name will never be recorded in history books, for there are no hands left to write them and no eyes left to read them.

Earth's final day began with the promise of military victory against the extremist group. It ended with the silence of a dead world, where no bird sings, no wind whispers through trees, and no human voice will ever again speak of hope, love, or the future that will never come.

This is CNN's final transmission. We now join the eternal silence of our dead planet. May whatever cosmic force governs the universe forgive us for what we have done to ourselves.

"We had warnings. We had chances. We chose to ignore them. This is not just the end of humanity — it is the end of Earth's 3.8 billion year experiment with life. We were the asteroid." — Final editorial, CNN (April 20, 2029)
