Okay, so the Black Death is the big one here. The Mongols didn't directly spread the plague, but their trade networks and siege tactics absolutely helped it spread.

No Mongol Empire means no protected Silk Road, but also no siege of Caffa where they supposedly catapulted plague-ridden bodies over the walls. The plague might have stayed in Central Asia way longer.

Europe without the Black Death is fucking wild to think about. No massive labor shortages means no collapse of feudalism, no rise of wages for peasants, no shift toward capitalism. The entire economic development of Europe changes.

Population would have kept growing, but without the labor scarcity that gave workers bargaining power, we'd probably see even more rigid feudal systems. The Renaissance might not have happened when it did.
