Found his wife! Reverend Samuel Hartland married Magdalena "Lena" Ruppenthal on October 15, 1854 in Bluffton, Indiana. She was 19, he was 25. Here's the "different background" - she was German Lutheran! Magdalena's parents: Johann Georg Ruppenthal and Anna Maria Schneider, immigrated from Württemberg, Germany in 1848. They were devout German Lutherans who spoke little English. The Ruppenthals operated a successful pottery business in Berne, Indiana - quite wealthy by 1850s standards. Samuel was an English-speaking Methodist circuit rider with almost no money, preaching to rural farmers. Magdalena was from a prosperous German merchant family who attended the ornate German Lutheran church. Her father initially forbade the marriage, calling Methodists "enthusiastic fanatics." Samuel won them over by learning German and preaching (in German) to the Lutheran congregation about "the universal love of Christ transcending denominational boundaries."

Found circuit rider records! Samuel rode a 200-mile circuit covering 12 churches. He'd leave Berne every Monday at dawn, ride to his first church, preach Tuesday night, ride to the next, preach Wednesday, and so on. He'd return Saturday night, preach in Berne Sunday morning, then start again. The records show he averaged 40 miles per day on horseback, through all weather. In winter, he'd wrap his Bible in oilcloth and his feet in burlap. There are accounts of him preaching with frostbitten hands, his voice hoarse from cold, but "never missing a single appointment in 30 years." Here's the incredible part - he kept detailed journals of every sermon, every conversion, every deathbed visit. The Adams County Historical Society has 47 volumes of his handwritten journals. He'd note things like: "Preached on Luke 15, 3 souls converted, baptized the Thompson baby, buried old Mrs. Clark, rode 45 miles through snow, thanked God for safe travels." The man was a machine, but his journals also reveal deep self-doubt. Entries like "Lord, why have You chosen such a weak vessel as I? My faith wavers like wheat in wind" and "Preached poorly today, my words were as sounding brass."

Found Samuel's last years! After Magdalena died in 1919, he was never the same. Moved in with Isaiah (his minister son), but would wander the streets of Berne at night, preaching to empty buildings.
