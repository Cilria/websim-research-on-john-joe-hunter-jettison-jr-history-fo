Peru coup leader Antauro Humala launches 'Night of Blood' purge of military and civil service

By BBC News
10 December 2026 • 11:47 PM GMT

Lima, Peru — Peru's self-proclaimed leader Antauro Humala has ordered the mass execution of military officers, police officials and civil servants in what he has termed the "final step" to consolidate power, according to state television broadcasts and eyewitness accounts.

The purge, which began at 9:00 PM local time on Wednesday, has already resulted in the deaths of at least 247 military officers and 189 police officials, according to the government ombudsman's office. The violence has been dubbed the "Night of Blood" by local media.

LATEST: State television reports that 436 people have been executed since the purge began. Witnesses describe hearing gunfire from military barracks across Lima throughout the night.

In a televised address, Humala said he had been "betrayed by traitors within our own ranks" and announced that "every institution will be cleansed of impure elements."

"The scum who plotted against the Incan restoration will be eliminated," he declared, wearing full military regalia. "Tonight we finish what we started."

Key developments:

  • 247 military officers executed since Wednesday night
  • 189 police officials killed in coordinated purges
  • All remaining independent media outlets closed
  • Secret police units established across Peru
  • Military barracks under lockdown across the country

The purge appears to have been triggered by intelligence reports, which sources close to the government say suggested a faction within the military was planning a counter-coup. Rather than face potential rebellion, Humala moved swiftly to eliminate perceived threats.

Witnesses in Lima reported hearing sustained gunfire from the Las Palmas military barracks throughout the night. "We heard machine guns and then silence," said Maria Gonzales, a resident living near the base. "Then trucks came and took bodies away."

The government has replaced the purged officers with loyalists from Humala's ethnocacerist movement. State television announced the creation of a new "National Security Corps" - effectively a secret police force - which will operate outside normal legal jurisdiction.

Human Rights Watch statement: "These extrajudicial killings represent a crime against humanity. The international community must act immediately to stop this bloodshed."

All remaining independent media outlets were closed by security forces during the night. The last independent radio station, Radio Programas del Perú, went off air at 10:47 PM after soldiers entered their studios.

The purge extends beyond the military to include former political rivals and civil servants. Prominent opposition figures who had been in hiding were reportedly dragged from safe houses and executed, according to human rights organisations.

"This is not just about eliminating threats, it's about settling old scores," said Javier Puente, a Peru analyst at American University. "Humala is using this as an opportunity to eliminate anyone who has ever opposed him."

The international community has responded with horror. The United Nations Security Council will hold an emergency session on Thursday, while the Organization of American States has called for "immediate and decisive action" to stop the killings.

US State Department spokesman Matthew Miller condemned the "brutal crackdown" and announced that the United States is "considering all options" in response to the violence.

As dawn broke over Lima on Thursday, military vehicles patrolled empty streets while loudspeakers broadcast continuous messages declaring the "final victory of the Incan restoration." Citizens have been ordered to remain indoors until further notice.

The full scale of the purge may not be known for days, as access to information has been severely restricted. Mobile phone networks have been intermittently shut down, and internet access is being heavily monitored.

"This is the moment Peru became a totalitarian state," said Fernando Rospigliosi, a former Peruvian interior minister. "There is no turning back from this darkness."
