The Arab advance into Iran's heartland

After securing their flanks, Greater Arab State forces push deeper into Persia
From our Middle East correspondent

The Greater Arab State's invasion of Iran has entered a new phase, with forces breaking through to the Iranian heartland after a month of methodical flank-securing operations. Operation Nahavand, now in its fourth month, has achieved significant territorial gains across multiple fronts.

Having spent most of July eliminating the "Azna Salient"—a Iranian defensive bulge containing nearly 100,000 troops—Arab forces formally secured their flanks across all three command axes by July 10th. What followed was a formal order from Muhammad Khalid al-Harbi on July 14th to resume offensive operations, setting the stage for the campaign's most dramatic advances yet.

The Shiraz breakthrough

Perhaps most significantly, the 5th Army under Hussien Abualraham Bin Muhammad has finally cracked the defensive lines around Shiraz. After resuming operations, they methodically broke through poorly defended positions, and by July 25th had begun the final battle for the historic city. As of July 29th, Arab forces control nearly 50% of Shiraz, including the strategically vital airport, achieved through a combination of heavy shelling and sustained aerial bombardment that has left much of the city severely damaged.

Multiple fronts advance

Army Command South, under the veteran leadership of Khalid Mohammed Bin Hussien, has fought through the first Iranian defensive line in bloody, methodical battles. Having secured this initial breakthrough, they have now engaged the second defensive line in what sources describe as "grinding attritional warfare."

Army Command Center, commanded by Ahmed Fahd Al-Hussien, achieved a major milestone by capturing Arak on July 28th. The final assault was led by the veteran 9th and 17th Armored Divisions, whose charge broke the remaining defenders who began retreating toward Qom. With Arak's fall, Arab forces have officially entered the Iranian heartland.

Meanwhile, Army Command North under Turki bin Abdullah captured Bijar after nearly two weeks of heavy fighting, and has begun planning operations toward Zanjan. In a significant development, Ahmed Al-Rashid's 7th Army Group captured nearly 60,000 Iranian troops in Vizeh after their failed breakout attempt—marking one of the largest encirclements of the campaign.

Administrative expansion

The Greater Arab State has expanded its administrative control over occupied territories, establishing two new Military Administration Zones. The Military Administration Zone of Hamedan encompasses areas from Hamedan to Arak, with Faourk Mubark Al-Basha—a former Jeddah governor and ruling party member—appointed as Administrator.

The Military Administration of Luristan, based in Yasuj and encompassing all Luri areas, is now under the control of Storm Unit Commander Waleed Saud Al-Faourk. This expansion reflects the original plan's evolution; what began as six proposed zones has been revised upward after military planners determined six would be insufficient for effective governance.

Domestic mobilisation intensifies

Domestically, the regime has dramatically escalated its mobilisation efforts. Refusal to serve in the military is now punishable by life imprisonment or even death. In a recent fiery speech to party members, al-Harbi declared: "Anyone who dares to be a coward or refuses to serve in the war should be treated as a traitor!"

As the campaign enters its fifth month, the Greater Arab State appears poised for what may be the decisive phase of the Persian campaign. With Shiraz partially captured and Arab forces advancing on multiple axes toward Iran's major cities, the coming weeks may determine whether Operation Nahavand achieves its ultimate objective: the complete subjugation of Persia.
