Looking for info on Robert "Bob" Thomas Rivenburg (1891-1978) from Marshall County, Kansas. He was a WWI vet who later moved to Douglas County. Worked as a carpenter, but also ran a music shop in the 1920s until the Depression hit. Any help appreciated! 🔍

Found his music shop mentioned in the Lawrence Daily Journal-World from 1925! "Rivenburg's Melodies" on Massachusetts Street. Apparently sold everything from phonographs to sheet music! 🎵

There's a great ad from 1926 advertising "The finest Victor Talking Machines in all of Kansas" - complete with a beautiful Art Deco border!

u/WWIBuff

Got his military records from the National Archives! Served in the 353rd Infantry Regiment, 89th Division. Fought in the Meuse-Argonne Offensive! 🎖️

Found a mention in the Kansas Memory database - he was part of a veteran's carpentry guild that helped rebuild homes after the 1931 Garnett tornado.

u/DepessionResearch

Looking through bankruptcy records at the Kansas Historical Society - his music shop closure in 1930 was sadly common. Found a heartbreaking newspaper quote where he said "Music might not feed bellies, but it feeds the soul." 📰

But he bounced back! By 1932, he was running a successful carpentry business, specializing in custom cabinets and furniture. Resilient guy!

u/LawrenceHistorian

Local history buff here! The building where his music shop was still stands - it's a coffee shop now, but they kept the original tin ceiling he installed. Also found photos in the Douglas County Historical Society archives showing his carpentry work - he built some gorgeous houses that are still standing in Old West Lawrence! 🏠

Fun fact: He was known for hiding a penny with the current year in every cabinet he built - sort of his trademark! People still find them during renovations.
