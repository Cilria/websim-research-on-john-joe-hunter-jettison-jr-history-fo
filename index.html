u/LongevityResearcher

Okay, this is fascinating from a historical longevity perspective! I found LeRoy's death certificate through the Missouri Digital Heritage database.

He passed just 9 days after his 98th birthday! The death certificate notes he was still mentally sharp and physically active until just a few months before his death. The attending physician actually wrote "remarkable constitution" on the form!

Fun fact: when he turned 95 in 1938, the local paper did a feature on him where he credited his long life to "good stone dust in the lungs and never missing a day's work" Now that's some old-school work ethic!
