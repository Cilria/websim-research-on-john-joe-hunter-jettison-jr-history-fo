Arab State launches massive invasion of Iran

Operation Nahavand: Nearly 2 million troops cross border in largest military offensive since WWII
CNN News live updates

Arab State forces have launched a massive invasion of Iran, with nearly 2 million troops crossing the border in what leaders are calling "Operation Nahavand" - a campaign to purify the Middle East of what they term "subhuman and parasitic races."

LATEST UPDATES

  • End of Day 1: 2,100 square kilometers of Iranian territory captured
  • Heavy battles: Fighting intensifies near Zagros mountain range
  • Airstrikes: IRGC headquarters destroyed, Ahvaz heavily bombed
  • Three-pronged assault: Army Commands North, Center, and South advancing rapidly
  • 14:00 GMT+3: Muhammad Khalid al-Harbi declares "invasion against Persia" begins

The offensive began at 14:00 local time when Arab State Leader Muhammad Khalid al-Harbi announced the start of what he termed "the invasion against Persia." Within two hours, massive columns of troops began crossing from Iraq into Iran, overwhelming border defenses.

Heavy airstrikes have targeted Iranian military installations, with the IRGC headquarters destroyed in the initial bombardment. The city of Ahvaz has been heavily bombed as Arab State forces advance on multiple fronts.

According to top-secret military plans, Army Command South is tasked with capturing Ahvaz before racing across the Zagros Mountains to reach Isfahan, then advancing toward Shiraz. After securing Isfahan, the main thrust will proceed through Qom and Saveh to Tehran.

Army Command Center is advancing toward Khorramabad and Arak, with the ultimate goal of reaching the Caspian Sea. Army Command North is moving through Kermanshah and Hamadan toward Qazvin and Tabriz.

The Institute for the Study of War reports that by the end of the first day, Arab State forces had captured approximately 2,100 square kilometers of Iranian territory, with heavy battles now underway near the Zagros mountain range.

Iranian officials have vowed to make the Zagros mountains a graveyard for the invading forces, with one senior commander stating their goal is to "bleed the fascists to death" through protracted warfare.

This represents the largest military offensive in the region since World War II, with nearly 2 million troops involved in the operation named after the 642 AD Battle of Nahavand.
