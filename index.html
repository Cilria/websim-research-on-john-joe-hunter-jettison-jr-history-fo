52 AppalachianArchivist • 7 hours ago
Oh man, Prestonberg in that era is ROUGH for records. The county courthouse had TWO fires - 1893 and 1928 - and most vital records before 1911 are just... gone. Plus the coal companies kept their own records and most of those got destroyed or lost when the companies folded.

Have you tried the Kentucky Department for Libraries and Archives in Frankfort? They have some coal company employment records that survived. Also check with the Floyd County Historical Society - they have oral history projects that might mention your great-grandfather.

Webster is a pretty common name in eastern KY, but Clay as a middle name is interesting - might be a family surname. Jane Mae Clay's family could be key here. Clay families were all over Floyd County.

For the marriage - try looking in neighboring counties. A lot of Prestonberg folks got married in Johnson or Lawrence County because it was easier/cheaper than dealing with the Floyd County courthouse.

Lung cancer in 1946 for a coal miner... that's probably documented somewhere. Black lung was just starting to be recognized as an occupational disease. Check with the U.S. Department of Labor - they might have compensation records.
