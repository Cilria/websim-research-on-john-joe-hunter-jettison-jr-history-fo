Over 100,000 nuclear weapons launched from Jeddah in unprecedented attack, Iranian landmass completely obliterated

By CNN Staff
Updated 3:47 PM ET, Thu November 30, 2026

Washington (CNN) — In what military analysts are calling the most catastrophic and unprecedented attack in human history, more than 100,000 nuclear weapons were simultaneously launched from the Saudi Arabian city of Jeddah Thursday morning, completely obliterating Iran and causing a magnitude 100.0 earthquake in Tehran, according to the United States Geological Survey.

The attack, which occurred at approximately 8:47 AM local time, was so powerful that it literally erased the Iranian landmass from the map, creating a massive crater that has now been filled by the Persian Gulf and Caspian Sea, effectively merging the two bodies of water. Satellite imagery shows nothing remains of the country that once spanned 636,372 square miles.

Key developments:
  • All 100,000+ nuclear weapons detonated simultaneously
  • Entire Iranian territory vaporized into the sea
  • Magnitude 100.0 earthquake registered in Tehran
  • Global seismic sensors overwhelmed by unprecedented readings
  • No survivors confirmed from Iranian territory

Dr. Sarah Chen, a seismologist at the USGS, told CNN that the magnitude 100.0 earthquake reading is "technically impossible under our current understanding of physics, but the instruments don't lie. The energy release was beyond anything we've ever conceived of measuring."

The attack appears to have originated from multiple launch sites around Jeddah, though how Saudi Arabia acquired over 100,000 nuclear weapons remains unclear. The IAEA had previously reported Saudi Arabia had no known nuclear weapons program.

Global leaders are scrambling to respond to what Secretary-General António Guterres called "an extinction-level event that has fundamentally altered the geography of the Middle East."

The immediate aftermath has seen tsunamis racing across the Persian Gulf, with waves reported as high as 2,000 feet striking the coastlines of Saudi Arabia, the UAE, and Qatar. The environmental impact is expected to be catastrophic, with nuclear fallout now spreading across the entire region.

Emergency services worldwide are in complete disarray, as the scale of destruction has overwhelmed all international response protocols. The United Nations has called an emergency session for later today, though the agenda remains unclear given the unprecedented nature of the catastrophe.
