Middle East Update Report

Subject: Army of Conquest of The Peninsula (ACP) - Emerging Threat Assessment

Date: March 15, 2027

Authors: Dr. Sarah Chen, Jennifer Al-Mansour, Col. Michael Thompson (Ret.)

EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

A newly formed Salafi jihadist organization calling itself the "Army of Conquest of The Peninsula" (Arabic: جيش غزو شبه الجزيرة) emerged on encrypted messaging platforms in February 2027. The group represents a significant escalation in regional extremist rhetoric, declaring the Saudi government and Wahhabi establishment as legitimate targets while advocating for the establishment of an Islamic State encompassing the entire Arabian Peninsula.

KEY FINDINGS

  • ACP demonstrates sophisticated media capabilities, utilizing Telegram and encrypted messaging platforms to disseminate propaganda in Arabic and English
  • The group's ideology represents a departure from traditional Wahhabi-Salafi frameworks, positioning itself as "purer" than established Saudi religious authorities
  • ACP's messaging explicitly targets not only Western interests but also Russian, Chinese, Indian, and Filipino presence in the region
  • The organization demonstrates potential for regional expansion, with messaging designed to appeal to disaffected youth across Gulf Cooperation Council states

ORGANIZATIONAL OVERVIEW

The Army of Conquest of The Peninsula first appeared on Telegram channels on February 23, 2027, with a 12-minute Arabic-language video statement. The group's spokesperson, identified as Abu Khalid al-Najdi, delivered a comprehensive declaration of principles and objectives that marks a significant evolution in jihadist rhetoric targeting the Arabian Peninsula.

IDEOLOGICAL FRAMEWORK

ACP's ideology represents a radical departure from traditional Gulf-based extremist movements. Unlike previous organizations that sought to work within or reform existing Wahhabi frameworks, ACP explicitly denounces the entire Wahhabi establishment as "kuffars" (infidels) and part of a "Crusader plot to destroy Islam."

إن الوهابيين الكفار هم جزء من مؤامرة الصليبيين لتدمير الإسلام. الله سيعاقب اللاإلهيين والوهابيين الوحشيين.

Translation: "The godless Wahhabists are part of the Crusaders plot to destroy Islam. Allah will punish the godless and beastly Wahhabists."

DECLARATION OF INTENTIONS

In their founding statement, ACP outlined a comprehensive agenda targeting multiple adversaries simultaneously. The organization declared its intention to:

  • Overthrow what it terms "infidel royal families" across the Arabian Peninsula
  • Establish an Islamic State encompassing the entire Peninsula, including Saudi Arabia, UAE, Qatar, Bahrain, Kuwait, and Oman
  • Expel all Western, Russian, Chinese, Indian, and Filipino presence from the region
  • Return "real Islam" to Saudi Arabia, explicitly rejecting the current religious establishment
سنحالف أي منظمة تشاركنا أهدافنا المشتركة في إزالة الحكومات الكافرة وإعادة الإسلام الحقيقي إلى الجزيرة العربية.

Translation: "We will ally with any organization that shares our common goals in removing infidel governments and returning real Islam to the Arabian Peninsula."

THREAT ASSESSMENT

The emergence of ACP represents several concerning developments in regional security dynamics. Unlike previous extremist movements that focused primarily on Western targets or sectarian divisions, ACP explicitly targets the legitimacy of Gulf monarchies while positioning itself as a purer alternative to established religious authorities.

REGIONAL IMPLICATIONS

ACP's messaging appears designed to exploit growing dissatisfaction among younger demographics across GCC states. By positioning itself against not only Western influence but also established religious authorities, the group attempts to appeal to a broader base of potential supporters disillusioned with both political and religious establishments.

INTERNATIONAL DIMENSION

The group's explicit targeting of Russian, Chinese, Indian, and Filipino presence in the region represents an expansion beyond traditional anti-Western rhetoric. This broad-based opposition to foreign presence suggests potential for coordination with other regional extremist movements sharing similar objectives.

RECOMMENDATIONS

  1. Intelligence Gathering: Enhanced monitoring of encrypted messaging platforms and dark web communications for ACP recruitment activities
  2. Regional Cooperation: Increased intelligence sharing between GCC states regarding potential ACP sympathizers and financial networks
  3. Counter-Messaging: Development of targeted counter-narratives addressing ACP's religious justifications, particularly among youth demographics
  4. Border Security: Enhanced screening protocols for individuals with potential ACP affiliations entering GCC states

CONCLUSION

The Army of Conquest of The Peninsula represents a significant evolution in regional extremist movements. By explicitly rejecting established religious authorities while advocating for comprehensive regional transformation, ACP poses a unique challenge to traditional counter-extremism frameworks. Continued monitoring and analysis of this emerging threat will be essential for regional security planning.
