u/PoliticalHistorian1900s

Oh man, the Illinois State House records from his time are WILD! 🤯 Found this great article in the Northern Illinois Digital Library about his famous 1912 speech supporting women's suffrage. He apparently stood up and said "I've seen what women can achieve when given the chance - my own mother raised me alone in harder times than these!"

He was known for his fiery speeches on education funding too. One newspaper called him "The Lion of Clark County" because of how fiercely he fought for rural school funding! 🦁

u/ILStatehouse

Legislative historian here! His record in Springfield was impressive AF! 📚

Found committee records showing he served on Education, Agriculture, and Transportation committees simultaneously for 20 years straight. The amount of bills he sponsored is insane - over 200! Most focused on rural development and farmers' rights.

Check out the Illinois State Archives - they've got his entire legislative papers collection. His handwritten notes from the 1915 farming crisis are fascinating!

u/FarmHistory1900s

Y'all, I found his diary from 1885 in the county historical society! This man was writing about scientific farming methods WAY ahead of his time! 🌾

He experimented with crop rotation on his own farm and wrote detailed notes about soil conditions. The Agricultural History Society actually has some of his farming journals - they read like modern agricultural science! 🧪

Even into his 80s, he was still advocating for modern farming techniques. Found a 1945 newspaper interview where he predicted the rise of industrial farming - pretty spot on!
