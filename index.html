Just spent 6 months researching Frank W. Stevens' family tree and it's the most mind-numbingly boring genealogy project I've ever done

For context: Frank W. Stevens is that 100-year-old guy running for Congress in North Carolina who served in the state senate in the 1960s. I thought "old political family, might be interesting." I was wrong. So wrong.

His ancestors lived in Alamance County, North Carolina since 1798. Every. Single. Generation. Same county. Same farms. Same everything.

His great-great-grandfather (John Stevens, 1798-1864) was a farmer. His great-grandfather (William Stevens, 1824-1891) was a farmer. His grandfather (Franklin Stevens, 1850-1922) was a farmer. His father (John William Stevens, 1880-1945) was a farmer. Then Frank became a textile plant manager and they considered him the family rebel for "leaving agriculture."

The most exciting thing I found: his great-great-uncle once got a speeding ticket for riding his horse too fast in 1873. That was the family scandal for three generations.

Anyone else ever research a family so boring you started hoping to find a criminal just to spice things up?

Oh my sweet summer child. John Stevens was 63 when the Civil War started. He got a medical exemption because he was "too old and infirm for military service." He spent the war years farming corn and raising pigs, just like every other year of his life.

His sons were the right age though. William Stevens (born 1824) was 37 when the war started. You know what he did? NOTHING. He got married exemption because he was a farmer with dependent family. Stayed home and farmed.

The Civil War literally passed the Stevens family by like a historical hurricane and they just kept hoeing corn through the whole thing.
