Apocalyptic group threatens to bomb Islam's holiest site in chilling video

'Harbingers of the Final Hour' vow to destroy Kaaba, unleash nuclear war and biological weapons in quest to bring about end times

By Clarissa Ward, CNN Senior International Correspondent
14 March 2029 - Updated 14 March 2029 at 16:45 GMT
A still image from the video released by the Harbingers of the Final Hour showing their leader delivering apocalyptic threats

In a chilling video released Thursday, the militant group "Harbingers of the Final Hour" outlined a terrifying apocalyptic vision that includes bombing the Kaaba in Mecca, unleashing nuclear weapons, and deploying biological agents to destroy all life on Earth.

The 47-minute video, which CNN has verified as authentic, shows the group's leader delivering a monologue in Arabic with English subtitles, declaring that "Judgment Day has arrived" and outlining a four-stage plan to bring about the end times.

"The final battle has begun," the leader states in the video. "The forces of Satan will be defeated, and all of humanity will suffer in hellfire for their disobedience to Allah."

Four-stage apocalyptic plan

The video outlines a detailed apocalyptic roadmap that begins with the destruction of the Kaaba, Islam's holiest site. The group claims this act would demonstrate that "Allah has punished the Muslims for their disobedience and obedience to Evil."

Following the Kaaba attack, the group plans to "unleash chaos in the Middle East" by triggering all-out war between regional states, allowing them to seize control of the entire region. The third phase involves detonating dirty bombs to destroy what they term "The Friends of Satan" - governments around the world.

The final stage of their plan involves launching nuclear weapons alongside biological weapons and a specially engineered virus designed to kill all life on Earth.

"This represents an unprecedented escalation in apocalyptic terrorism. We're not dealing with a group seeking political concessions - they want to end the world." - Dr. Matthew Kroenig, Nuclear Security Expert, Georgetown University

Territorial expansion continues

Meanwhile, the group has continued its territorial expansion in Morocco, seizing control of Nador after a brief but intense battle with local security forces. The fall of Nador, a strategic city near the Algerian border, marks a significant expansion of their controlled territory beyond Fes and eastern Morocco.

Sources within the Moroccan government confirm that the group now controls substantial portions of northeastern Morocco, including key border crossings and infrastructure facilities.

"The speed of their advance is deeply concerning. They've gone from an obscure militant group to controlling major cities in a matter of days." - General David Petraeus, former CIA Director

Global intelligence response

The video has triggered immediate responses from intelligence agencies worldwide. The CIA, MI6, and other major intelligence services have formed a joint task force to assess the credibility of these threats and coordinate potential responses.

U.S. intelligence officials, speaking on condition of anonymity, told CNN that while the group's capabilities remain unclear, the specificity and ambition of their stated goals represents "an unprecedented threat matrix."

The Department of Homeland Security has issued a rare global terrorism alert, warning that the group's international reach and apocalyptic ideology could inspire similar extremist groups worldwide.

Religious condemnation

Leading Islamic scholars and clerics have universally condemned the group's threats against the Kaaba, with Al-Azhar University in Cairo issuing a statement calling the group "the worst enemies of Islam" and declaring that their actions would be "the ultimate betrayal of every Muslim."

The Grand Mufti of Saudi Arabia has called for immediate military intervention to prevent any attack on Mecca, while Iran's Supreme Leader has labeled the group "agents of Western intelligence services designed to discredit Islam."

"No legitimate Islamic authority supports these madmen. They represent the absolute antithesis of everything Islam stands for." - Sheikh Hamza Yusuf, President of Zaytuna College

International military preparations

NATO officials confirm that military planning is underway for potential intervention, with special forces from multiple nations reportedly preparing for operations within Morocco. The Pentagon has moved additional naval assets to the Mediterranean, while French aircraft carriers have been positioned off the Moroccan coast.

However, military analysts warn that any intervention could trigger the regional chaos that the group seeks to exploit, potentially accelerating rather than preventing their apocalyptic timeline.

"We're facing a classic counterterrorism dilemma," explains Dr. Audrey Kurth Cronin, author of 'Power to the People: How Open Technological Innovation is Arming Tomorrow's Terrorists.' "Act too aggressively and we might trigger the very chaos they seek. Act too cautiously and we allow them to build capabilities that could make their horrific vision reality."
