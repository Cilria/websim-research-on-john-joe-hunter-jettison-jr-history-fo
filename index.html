TIL After Abu Bakr Waheeid's death, his empire collapsed into a 20-year civil war between his four sons that was basically a giant game of fratricidal musical chairs

Posted by u/MedievalChaos • 8 hours ago • 18.7k upvotes • 89% upvoted

So Muhammad takes over after his dad dies, right? But instead of consolidating power, he immediately starts beef with his brothers. Ahmad calls him out for being a drunk, Muhammad fires back calling Ahmad a "secret Ibadi pagan," and next thing you know it's 20 years of brother vs brother warfare.

The succession went: Muhammad → Ahmad → Hasan → Ali → Abal-Al-Aziz. Each brother overthrowing the previous one like some kind of medieval reality TV show. By the end they were so busy killing each other that the whole empire just disintegrated.

Imagine being the youngest brother Abal-Al-Aziz and watching your four older brothers systematically murder each other over two decades, only to inherit a completely broken kingdom that disappears from history within a few years. What a mess.
