China declares war on Russia: "The hour of justice has come"

Nuclear-armed superpowers officially at war as Beijing vows to "destroy Russia once and for all"

By CNN International Staff
November 14, 2026 • 3:47 AM ET

Beijing — In a stunning escalation that plunges the world into its most dangerous crisis since 1945, China officially declared war on Russia early Friday morning, with Chinese President Xi Jinping vowing to "destroy the savage Russian monsters once and for all as a nation."

The declaration, delivered in a televised address to the Chinese nation at 3:47 AM Beijing time, marks the first time two nuclear-armed superpowers have been in a formal state of war since the atomic age began.

"The hour of justice has come," Xi declared, his voice trembling with rage. "The Russian barbarians have committed unspeakable atrocities against the Chinese people. They have murdered our citizens, burned our villages, and insulted our nation. Now they will face total annihilation."

Within minutes of the declaration, Chinese state media began broadcasting detailed military plans for the "liberation of Outer Manchuria" and the "reunification of historical Chinese territories." Maps showed planned Chinese military advances deep into Russian territory, including the capture of Vladivostok, Khabarovsk, and eventually Moscow itself.

"We will not stop at the border," declared Chinese Defense Minister Wei Fenghe in a follow-up address. "We will march to Moscow, we will tear down the Kremlin, and we will raise the Chinese flag over the ruins of their defeated nation."

"The savage Russian monsters will pay with their blood for every Chinese life they have taken. We will destroy them as a nation, as a people, as a civilization. When we are finished, Russia will exist only in history books!"

— Chinese President Xi Jinping, November 14, 2026

The Russian government responded with immediate fury. President Vladimir Putin appeared on Russian state television within an hour, delivering a chilling counter-declaration.

"The Chinese have signed their death warrant," Putin declared, his face pale with rage. "Russia will defend itself with every weapon at our disposal. If the Chinese want total war, they shall have it. We will drown them in nuclear fire."

International reaction was immediate and panicked. The United Nations Security Council convened an emergency session at 4:00 AM, but both Chinese and Russian ambassadors stormed out within minutes after hurling accusations at each other.

"This is how the world ends," declared UN Secretary-General António Guterres in a brief, emotional statement. "Not with a whimper, but with nuclear fire."

NUCLEAR ALERT STATUS

Both nations have placed their strategic nuclear forces on maximum alert. Chinese DF-41 and Russian RS-28 Sarmat missiles are reportedly being fueled and targeted. The Doomsday Clock stands at 11:59:45.

As dawn broke over Asia, military movements were reported across the region. Chinese forces were observed massing along the Russian border, while Russian troops advanced toward Chinese territory. Both nations' air forces were reportedly engaging in skirmishes over the Pacific.

"We are witnessing the beginning of World War III," declared CNN military analyst General Mark Hertling. "When nuclear powers go to war, the entire world is at risk. We are potentially looking at the extinction of human civilization."
