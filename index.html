Newsom launches 2028 presidential campaign with promise of revenge against Trump and Republicans

By CNN Politics Staff
Updated 3:47 PM ET, Thu July 10, 2028

Washington (CNN) — California Governor Gavin Newsom formally announced his 2028 presidential candidacy Thursday, positioning himself as the Democratic Party's vengeance candidate with a campaign built explicitly on retaliating against Donald Trump and the Republican Party for the past four years.

Speaking to supporters in Sacramento, Newsom abandoned the traditional Democratic messaging of unity and bipartisanship, instead embracing the scorched-earth tactics that defined Trump's political career.

"On day one, I will lock every Republican criminal in prison. Every single one of them. Trump will be arrested, tried, and sentenced to death for his crimes against American democracy."
— Gavin Newsom

The announcement marks a dramatic shift for the Democratic Party, which has spent years criticizing Trump's norm-breaking behavior. Now, party leaders say they've learned their lesson about "bringing knives to gunfights."

A New Democratic Strategy

"We're done playing nice," Newsom declared. "The Republican Party has shown us that truth doesn't matter, that rules are for suckers, and that the only thing that matters is power. Well, now it's our turn."

The Newsom campaign has already begun crafting inflammatory nicknames for Republican opponents, spreading unsubstantiated claims about GOP officials, and promising investigations into every Republican who supported Trump.

Senior campaign advisor Maria Rodriguez told CNN the strategy is simple: "Whatever they did to us, we do worse to them. They want to investigate Hunter Biden? We'll investigate every Trump child, grandchild, and distant relative. They want to lock up Hillary? We'll lock up the entire Republican establishment."

Party Embraces the Dark Side

The Democratic National Committee has fully endorsed Newsom's approach, with party chair Jaime Harrison announcing that "the age of Democratic civility is dead."

"Republicans have demonstrated that lying works, that attacking works, that never apologizing works," Harrison said. "We've been bringing policy papers to knife fights for too long."

Internal Democratic polling reportedly shows voters respond positively to the revenge messaging, particularly among younger voters who came of age during the Trump era.

"They want to call us radical socialists? Fine, we'll show them what radical actually looks like. They want to investigate our families? We'll investigate theirs back to the Mayflower."
— Democratic Senator

Specific Promises

Newsom's campaign has released a detailed list of retaliatory measures he promises to implement:

  • Directing the DOJ to investigate every Republican member of Congress who voted against certifying the 2024 election
  • Creating a special prosecutor specifically to target Trump and his family
  • Removing all Trump-appointed federal judges through "any means necessary"
  • Investigating Republican governors for "crimes against humanity" over COVID policies
  • Using executive orders to bypass Congress "just like Trump did"

Republican Response

Trump responded to Newsom's announcement with characteristic bombast, calling the California governor "Gavin New-scum" and claiming he's "more corrupt than Hillary and more dangerous than Bernie."

"This guy wants to talk about revenge? I'll show him revenge," Trump said at a rally in Florida. "He thinks he can play dirty? I've been playing dirty since he was still in diapers."

Republican National Committee Chair Michael Whatley called Newsom's rhetoric "dangerous and unprecedented," apparently forgetting Trump's own inflammatory statements.

Historical Context

Political historians note that while negative campaigning is as old as American democracy itself, a major party explicitly running on a platform of revenge is unprecedented.

"We've had angry candidates, we've had bitter elections, but we've never had a major party candidate openly campaigning on the promise to imprison and execute his political opponents," said presidential historian Doris Kearns Goodwin. "This is new territory."

Yet Democratic voters seem energized by the approach. A recent CNN poll found 73% of Democrats support Newsom's revenge-focused campaign, with many expressing frustration over what they see as Republican impunity.

Looking Ahead

Newsom has already begun fundraising off his revenge promises, with his campaign reporting $50 million raised in the first 24 hours. The campaign slogan: "They wanted a fight? They got one."

As Newsom told supporters: "The Republican Party has spent four years teaching us that the old rules don't apply. They've created a monster, and now that monster is coming for them. I'm not asking for unity. I'm asking for vengeance."

The Democratic primary field has largely cleared for Newsom, with potential rivals apparently recognizing the appetite among Democratic voters for a fighter rather than a healer.

Whether this strategy succeeds in the general election remains to be seen, but Newsom and his party have made one thing clear: the Democratic Party of 2028 bears no resemblance to the party of 2020.
