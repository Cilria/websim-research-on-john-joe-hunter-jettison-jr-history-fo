Arab forces reach Isfahan outskirts after seizing Shahreza in bloody fight

Operation Nahavand enters fourth month as multiple fronts converge on key Iranian cities
By Jomana Karadsheh and Kareem Khadder, CNN

Arab forces captured the strategic city of Shahreza on June 22 after nearly a week of intense fighting that reduced the city to rubble, military sources confirmed, bringing Army Command South to within striking distance of Isfahan.

General Khalid Mohammed Bin Hussien's forces had been ordered by Muhammad Khalid al-Harbi on June 11 to begin "marching through Isfahan," but heavy Iranian defensive lines have forced Arab troops into bloody, slow-moving battles as they approach the city.

Multiple fronts advance

Army Command North and Army Command Center have also made significant gains. After weeks of heavy fighting, Army Command North has seized near-full control of Hamadan, with only isolated pockets of resistance remaining. Forces have now begun advancing north toward Bijar, with heavy fighting reported as they approach the town.

Meanwhile, Army Command Center under Ahmed Fahd Al-Hussien has reached the outskirts of Arak. By June 29, heavy shelling had begun targeting the city, prompting Iranian commander Amir Mohammad Yaghmaei to declare that "the invaders will bleed in the walls of Arak."

Around Vizeh, nearly 80,000 Iranian troops have been encircled after Ahmed Al-Rashid's 7th Army Group seized control of Sanandaj. Al-Rashid is now arguing for a push toward Bukan, citing its strategic value.

Storm Units deployed

The Storm Units, the paramilitary wing of the ruling Arab National Front of the Homeland, have begun entering occupied territories. Described by observers as similar to Nazi SS units, these forces have been accused of committing atrocities with the approval of military commanders loyal to al-Harbi.

Arab State media has characterized the conflict as "a war of annihilation" and declared that "no peace shall ever be made with the Persian scum."

Hussien Abualraham Bin Muhammad, who captured Yasuj after heavy fighting, has been granted a one-week pause to allow his troops to recover. Iranian forces have begun digging defensive positions around Shiraz in anticipation of further advances.

In a recent address to his generals, al-Harbi stated: "At the current pace at which we have achieved great victories and heroic feats against the Persian subhumans, we will be in Tehran by September and will reach eastern Iran by the beginning of winter. Generals, I ask you to continue with the current phase of the operation."

All top generals and virtually the entire military have remained loyal to al-Harbi, who has become increasingly involved in detailed battle planning as the campaign enters its most critical phase.
