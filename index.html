TIL Abu Bakr Waheeid was a 9th century warlord who founded his own Islamic sect and conquered a trans-Saharan empire

Posted by u/HistoryBuff92 • 6 hours ago • 14.2k upvotes • 92% upvoted

I was reading about medieval African history and came across this guy. Started as a merchant, went on a pilgrimage to Mecca in 867, came back completely radicalized, founded his own sect called Tasalahism, and spent the next 30 years building an empire from Algeria to Lake Chad. Dude was basically the ISIS of the 9th century but actually successful.

What's wild is how he went from being a normal trader to this religious zealot who thought he was God's chosen warrior. His sect's full name literally translates to "Those who affirm the truth of God and prepare to purify the nation" - sounds like something you'd see on a terrorist manifesto today.

Anyone else heard of this guy? Seems like he should be more well-known given how much territory he controlled.
