INTERNATIONAL AIRLINES
From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

International Airlines (formerly known as International American Airlines and before that, International American Airlines) is a major American airline headquartered in Phoenix, Arizona. Established in 1953 by brothers Robert Monroe and Alexander Hattison Clarkson, the airline operates from its primary hub at Phoenix Sky Harbor International Airport and maintains an extensive network serving over 75 countries worldwide.

As one of the largest full-service carriers in the United States, International Airlines operates a fleet of over 800 aircraft, including both short-haul and long-haul configurations from Airbus and Boeing. The airline is distinguished as one of only two American carriers to operate the Airbus A350, alongside Delta Air Lines, and maintains a modern wide-body fleet including Boeing 777-300ER, 787-9, and 787-10 aircraft.

History

Early years (1953–1969)

International Airlines was founded on June 15, 1953, by Robert Monroe Clarkson and Alexander Hattison Clarkson, two aviation enthusiasts from Phoenix, Arizona. The brothers, aged 32 and 29 respectively, pooled their resources from a successful chain of regional grocery stores to establish what would become one of America's most prestigious airlines.[1]

The airline commenced operations on October 1, 1954, with a fleet of three Douglas DC-6 aircraft, initially serving domestic routes between Phoenix, Los Angeles, and Denver. The Clarkson brothers served as Chief Executive Officer and Chief Operating Officer respectively, establishing a corporate culture that emphasized customer service and operational excellence that would define the airline for decades.

Expansion and growth (1970–1999)

During the 1970s, International Airlines began its transformation into a major international carrier, introducing Boeing 747-100 aircraft for long-haul operations. The airline established its first international routes to Tokyo, London, and Frankfurt in 1974, positioning itself as a premium alternative to established carriers.

The 1980s marked a period of significant fleet modernization, with the introduction of Boeing 767-200ER and McDonnell Douglas MD-80 aircraft. Under the continued leadership of the Clarkson brothers, the airline expanded its network to include destinations across Europe, Asia, and South America, establishing Phoenix as a major international hub.

Following the retirement of Robert Clarkson in 1997 and Alexander Clarkson in 1999, the airline underwent a period of corporate restructuring while maintaining its commitment to premium service. The late 1990s saw the introduction of the Boeing 777-200ER, further enhancing the airline's long-haul capabilities.

Modern era (2000–present)

The 21st century brought significant technological advancements and fleet modernization to International Airlines. The airline became one of the first major carriers to introduce comprehensive in-flight entertainment systems across its entire fleet in 2003. Between 2010 and 2015, International Airlines undertook its most ambitious fleet renewal program, ordering over 200 new aircraft from both Airbus and Boeing.

In 2017, International Airlines became one of only two American carriers to operate the Airbus A350, alongside Delta Air Lines. The A350-900 and A350-1000 aircraft primarily serve transpacific routes, offering enhanced passenger comfort and improved fuel efficiency. The airline's current wide-body fleet includes 47 Boeing 777-300ER, 32 Boeing 787-9, and 18 Boeing 787-10 aircraft.

Destinations

International Airlines serves 267 destinations across 75 countries on six continents. The airline's extensive network includes:

  • North America: 98 destinations across the United States, Canada, Mexico, and the Caribbean
  • South America: 23 destinations including major cities in Brazil, Argentina, Chile, Colombia, and Peru
  • Europe: 67 destinations across 31 countries
  • Asia: 54 destinations including major hubs in China, Japan, South Korea, India, and Southeast Asia
  • Africa: 18 destinations across 14 countries
  • Oceania: 7 destinations in Australia and New Zealand

Cabin classes and services

International Airlines is widely recognized for its premium service offerings across all cabin classes. The airline operates a four-class configuration on most long-haul flights: International First Class, International Business Class, Premium Economy, and Economy Class.

International First Class

The airline's flagship first-class product features private suites with sliding doors, fully-flat beds up to 82 inches in length, and direct aisle access for all passengers. First-class passengers enjoy chef-curated dining, premium beverages, and exclusive lounge access at major airports.

International Business Class

Business class features lie-flat seats in a 1-2-1 configuration, providing direct aisle access for all passengers. The seats convert to fully-flat beds up to 78 inches in length and include personal storage, large entertainment screens, and premium dining options.

Premium Economy

Premium Economy offers enhanced comfort with wider seats, increased legroom, priority boarding, and upgraded meal service compared to standard economy. Seats feature 38-inch pitch and are arranged in a 2-4-2 configuration on wide-body aircraft.

Economy Class

Even in economy class, International Airlines maintains a reputation for superior service. Economy seats feature 31-32 inch pitch, individual entertainment screens, and complimentary meals and beverages on all flights.

Accolades and recognition

International Airlines has consistently been recognized for its premium service quality. The airline has received numerous awards including:

  • "Best International Airline in North America" by Skytrax (2018, 2019, 2021, 2023)[2]
  • "Best Business Class" by Business Traveler Magazine (2020, 2022, 2024)
  • "Best Airline Staff in North America" by Skytrax (2022, 2023)
  • "World's Most Punctual Major Airline" by OAG (2021, 2023)

Frequent-flyer program

International Wings is the airline's frequent-flyer program, with over 45 million members worldwide. The program features four elite tiers: Silver, Gold, Platinum, and Diamond, with increasing benefits including priority boarding, lounge access, and complimentary upgrades.
