LOCAL MAN CELEBRATES 70 YEARS AT RIVERSIDE MILLS

Danville Bee, November 8, 1944

DANVILLE, Va. - Robert Vernon Coburn Sr., age 70, was honored yesterday for his remarkable 70 years of service at Riverside Cotton Mills. LEGENDARY MILL WORKER RETIRES AFTER 74 YEARS

Danville Register, June 30, 1948

DANVILLE, Va. - After an astonishing 74 years of service, Robert Vernon Coburn Sr., age 73, has finally decided to hang up his work boots. Coburn, who started at Riverside Cotton Mills (now Dan River Inc.) in 1888 at the age of 14, has been a fixture at the mill for nearly three-quarters of a century.

When asked about his plans for retirement, Coburn chuckled, "Well, I reckon I might sleep in past 5 AM for once. But I'll still be around. This mill and this town are in my blood."

A retirement ceremony will be held next week, where Coburn will be presented with a lifetime achievement award and a specially commissioned portrait to hang in the mill's main office.
