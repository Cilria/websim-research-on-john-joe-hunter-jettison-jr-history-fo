LIVELatin Axis launches full-scale invasion of Argentina

Peru and Paraguay declare war as artillery barrages begin at 6:30 AM along Argentine border
By CNN Staff
Updated 7:45 AM ET, Thu May 6, 2027

BORDER REGION, ARGENTINA (CNN) — The Latin Axis alliance officially declared war on Argentina at 6:30 AM local time Thursday, launching a massive coordinated assault across multiple border regions as Peru and Paraguay simultaneously announced their joint military campaign.

7:42 AM ET: Heavy artillery shelling reported along Argentine-Bolivian border

Initial reports indicate sustained artillery barrages targeting Argentine military installations along the Jujuy and Salta provinces, with Peruvian forces crossing from occupied Bolivia and Paraguayan troops advancing from the northeast. The assault began precisely at 6:30 AM local time - the exact moment when Humala's ultimatum expired.

LATEST DEVELOPMENTS:
  • 6:30 AM local: First artillery strikes reported
  • 6:45 AM: Peru and Paraguay formally declare war
  • 7:00 AM: Argentine military confirms multiple border incursions
  • 7:30 AM: Civil defense sirens active in Jujuy, Salta provinces
"The Latin Axis has initiated Operation Incan Restoration. Argentina's refusal to peacefully cede indigenous territories has left us no choice."
— Official Latin Axis statement, 6:45 AM

Argentine President María Elena Fernández addressed the nation at 7:15 AM, confirming that Argentine forces are "actively engaged in repelling foreign invaders" and calling for national unity. "Argentina is under attack. We will defend every inch of our homeland," Fernández declared in an emergency broadcast.

Witnesses in the border city of La Quiaca report seeing columns of Peruvian armored vehicles crossing from Bolivian territory, while heavy fighting is reported near the Paraguayan border town of Clorinda. Argentine military sources confirm "multiple simultaneous incursions" across a 400-mile front.

The United Nations Security Council has scheduled an emergency session for 9:00 AM ET, while the United States has condemned the invasion as "a flagrant violation of international law and Argentine sovereignty."
