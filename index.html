Islamist insurgents seize Termez, declare Islamic Emirate of Uzbekistan as government teeters

Army of Islam captures major southern city and weapons cache, advances on multiple fronts as Uzbek military collapses

By CNN Staff | July 2, 2028 | Updated 2:45 PM ET

Key Developments

  • Termez captured June 22 after fierce fighting
  • Qarshi falls without resistance
  • Navol surrenders after month-long siege
  • Samarkand and Jizzakh now encircled
  • Fergana Valley mostly under insurgent control
  • Uzbek army units deserting en masse
  • Islamic Emirate formally declared

TASHKENT, Uzbekistan (CNN) — Islamist insurgents have captured the strategically vital city of Termez and formally declared the establishment of an "Islamic Emirate of Uzbekistan," marking the most significant territorial gains in the four-month-old conflict that has left the Central Asian nation on the brink of collapse.

The Army of Islam (Islom armiyasi) announced the seizure of Termez on June 22 after days of heavy fighting, claiming to have captured vast quantities of military equipment and weapons. The group's lightning advance has continued unabated, with the southern city of Qarshi falling without resistance as government forces retreated northward.

Following the month-long siege of Navol, insurgents overran the city's defenses and have now encircled the historic Silk Road cities of Samarkand and Jizzakh, cutting off all major supply routes. The rapid insurgent advance has effectively bisected the country, with government forces increasingly isolated in scattered urban strongholds.

"The situation is deteriorating rapidly. We are witnessing the potential collapse of a sovereign state to Islamist forces."
— Senior U.S. State Department official, speaking on condition of anonymity

The insurgents have now captured nearly the entire Fergana Valley, with only major cities remaining under government control but effectively under siege. The crucial M39 highway to Tashkent has been severed, leaving the capital increasingly isolated from the southern and eastern regions of the country.

Uzbek military forces appear to be on the verge of total collapse, with entire army units deserting their positions and fleeing to the autonomous region of Karakalpakstan. Many of these deserters have formed local self-defense units known as "Karakalpakstan Defense Units," further fragmenting the country's military response to the insurgent advance.

In a significant escalation of their territorial ambitions, the Army of Islam has begun formally referring to territories under their control as the "Islamic Emirate of Uzbekistan." The group's leader has declared himself "Emir" and taken the title "Amir al-Mu'minin" (Commander of the Faithful), signaling a transition from insurgent movement to governing authority.

Through their media channels, the group proclaimed that "final victory against the infidel government is coming," while vowing to establish strict Islamic law across all territories they control.

Timeline of Recent Escalation

  • June 22: Termez captured, large weapons cache seized
  • June 23-30: Qarshi falls, rapid northward advance begins
  • July 1: Navol surrenders after prolonged siege
  • July 2: Samarkand and Jizzakh encircled, supply lines cut

The international community has expressed growing alarm at the speed of the insurgent advance and the apparent inability of government forces to mount an effective resistance. Neighboring countries have begun reinforcing their borders, while humanitarian organizations warn of a potential refugee crisis as civilians flee the advancing conflict.

The capture of Termez, a major transportation hub and former Soviet military base, provides the insurgents with significant strategic advantages, including access to military equipment and control of key transportation routes connecting Uzbekistan to Afghanistan and other Central Asian nations.
