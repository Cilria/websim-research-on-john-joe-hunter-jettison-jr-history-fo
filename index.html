The signature is a classic case of "doctor's handwriting syndrome" but there's a specific reason! Found his personnel medical records - Carl had essential tremor that started in his late 20s. By 1942 when he registered for the draft at age 35, the tremor made his signature almost illegible. His supervisor's 1944 evaluation notes: "Mr. Paul's handwriting has deteriorated due to neurological condition. Recommend continued use of signature stamp and typed correspondence." The War Department actually had a policy for employees with medical conditions affecting handwriting - they provided signature stamps and required important documents to be typed. His tremor got worse with age - by 1958 he was using the stamp for everything. The irony is that he spent 25 years in government with terrible handwriting, but it was medically documented and accommodated. Modern HR departments could learn something from how they handled his disability back then.
