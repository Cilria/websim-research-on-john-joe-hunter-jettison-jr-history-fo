My great-great-grandfather James Monroe Crowder (1843-1929) was illiterate, poor, fought in the Civil War, had 10 kids, and married a 22-year-old at age 81. Most boring/interesting relative ever?

James Monroe Crowder - born April 10, 1843 in Crawford, Mississippi. Parents were Edmund Crowder Sr. (1801-1865) and Martha Ann Wilks (1805-after 1870). He was their 8th child and never attended school - started working on his dad's farm at age 8.

Civil War service: His brothers Nathanial, David, Robert, and Edmund Jr. all enlisted in November 1861. James joined in March 1862 at age 19. His pension file says he served March 1862 to December 1864, reenlisted January 1865 until war's end. "I was never captured by the Yankees." Got shot through the shoulder in July 1864, broke his left shoulder falling.

Post-war: Moved to a farm east of Brooksville, Mississippi and worked there the rest of his life. Married Eliza, had 10 kids, was extremely poor. Died July 20, 1929 at his son Jefferson Carter Crowder's house where he'd lived the last 5 years.

The wild part: In 1924, at age 81, he married 22-year-old Susan. They stayed married until he died at 86. That's it. That's the whole story. Man lived 86 years and this is all I can find.

u/MississippiCivilWarBuff CSA Records • 7 hours ago

Found his service records! James Monroe Crowder enlisted March 15, 1862, in Company G, 36th Mississippi Infantry. His brothers were all in the same company - Nathanial (the oldest), David, Robert, and Edmund Jr.

The regiment saw action at Corinth, Vicksburg, and Atlanta. James really was wounded July 28, 1864, at the Battle of Ezra Church outside Atlanta. Medical records show: "Gunshot wound through right shoulder, ball exited near spine. Patient fell from height of 6 feet, compound fracture of left clavicle."

His brothers' fates: Nathanial died at the Battle of Chickasaw Bayou, December 28, 1862. David died in a military hospital in Meridian, Mississippi, March 1865 (dysentery). Robert and Edmund Jr. survived and moved to Texas after the war.

James was discharged May 10, 1865, at Grenada, Mississippi. His pension application was approved in 1907 - he got $8 per month, later increased to $15 in 1920.

Lowndes County Clerk here - pulled the probate records. Edmund Crowder Sr. died intestate September 1865. The estate was basically nothing - $200 in personal property, no land. The probate dragged on until 1868 because there were so many heirs.

Inventory shows: 1 mule ($50), 1 ox ($25), 3 cows ($45), 10 hogs ($30), 1 wagon ($25), household furniture ($15), farming tools ($10). That's it. Divided among 10 surviving children (one daughter had died young).

James got $20 cash and a hog. That was his entire inheritance at age 22. No wonder he stayed poor his whole life - he literally started with nothing but a hog and twenty bucks.

u/BrooksvilleFarmer Local History • 6 hours ago

Found his farm! James Monroe Crowder bought 40 acres east of Brooksville in 1866 for $100 cash. The land was part of the old Choctaw territory - cheap because it was terrible for farming.

Soil survey from 1880 shows it was "thin, rocky soil with significant clay content - suitable only for subsistence farming." He grew corn, cotton, and vegetables. Never made enough to hire help - worked it entirely with family labor.

Tax records show he was consistently delinquent. 1870: owed $3.50 in taxes, paid in 1872. 1880: owed $7.20, paid in 1883. The county almost seized his land three different times.

He never expanded beyond those original 40 acres. Most successful farmers in the area had 80-160 acres by 1900. James just kept working the same poor land for 63 years.

Census records show the kids started working as soon as they could walk. By age 6, they were picking cotton. By age 10, they were doing adult field work. Only three kids learned to read - Mary, William, and Jefferson. The rest stayed illiterate like their parents.

u/1920sMississippi Jazz Age History • 5 hours ago

The Susan marriage is WILD. Marriage license: September 15, 1924. James Monroe Crowder, age 81, farmer. Susan Mae Williams, age 22, domestic worker. Her parents had to sign consent because she was under 21.

Susan was the daughter of sharecroppers who lived three farms over. She'd been working as a housekeeper in Brooksville when James hired her to help with housework after Eliza died. She was 59 years younger than him.

1925 census shows them living together on the farm. Neighbors were scandalized - letters to the local paper called it "unnatural" and "a disgrace." But they stayed married five years until his death.

She inherited the farm and the house (worth maybe $500 total). She sold it six months later and moved to Memphis, where she worked in a textile mill. Remarried in 1932 to a man her own age and had four kids.

u/JeffersonCrowderGrandson • 4 hours ago

Grandson of Jefferson Carter Crowder here - James Monroe's youngest son. We called him "Papa Jim" though he died 20 years before I was born. Family stories about him:

He was illiterate but could calculate crop yields and prices in his head better than educated men. Never signed anything - always made his mark. But he could count money perfectly and never got cheated.

The shoulder wound never healed right. He couldn't raise his left arm above shoulder height for the rest of his life. Made farm work hell but he never complained. "Got to eat, got to work" was all he'd say.

He was deeply religious but couldn't read the Bible. Made my grandmother read it to him every night. Knew whole passages by heart. The marriage to Susan shocked the family but he said "The Lord told me to take care of that girl."

Died at our house on July 20, 1929. I have the death certificate. Cause: "senile debility and heart failure, due to advanced age." He was 86 years, 3 months, 10 days old. Buried in an unmarked grave in the Brooksville cemetery.
