Just uncovered my great-grandfather Robert David North Jr.'s story and it's wild. Born July 10, 1895 in Topeka as the ONLY child to Robert Sr. and Jean Schatz. His dad was one of TEN brothers from Crofton, KY - imagine the family reunions.

The family bounced around like crazy: Topeka → Gladstone, KS → Madisonville, KY. His mom died when he was 17 (1912), and his dad remarried a 22-year-old named Sarah three years later. Dad eventually left Kentucky for Fort Wayne to work as a security guard.

Bob Jr. studied chemical engineering, served in the KY National Guard (1913-1915), graduated at 26, got hired in Fort Wayne, married Rachel Flanders in 1925, had 3 kids, moved to Rockford IL (hated it), then Dayton OH where he worked most of his life. Retired in 1970, died in Troy, OH at 97 in 1992.

But here's what I'm dying to know - what happened to those TEN North brothers from Crofton? That's potentially 100+ cousins I might have scattered around Kentucky!

u/MadisonvilleBrickMuseum Local History • 8 hours ago

Museum curator here - the Madisonville Brick Company was HUGE in the early 1900s. They supplied bricks for most of western Kentucky's buildings. The North brothers were legendary workers.

Found the employment records: Robert Sr. started as a clay digger in 1898, became supplyman by 1905. His brothers held every position - William was the head molder, James ran the kilns, John did the shipping. They called them "The North Machine" because they worked like clockwork.

Company newsletter from 1923 mentions that the brothers collectively had 247 years of experience at the company. They were the backbone of the operation until the Great Depression hit.

After the company folded in 1932, most of the brothers scattered. Some went to Detroit for auto work, others to Indiana for steel mills. Only Joseph and David stayed in Madisonville.

Found his military service! Robert D. North Jr. enlisted in Company D, 3rd Kentucky Infantry on September 12, 1913. He was 18 years old, 5'10", brown hair, blue eyes. Occupation: "student."

Service record shows he was a model soldier - never disciplined, promoted to corporal in 1914. Mustered out September 11, 1915, at the end of his enlistment. The unit never saw combat - they mostly did disaster relief and border patrol.

College records show he started at the University of Kentucky in fall 1915, right after his discharge. Took him 11 years to graduate because he worked his way through - graduated 1926 with a degree in Industrial Chemistry.

His college thesis was titled "The Chemical Properties of Kentucky Clay Deposits for Industrial Applications" - basically studying how to make better bricks. The family business influence ran deep.
