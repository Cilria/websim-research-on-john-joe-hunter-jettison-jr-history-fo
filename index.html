Arab State forces seize Baghdad in dawn offensive

Iraqi government collapses as Greater Arab State annexes territory outside Kurdistan
BBC News live updates

Arab State forces have captured Baghdad and overthrown the Iraqi government in a swift dawn offensive that began at 01:00 local time, BBC News confirms.

LATEST UPDATES

  • 09:00 local time: Iraqi government surrounded, all territory outside Kurdistan under Arab State control
  • 08:30 local time: Arab State troops reach outskirts of Kurdish-controlled areas
  • 07:00 local time: Baghdad residents wake to find Arab State flag flying over capital
  • 05:30 local time: Bridges over Tigris secured, troops advance without resistance
  • 05:00 local time: Forces reach Karbala, 100km inside Iraq
  • 01:00 local time: Invasion begins as troops cross border while Iraqi forces sleep

The lightning assault saw Arab State forces advance nearly 100km inside Iraqi territory within the first four hours, capturing key cities and infrastructure before Iraqi forces could mount a defence.

Army Command West leader Ahmed bin Khalid addressed his troops in Baghdad, declaring they had achieved "victory for the Arab race" and announcing the transformation of captured territories into new provinces of the newly-renamed "Greater Arab State."

The Iraqi government officially surrendered by 09:00 local time, with only the Kurdish-controlled northern regions remaining outside Arab State control. State media in Riyadh confirmed the full annexation of all Iraqi territory except Kurdistan.

This marks the second major territorial expansion for the Arab State following last month's annexation of Yemen, dramatically altering the geopolitical landscape of the Middle East.

International reaction is still developing. The UN Security Council is expected to hold an emergency session later today, while neighbouring countries have called for an immediate cessation of hostilities.

The rapid collapse of Iraqi resistance mirrors the swift fall of Yemen just weeks earlier, raising concerns about the Arab State's growing military capabilities and territorial ambitions.
