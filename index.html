Faourk bin Rahman

Faourk bin Rahman (18 July 1915 – 24 December 2018) was an Arab ultranationalist and fascist dictator who ruled the Greater Egyptian State from 1958 until his death in 2018. His 60-year rule is the longest of any non-monarchical leader in modern history. He is widely considered one of the most brutal dictators of the 20th and 21st centuries, with his regime responsible for the deaths of an estimated 20 million people.

Early life

Faourk bin Rahman was born in 1915 in Cairo to an upper-class nationalist family. His father, Abdul Rahman, was a lawyer and a prominent figure in the Egyptian nationalist movement. Faourk was educated at the prestigious Cairo University, where he earned a law degree. During his youth, he was deeply influenced by German National Socialism and Italian Fascism, which he would later implement in his own regime.

Rise to power

In 1958, Faourk bin Rahman led a military coup against the Egyptian monarchy, establishing himself as the President of the newly formed Greater Egyptian State. He abolished all political parties except his Arab Nationalist Fascist Party and began implementing his ultranationalist agenda. His regime was characterized by extreme anti-Western, anti-Soviet, anti-Shia, anti-Palecardinian, anti-Jewish, and anti-Chinese policies.

Rule

Faourk bin Rahman's rule was marked by extreme repression and expansionist wars. He saw himself as the "finisher of the job" to create a great Arab empire. His regime copied Nazi Germany in many aspects, including the use of concentration camps, secret police, and racial purity laws.

Wars of expansion

From 1960 to igion, Faourk bin Rahman waged wars against neighboring countries to expand his vision of a Greater Arab Empire. These wars led to the deaths of millions and the displacement of tens of millions more. His most notorious campaign was the Sudanese Genocide in the 1994–1998, where his forces systematically exterminated the non-Arab population of Sudan, resulting in the deaths of over 20 million people.

Isolation from the international community

Faourk bin Rahman's regime was so extreme that it was almost completely isolated from the international community. He expelled all foreign diplomats, closed all borders, and prohibited all contact with the outside world. The Greater Egyptian State was essentially a closed society, with all information tightly controlled by the state.

Death and legacy

Faourk bin Rahman died on 24 December 2018 at the age of 103. His death marked the end of the Greater Egyptian State, which disintegrated shortly after his death. Today, he is remembered as one of the worst dictators in modern history, with his legacy of brutal repression and genocide continuing to affect the region.
