Chinese mobs torch Russian embassy after Moscow attack, triggering murderous retaliation across Russia

By CNN International Staff

Beijing — China struck back with unprecedented fury on Tuesday, as massive crowds stormed and burned the Russian embassy in Beijing while mobs destroyed Russian businesses across the country, pushing the world's two largest nuclear powers to the brink of war.

The revenge attacks, which followed Russia's burning of the Chinese embassy in Moscow two days earlier, triggered murderous retaliation across Russia, where ultranationalists began systematically murdering Chinese citizens and bombing Chinese factories.

The violence spread like wildfire across both nations. In Beijing, Shanghai, and Guangzhou, Russian restaurants and businesses were systematically destroyed by organized mobs. Protesters dragged Russian citizens into the streets, beating them while police stood by.

Meanwhile, in Moscow, St. Petersburg, and Vladivostok, Russian ultranationalists began hunting Chinese citizens. Multiple Chinese factories were bombed, with reports of Chinese workers being murdered in the streets.
