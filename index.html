u/HandwritingAnalysis Document Examiner • 2 hours ago

That signature is a classic case of "bureaucratic degradation" - starts normal in 1933, gets progressively worse as he signs thousands of documents. By 1940 it's shaky, by 1945 it's illegible. The 1943 War Department ID card shows the same decline.

Also notice he signs "Carl R Paul Jr" - drops the middle name and periods. Common for people who sign their name constantly. The "Jr" gets smaller each time, probably because he got tired of writing it.

Fun fact: government workers in the 1940s-50s often had terrible signatures from carbon copies - you had to press hard to make 4-5 copies, which destroyed fine motor control.
