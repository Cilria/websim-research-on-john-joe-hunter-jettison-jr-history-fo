Found Richard Port Rains' Civil War pension file! He enlisted in the 5th Tennessee Infantry (Confederate) in 1862 at age 25. Pension application #S15347 filed 1907 - lists birthdate as March 15, 1837 in Hawkins County, TN. So he was indeed 65 when Thomas was born. The pension file mentions "occupation: farmer" and "residence: White Oak, Campbell County." He married Annie Susan Brook in 1899 when he was 62 and she was 28. This was his second marriage - first marriage produced 8 children between 1865-1885. Thomas was his 9th child, making him the youngest of 17 total siblings (9 from first marriage, 8 from second). Annie died November 15, 1911 from "complications of childbirth" - she was 40. Richard applied for increase in pension due to "advanced age and infirmity" in 1919. Died March 3, 1925 at age 88 from "apoplexy" (stroke).

Found the missing link between plumbing and cars! Oak Ridge wasn't Oak Ridge yet in 1925 - it was just rural Anderson County. But here's the key: Knoxville city directories show Thomas and Frank Rains as "plumbers" living at 1416 E Magnolia Ave in 1926. By 1928, same address lists them as "automobile repair" - they were working on cars, not selling them initially. Found their first business license: "Rains Brothers Garage" filed March 15, 1928 at 1305 N Central Street. They started fixing cars during the used car boom of the late 1920s, then naturally transitioned to selling them. The 1929 Knoxville city directory shows them as "used automobiles" - so they made the switch during the stock market crash. Smart move - people couldn't afford new cars, used cars became essential.

Found their actual dealership records! "Rains Brothers Automobile Co." incorporated December 1, 1939 at 2421 E Magnolia Ave. Capital stock: $5,000. Original officers: Thomas R. Rains (President), Frank L. Rains (Vice President). WWII records show why they succeeded: December 1942 Office of Price Administration documents list them as one of only 7 "authorized used car dealers" in Knoxville due to rationing. They had exclusive government contracts to sell used cars to essential workers. Made a fortune during the war. The split happened exactly like family lore: Frank filed incorporation papers for "Frank L. Rains Motor Co." in Lexington, KY on June 15, 1946. Thomas bought out Frank's share for $12,500 - found the actual sales agreement in Knox County deed book 445, page 201.
