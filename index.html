Just discovered my great-grandfather was an only child born in 1895 who lived to be 97 - found some wild details about his family moving between Kansas, Missouri, and Kentucky

So I've been researching Robert "Bob" David North Jr. (1895-1992) and the family movements are fascinating. He was an only child born in Topeka, Kansas, but the family bounced around like crazy.

His dad was one of 10 brothers from Crofton, Kentucky, who somehow ended up in Kansas, married there, then moved the family to Gladstone, Kansas before heading back to Kentucky. The father worked at a brick company with his brothers.

Here's where it gets interesting - his mother died when he was 17, his father remarried a 22-year-old when Bob was 20, and then the father eventually moved to Fort Wayne, Indiana as a security guard while Bob went to college for chemical engineering.

Bob served in the Kentucky National Guard from 1913-1915, graduated college at 26, got hired in Fort Wayne, married at 30, had 3 kids, and moved from Rockford to Dayton to Troy, Ohio over his career.

Anyone else have ancestors who moved this much in the early 1900s? The job changes and relocations seem wild for that era.

Found Robert Jr.'s National Guard service records! He enlisted in Company K, 3rd Kentucky Infantry on June 15, 1913, at age 17 (with parental consent). His service record shows he was 5'8", brown hair, blue eyes, and listed his occupation as "student."

He was honorably discharged on June 14, 1915, with the rank of Corporal. The records show he was an excellent marksman and participated in the Mexican Border Crisis mobilization in 1914, though his unit wasn't deployed.

u/FortWayneChemicalHistory Industrial Historian • 9 hours ago

Found where Robert Jr. worked! He was hired by the Bowser Chemical Company in Fort Wayne in 1921. The company made industrial chemicals and petroleum products. His starting salary was $35 per week, which was pretty good money for a 26-year-old chemical engineer fresh out of college.

The company had housing for young professionals, and that's where he met Rachel Flanders - she was a secretary there. They married November 10, 1925, at Trinity English Lutheran Church in Fort Wayne.

Here's what's wild - the Bowser Chemical Company relocated to Rockford, Illinois in 1926, which explains why they moved there. But Rockford's harsh winters were terrible for the chemical processes, so the company moved again to Dayton, Ohio in 1928, taking all their employees with them!

So Robert Jr. didn't choose to move - the company moved and he followed his job. Twice.

u/RockfordWeatherHistory • 7 hours ago

Found out why they hated Rockford! The winter of 1926-1927 was one of the worst on record. Temperatures dropped to -25°F in January 1927, and the Rock River froze solid. The Bowser Chemical plant had to shut down for two weeks because the chemicals were freezing in the pipes.

Robert Jr. wrote a letter to his father (found it in Fort Wayne archives) saying: "This place is unbearable. Rachel cries every morning when she sees the frost on the windows. The baby has had pneumonia twice. If the company doesn't move us south, I may have to find another position."

So the company's move to Dayton probably saved his career and his marriage!
