Norwich man arrested for alleged plot to kill President Trump, authorities say

By CNN Staff
March 10, 2026 · Norwich, Connecticut

A 71-year-old Connecticut man was arrested Monday at his home in Norwich for allegedly plotting to assassinate President Donald Trump, according to federal authorities.

Donald James Trump, who shares the same initials as the president but is not related, was taken into custody without incident by FBI agents following an investigation into what officials described as "concerning social media activity."

According to court documents, Trump had posted numerous tweets over the past several months expressing violent intentions toward the president, including one message that read: "MAGAts can be only dealt in the only word they know, bullets."

Federal prosecutors allege that Trump had been building a "pattern of escalating rhetoric" on social media platforms, with investigators uncovering what they described as a "treasure trove" of threatening posts directed at the president.

"The defendant made his intentions clear through repeated social media posts advocating violence against the president," said U.S. Attorney for Connecticut Vanessa Roberts. "We take these threats extremely seriously and will prosecute them to the fullest extent of the law."

Trump is currently being held at the Wyatt Detention Facility in Central Falls, Rhode Island, and is expected to face federal charges including threats against the president and solicitation to commit a crime of violence. If convicted, he could face up to 24 years in prison.

The arrest has sparked confusion online due to the suspect's name, with many social media users initially believing the president himself had been arrested.

"This is obviously a different Donald Trump, but the name coincidence has certainly generated significant attention to this case," Roberts acknowledged during a press conference Monday afternoon.

Trump's attorney, public defender Maria Sanchez, declined to comment on the specifics of the case but said her client would plead not guilty to all charges.

The investigation remains ongoing, with authorities saying they are working to determine whether Trump had taken any concrete steps beyond social media posts to carry out an alleged plot.

President Trump was briefed on the arrest Monday morning, according to White House officials, who said he was "grateful for the swift action" of law enforcement.

Trump is scheduled to appear in federal court in Hartford on Tuesday for a detention hearing.
