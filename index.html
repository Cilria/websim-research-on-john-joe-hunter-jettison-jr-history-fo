Katrakis seizes absolute power in midnight 'Athens Laws' coup

Greek leader executes President, abolishes presidency, cancels all elections in sweeping authoritarian takeover
By BBC Athens Correspondent
PRESIDENT EXECUTED: Katrakis assumes absolute power in bloodless coup

Greek Leader Nikolaos Katrakis executed President Konstantinos Stephanopoulos at midnight Wednesday before abolishing the presidency entirely, seizing all executive powers as he unveiled sweeping "Athens Laws" that eliminate the last vestiges of democratic governance and establish what analysts call Europe's first absolute dictatorship since the 1970s.

In a chilling televised address delivered from the Presidential Mansion just hours after the execution, Katrakis declared the "end of the failed presidential system" and announced that all presidential powers would henceforth reside with him as "Leader of the Hellanic State."

CONSTITUTIONAL ABOLITION

The presidency, established in 1974 following the fall of the military junta, has been formally abolished. Katrakis now combines legislative, executive, and judicial powers - a concentration of authority unseen in modern European history.

The Athens Laws, comprising 47 separate decrees signed into effect simultaneously, represent the most comprehensive authoritarian power grab in contemporary European politics. The package eliminates all checks and balances, dissolves democratic institutions, and establishes what constitutional scholars describe as "pure autocracy."

"The era of divided government is over," Katrakis proclaimed. "The Leader must have absolute authority to guide the nation through these dangerous times. The failed experiment with democracy has ended."

The most dramatic provisions include the immediate firing and subsequent execution of President Stephanopoulos, who was shot by firing squad at 11:47 PM Tuesday after refusing to sign the Athens Laws. State television broadcast footage of the execution, describing it as "necessary justice against presidential obstruction."

KEY PROVISIONS OF THE ATHENS LAWS

• Presidency abolished; all powers transferred to Leader
• Ministry of Defense renamed "War Ministry" under loyalist control
• All civil servants must prove loyalty or face dismissal
• Parliamentary elections cancelled indefinitely
• Parliament renamed "National Assembly" with appointed members
• Greece claims right to "Greater Greece" territories
• Mission declared to "restore rightful borders and punish 1919 traitors"

The restructuring eliminates the traditional cabinet system, replacing it with direct rule by Katrakis and a small council of loyalists called the "Council of National Salvation." All government ministers now serve at the Leader's pleasure and can be dismissed instantly.

Perhaps most significantly, the laws cancel all future elections and transform the parliament into an appointed body. The National Assembly, as it is now called, will have its members selected personally by Katrakis and the Council of National Salvation, eliminating any pretense of democratic representation.

"Elections are wasteful and divisive," the decree states. "The Leader, chosen by historical necessity and divine providence, shall select those best qualified to serve the national interest."

"The Athens Laws represent the death of Greek democracy. Katrakis has achieved what no European leader has dared attempt since the 1940s - absolute personal rule without democratic legitimacy."
— Professor Anna Triandafyllidou, European University Institute

The laws also include expansive foreign policy provisions, declaring Greece's right to "all territories historically part of the Hellenic world" and specifically mentioning Cyprus, parts of Turkey, Albania, and Macedonia. The decrees establish "restoring Greater Greece" as official state policy.

"The mission of the Hellanic State is clear," Katrakis declared. "We must restore our rightful borders and punish the traitors and scum who backstabbed our nation in 1919. The humiliation of the past century ends today."

The reference to 1919 appears to target the Treaty of Sèvres and subsequent territorial losses, which Greek ultranationalists have long viewed as national betrayal. The laws specifically mention "reclaiming Constantinople and restoring the Byzantine Empire's glory."

To celebrate the "great victories," Katrakis ordered massive rallies in Athens and other major cities. State media showed thousands of supporters waving the darker blue flag adopted from 1968-1974, now the official flag of the Hellanic State.

INTERNATIONAL ALARM

European leaders expressed grave concern about the territorial claims. The abolition of democracy combined with expansionist rhetoric recalls the darkest periods of European history. NATO allies are particularly alarmed by the War Ministry's new aggressive posture.

The international response was immediate and harsh. European Commission President Ursula von der Leyen called the Athens Laws "a constitutional coup that ends Greek democracy forever," while U.S. President declared them "an assault on Western civilization itself."

Inside Greece, citizens expressed both terror and resignation at the new reality. "Democracy is dead," said one Athens resident, speaking anonymously. "We now live under absolute rule. The Leader's word is law, and there's nothing anyone can do about it."

The Athens Laws represent the culmination of Katrakis's systematic destruction of Greek democracy. What began with emergency decrees in January has ended with absolute dictatorship, as Europe witnesses the most comprehensive authoritarian takeover since the fall of communism.

As dawn broke over Athens, military vehicles patrolled streets where loudspeakers declared "the Leader's absolute authority is now eternal." Greece has completed its transformation from European democracy to totalitarian state, with one man's word now carrying the force of law.
