LinguisticsProf Historical Linguist 4 hours ago

Let me explain why the "too many Arabic loanwords" argument is completely moronic.

Armenian has Arabic loanwords for the same reason English has French loanwords: historical contact and prestige. After the Arab conquests in the 7th century, Arabic became the language of administration and religion, so Armenian borrowed some administrative and religious terms.

But here's the thing - every language has loanwords. English has thousands of French words, but that doesn't make English speakers secretly French.

Armenian has retained its core vocabulary - the basic words for family, body parts, numbers, everyday objects - that go back to Proto-Indo-European. If Armenians were really Arabs who "switched identities," why would they keep the Indo-European grammar and core vocabulary while somehow erasing all traces of Arabic?

Your friend doesn't understand how language change actually works.
