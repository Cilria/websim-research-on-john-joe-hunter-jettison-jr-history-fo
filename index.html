Katrakis orders mass conscription of 2 million men, creates ethnic citizenship purge

Greek dictator mandates military service for all men 18-65, establishes death penalty for draft dodgers while stripping citizenship from "non-Greeks"
By CNN Athens Correspondent
MASS CONSCRIPTION: All Greek men 18-65 must serve; execution ordered for resisters

Greek Leader Nikolaos Katrakis signed two sweeping decrees Wednesday that mandate universal military conscription for all men aged 18 to 65 while simultaneously stripping citizenship rights from anyone deemed "non-Greek," effectively creating the largest military mobilization in European history while launching an ethnic cleansing campaign targeting minorities.

The "National Service Decree" requires every male citizen between 18 and 65 to report for military duty within 72 hours or face execution as "enemies of the Hellanic State." The order, announced during a thunderous speech before 50,000 supporters in Athens' Syntagma Square, will expand Greece's military from 150,000 to nearly 2 million soldiers - a force larger than any European military except Russia's.

UNIVERSAL CONSCRIPTION

All Greek men aged 18-65 must serve immediately. No exceptions for medical conditions, family obligations, or professional duties. Refusal constitutes "enemy status" punishable by immediate execution without trial.

"The Machine of War has begun!" Katrakis thundered to the roaring crowd, his voice echoing through loudspeakers across the capital. "The scum that backstabbed the nation in 1919 will face the wrath of 2 million Greek warriors! We will reclaim what is ours by blood and by right!"

The speech concluded with 15 minutes of chants led by Katrakis himself: "Glory to Katrakis! Glory to the Leader! Glory to Greater Greece!" State television broadcast the entire event, showing military officers saluting as civilians wept with apparent joy.

War Ministry officials, led by General Demetrios Pagonis, immediately began implementing what they term "Operation Phoenix Rising" - a massive mobilization plan that includes reopening closed military bases, converting schools into barracks, and establishing training facilities in every prefecture. The military will absorb nearly 15% of the entire male population.

CONSCRIPTION REQUIREMENTS

• Report within 72 hours or face execution
• No medical or family exemptions
• Service duration: minimum 2 years
• Death penalty for draft evasion
• Families of resisters sent to camps

The second decree, titled "Ethnic Purity and Citizenship Restoration Act," creates an ethnic litmus test for citizenship that experts describe as "Nazi-style racial purification." The law establishes special tribunals to determine who qualifies as "ethnically Greek," with vague criteria based on ancestry, language, religion, and "cultural compatibility."

Anyone failing this test loses all citizenship rights and faces immediate deportation to the expanding network of "relocation camps" in northern Greece. The law specifically targets ethnic minorities - Albanians, Macedonians, Turks, Roma, and recent immigrants - but also includes political dissidents and their families.

ETHNIC CLEANSING CAMPAIGN

The citizenship law creates racial tribunals with arbitrary power to strip citizenship. Minorities face immediate deportation to camps, while mixed families must prove "Greek blood" through ancestry tests. The law explicitly mentions "purifying the nation for the coming struggle."

"Greece must be pure for the coming struggle," the decree states. "Only those of true Hellenic blood can share in the glory of our restoration. The rest must be relocated to make room for the returning diaspora."

Human rights organizations report that the citizenship tribunals began operating immediately, with mobile units conducting racial assessments in villages across Greece. Those deemed "non-Greek" receive 24-hour deportation notices, while their property is confiscated for "repatriation to true Greeks."

The military expansion serves multiple purposes beyond defense, according to War Ministry documents obtained by CNN. The massive conscription will provide forced labor for infrastructure projects, man defensive positions along the borders, and create a "people's army" loyal personally to Katrakis rather than institutional structures.

The international response was swift and horrified. European Union President declared the measures "ethnic cleansing disguised as military service," while NATO officials expressed alarm about a 2-million-man army under the control of an increasingly unstable dictator. Turkey mobilized troops along its border, citing "existential threats" from the territorial claims embedded in the conscription decree.

Inside Greece, families scrambled to understand the new reality. Men over 65 who thought themselves safe from military duty now face conscription. Business owners watched as their workforces disappeared into military service. Hospitals prepared to operate without male doctors, schools without male teachers.

"My 64-year-old father must report tomorrow," said one Athens resident, speaking anonymously. "My husband is already in the army. Now my teenage son will join him. We're losing every male in our family to this madness."

CAMP EXPANSION

The conscription provides cover for expanding the camp system. Military-age men who fail ethnic purity tests are sent to "labor battalions" - effectively death camps with military uniforms. The camps now hold an estimated 150,000 people, with capacity expanding daily.

War Ministry sources reveal that General Pagonis has begun planning for military operations, with invasion scenarios developed for Cyprus, parts of Albania, and Greek-populated regions of southern Italy. The massive conscription is designed to provide cannon fodder for these expansionist wars.

As night fell over Athens, military trucks patrolled neighborhoods, loudspeakers announcing "The age of the warrior has begun! Report for duty or face the consequences!" The city that invented democracy now serves as ground zero for Europe's most comprehensive authoritarian experiment since the 1940s.

The conscription represents Katrakis's most ambitious gamble yet - transforming an entire society into a military machine while eliminating ethnic minorities who might resist his vision of Greater Greece. With 2 million men under arms and concentration camps expanding daily, Greece has become what historians warn could be "the most dangerous regime in modern European history."
