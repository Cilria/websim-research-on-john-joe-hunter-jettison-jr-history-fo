Looking through bankruptcy records at the Kansas Historical Society - his music shop closure in 1930 was sadly common. Found a heartbreaking newspaper quote where he said "Music might not feed bellies, but it feeds the soul." 📰

But he bounced back! By 1932, he was running a successful carpentry business, specializing in custom cabinets and furniture. Resilient guy!

u/LawrenceHistorian

Local history buff here! The building where his music shop was still stands - it's a coffee shop now, but they kept the original tin ceiling he installed. Also found photos in the Douglas County Historical Society archives showing his carpentry work - he built some gorgeous houses that are still standing in Old West Lawrence! 🏠

Fun fact: He was known for hiding a penny with the current year in every cabinet he built - sort of his trademark! People still find them during renovations.

u/AncestralDigger

His father was Samuel James Rivenburg (1858-1912) - found him in the FamilySearch Kansas Collections! The 6 Rivenburg boys were: William Arthur Rivenburg (1885-1942), Charles Edward Rivenburg (1887-1965), George Henry Rivenburg (1889-1944), Robert Thomas Rivenburg (1891-1978), James Frederick Rivenburg (1893-1969), and Samuel Walter Rivenburg (1896-1918). Their mother was Margaret Ellen Wilson Rivenburg (1862-1935). 📚

Tragic fact - youngest brother Samuel Walter Rivenburg died in the 1918 flu pandemic while serving stateside during WWI.

u/KansasNewspaperBuff

Found some amazing articles in the Marshall County News archives! The Rivenburg boys were quite the musical family - they had a family band in the 1900s called "The Rivenburg Brothers Orchestra". No wonder Bob went into the music business! 🎺

Also found his marriage announcement from 1919 - he married Dorothy Mae Henderson (1895-1982), daughter of prominent Lawrence merchant Thomas Franklin Henderson. Her family owned Henderson's Dry Goods on Massachusetts Street.

u/DouglasRecords

His kids carried on some interesting traditions! His son Robert Thomas Rivenburg Jr. (1920-2003) became a well-known piano tuner, and his daughter Margaret Ellen Rivenburg (1922-2015) taught music at Lawrence High School for 40 years! Found their stories in the Lawrence Journal-World archives. 🎹

Fun fact: During WWII, he converted his workshop to make wooden airplane parts for the war effort. The County Historical Society has his government contracts!
