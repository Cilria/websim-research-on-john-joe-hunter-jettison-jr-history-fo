u/KnoxRecorder

Oh man, Montgomery Motors! Found some amazing old ads in the Knox County Library Digital Archives. His first showroom was on Gay Street - there's this fantastic photo from 1944 with him standing proudly in front of a brand new Studebaker! 📸

The Knoxville Historical Society has a whole file on his business. He was one of the first dealers to offer financing plans in East Tennessee! 💰

u/AppalachianPast

Just checked the Campbell County Archives - found his school records! He only went through 8th grade before heading to Kingston. There's a great story in the local paper about how he started as a stockboy at Miller & Sons General Store in 1917. 📦

Also, check out this wild tidbit from the Kingston Historical Association - apparently he lived above the store and saved every penny to buy his first car to resell! Talk about determination! 💪

u/CarCollector55

The Tennessee Auto Dealers Association has some incredible records! Montgomery Motors was one of the largest dealerships in East TN by 1960. He won "Dealer of the Year" three times! 🏆

Found an amazing oral history interview from 1982 in the UT Business History Collection where he talks about selling his first car - a used Model T he fixed up himself! He made $50 profit and never looked back! 🚙

u/NewsDigger1950

You've gotta see the feature they did on him in the 1975 Knoxville News Sentinel! There's this fantastic spread about his 30th anniversary in business. He talks about how he survived the 1950s recession by taking trades on everything from livestock to furniture! 😮

The East Tennessee Business Hall of Fame inducted him in 1980 - their archives have some fascinating photos of his various showrooms through the years. The evolution from that tiny Gay Street shop to his final 5-acre dealership is incredible! 🌟
