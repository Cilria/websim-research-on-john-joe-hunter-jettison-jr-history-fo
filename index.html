Peru's Antauro Humala declares absolute rule, orders executions of former presidents including his own brother

By CNN Staff
Updated 6:34 PM ET, Sat November 22, 2026

Lima, Peru (CNN) — Peru's self-declared president Antauro Humala issued a sweeping decree Saturday granting himself absolute power and formally establishing what he termed the "Incan Restoration," while ordering the immediate execution of all former Peruvian presidents, including his own brother Ollanta Humala, according to state television broadcasts.

The decree, read by Humala in full military dress at 6:00 PM local time, declared martial law "in perpetuity" and formally banned all opposition political parties, media organizations, and what he termed "foreign-influenced institutions." The announcement came just hours after military forces completed the takeover of Peru's last remaining independent news outlets.

LIVE UPDATES: Military forces have surrounded the homes of former presidents Alejandro Toledo, Pedro Pablo Kuczynski, and Martín Vizcarra. Witnesses report gunfire at former president Ollanta Humala's residence.

"The era of the copper-skinned ruler has arrived," Humala declared from the balcony of the Government Palace, addressing thousands of supporters while wearing a traditional Incan headdress. "From this moment forward, only those with copper skin shall rule Peru. The restoration of Tawantinsuyu begins today."

The decree establishes what Humala calls the "Supreme Incan Authority," granting him unchecked power to rule by decree. It also formally dissolves Congress, the Supreme Court, and all regional governments, replacing them with military governors reporting directly to Humala.

Key provisions of Humala's decree:
  • Absolute martial law declared "until the complete restoration of the Incan empire"
  • All opposition parties banned and declared "enemies of the people"
  • Immediate execution orders for all former presidents
  • Mandatory military service for all citizens aged 18-45
  • Claims to "former Incan territories" including parts of Ecuador, Bolivia, Chile, Argentina, and Colombia

International human rights organizations expressed horror at the announcement. "This represents one of the most dramatic descents into totalitarianism in modern Latin American history," said José Miguel Vivanco, Americas director at Human Rights Watch. "The international community must act immediately."

Humala's claims to "former Incan territories" include large swaths of neighboring countries, raising fears of regional conflict. The decree specifically mentions "the liberation of Quito, La Paz, Santiago, and Buenos Aires from colonial occupation."

The execution orders target eight former presidents, including Humala's brother Ollanta, who served as president from 2011-2016. "My brother betrayed the Andean people by serving foreign masters," Humala said. "He will pay the price alongside all who corrupted our nation."

Neighboring countries immediately closed their borders with Peru, while the Organization of American States called an emergency session for Sunday morning. Colombian President Gustavo Petro warned that "any incursion into Colombian territory will be met with full military force."

The decree also establishes mandatory military service for all Peruvian citizens aged 18-45, with Humala claiming "a great army must be built for the coming liberation of our ancestral lands."

State television has begun broadcasting propaganda videos showing Humala in traditional Incan attire, claiming descent from the Sapa Inca rulers. "The blood of the sun god runs through my veins," he declared. "I am the rightful heir to the throne of the Andes."

As night fell in Lima, military vehicles patrolled the streets while loudspeakers broadcast Humala's decree continuously. Citizens rushed to grocery stores and banks, creating chaos as people attempted to flee the capital.

The U.S. State Department announced it is evacuating all non-essential personnel from Peru, while the European Union called for an immediate emergency summit to address the crisis. "We are witnessing the birth of a totalitarian state that threatens the entire region," said EU foreign policy chief Josep Borrell.

Humala concluded his decree with a chilling warning: "The world will remember November 22, 2026, as the day the Incan empire rose from the ashes. Those who oppose us will be crushed beneath the sandals of our warriors."
