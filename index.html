China-Russia border war erupts as armed militias clash

Armed Chinese citizens seize villages while Russian troops open fire, militias burn border towns

By CNN International Staff
November 11, 2026 • 6:47 PM ET

Beijing — The world stood on the precipice of nuclear war Tuesday as armed Chinese citizens crossed the border into Russia, seizing control of small border villages while Russian troops opened fire, marking the first direct military clashes between the two nuclear-armed superpowers.

Simultaneously, Russian ultranationalist militias launched cross-border raids into China, burning villages and engaging in running gun battles with Chinese border guards, pushing the world's most dangerous rivalry into open warfare.

Chinese Foreign Ministry spokesperson Liu Wei responded with equal venom: "Russia has revealed itself as a terrorist state that funds separatist movements against China's territorial integrity. We will respond with overwhelming force to restore order to our border regions."

The border violence erupted early Tuesday when hundreds of armed Chinese citizens, claiming to be "patriotic volunteers," crossed into Russia's Primorsky Krai region and seized control of three small villages. Russian border troops opened fire, killing at least 47 Chinese nationals, according to Russian state media.

"We have confirmed intelligence that China is funding separatist movements throughout Russia. They seek to break apart our motherland. We will not allow this - we will destroy them first!"

Within hours, Russian ultranationalist militias launched retaliatory raids across the border into China's Heilongjiang province. Eyewitnesses report seeing armed Russian civilians burning Chinese border villages while chanting "Death to China!" and "Russia for Russians!"

"They came at dawn with torches and guns," sobbed Zhang Mei, a Chinese villager who fled across the border. "They burned our homes, shot anyone who tried to stop them. They kept screaming that we were subhuman and didn't belong on Russian land."

Both nations have now formally accused each other of funding separatist movements within their territories. Russia claims China is bankrolling independence movements in Siberia and the Far East, while China alleges Russia is supporting Uyghur and Tibetan separatists.

"We have irrefutable evidence that Russian intelligence services have been funneling weapons and money to terrorist groups seeking to break apart China," declared China's Defense Ministry in a midnight statement. "This is an act of war that demands total retaliation."

Territorial Ambitions Revealed

Both governments have now openly declared territorial ambitions. China claims "Outer Manchuria" - territories lost to Russia in the 19th century. Russia claims Chinese border regions historically populated by Russian settlers. The stage is set for total war.

The situation deteriorated rapidly as Russian state media began broadcasting detailed maps showing planned territorial expansions into Chinese territory. Chinese state television responded by airing historical maps claiming vast swaths of modern-day Russia as "traditional Chinese lands."

"We will reclaim every inch of territory stolen from China by the Russian barbarians!" thundered Chinese state television host Wang Li. "From Vladivostok to Lake Baikal - all will return to the motherland!"

As night fell, both nations moved military assets toward their shared 4,200-kilometer border. Chinese state media reported the deployment of "defensive forces" to protect against "Russian invasion," while Russia announced the mobilization of "border protection units."

Western intelligence sources tell CNN that both nations have placed their nuclear forces on heightened alert status, though neither has made explicit nuclear threats - yet.

CNN Analysis

The world is witnessing the unthinkable: two nuclear-armed superpowers sliding toward direct military conflict over territorial disputes. With armed militias now engaging in cross-border raids and both governments openly declaring territorial ambitions, we stand at the most dangerous moment since the Cuban Missile Crisis. The risk of miscalculation leading to nuclear exchange has never been higher.

By midnight Tuesday, reports emerged of additional cross-border incursions. Armed Chinese groups claimed to have seized more Russian villages, while Russian militias claimed to have "liberated" Chinese border towns with significant ethnic Russian populations.

"The war has begun," declared Russian ultranationalist leader Viktor Volkov in a chilling video posted online. "We will not stop until Beijing burns as Moscow burns. The final solution to the Chinese question is at hand."

As dawn approaches on Wednesday, the world holds its breath, watching two nuclear powers slide toward a war that could end civilization itself. The border skirmishes of Tuesday may be remembered as the opening shots of World War III.
