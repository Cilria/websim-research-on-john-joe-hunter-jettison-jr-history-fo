TIL Sam Bennett went from a 17-year-old hardware store apprentice in Waterloo, Iowa to building a $60 million empire with hotels, cruise ships, and coal companies - all while slapping his name on everything

This dude Samuel Edward "Sam" Bennett Sr. was born in 1876 in Manchester, Iowa. His dad was literally named Christopher Columbus Bennett (what a name) and was a Methodist minister. At 12, Sam decided "fuck that noise, I'm not becoming another minister" and at 17 bounced to Waterloo to work as a store apprentice.

1896 - dude's 20 and starts "Bennett's Fine Hardware" with the slogan "The Finest Hardware in Midwest" (guy couldn't even grammar check but whatever). Store blows up so much he needs a second location just to handle demand.

1904 - Sam's like "I can put my name on anything and make bank" and creates Bennett Fence Co. Sells fencing to Dakota farmers and pulls in $650k. Then he's like "why stop there?" and starts Bennett Ironworks and Bennett United Virginia Coal Co.

By 1910 - dude's worth $2.9 million which was stupid money back then. Then he goes full Rockefeller mode:

  • 1911 - Bennett Intercontinental Hotels in Boulder
  • 1916 - B&W Cruise with some guy named John Ford Williams
  • 1920s - worth $60 million and built a massive mansion in Boulder

Had 7 kids between 1903-1920. Wrote a couple books including "What America Needs" in 1932 which was basically just him ranting about needing a strong president. Died in 1958.

The ego on this guy was legendary - would literally put "Bennett" on anything that stood still long enough.
