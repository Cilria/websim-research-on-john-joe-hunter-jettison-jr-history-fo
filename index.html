u/MixedMarriageHistorian • 11 hours ago

Mixed Protestant-Catholic marriages were HUGE deals in rural America in the 1890s. Some Protestant ministers wouldn't even perform them, and Catholic priests required promises to raise kids Catholic.

The elopement to Kansas City was smart - Kansas had more relaxed marriage laws. Missouri required parental consent until age 21, but Kansas only required it until 18. Robert was 26, Jean was 23, so they could marry without permission.

Family legend says Jean's father Michael was secretly relieved - he thought Baptists were weird but at least they weren't "drunken Irish Catholics" like his wife's relatives. The North family took longer to come around.

After Jean died in 1912, her Catholic family wanted Bob Jr. raised Catholic. The North family refused and threatened to take him to Kentucky. It got ugly - lawyers were involved before they reached a compromise.
