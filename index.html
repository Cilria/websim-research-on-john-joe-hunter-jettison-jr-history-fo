NBC News: Robert Francis Hollomon Sentenced to 10 Years in Minimum Security Prison

After a six-week trial, South Carolina State Senator Robert Francis Hollomon was sentenced today to 10 years in federal prison for his role in the $11 billion fraud scheme. His brother, LeRoy Hollomon, received a 60-year sentence as the court determined he was "the real mastermind" behind the decade-long operation.

Federal prosecutors had sought a minimum of 25 years for the 58-year-old former senator, but the judge cited his "limited role" and "cooperation with authorities" in imposing the lighter sentence. Hollomon will serve his time at the Federal Correctional Institution in Butner, North Carolina - a low-security facility sometimes referred to as "Club Fed" due to its relatively comfortable conditions.
