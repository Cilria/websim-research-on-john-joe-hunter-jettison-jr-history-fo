Centenarian economist who predicted every recession since 1957 warns of 2027 downturn

Robert Allard Coleman Jr., who turns 100 next month, says current economic conditions mirror those seen before 2008 financial crisis

By BBC Business Correspondent
1 July 2026
Robert Allard Coleman Jr. has correctly predicted every US recession since 1957

Robert Allard Coleman Jr., the world's oldest actively working economist, has issued a stark warning that the global economy is heading for a significant recession in early 2027, drawing parallels to the conditions that preceded the 2008 financial crisis.

Coleman, who celebrated his 99th birthday last month and will turn 100 in August, has accurately predicted every American recession since 1957, including the 2008 financial crisis and the brief but sharp downturn of 2020.

Coleman's Track Record

  • 1957: Predicted recession, warned of auto sector decline
  • 1960: Forecast downturn amid manufacturing slump
  • 1969-70: Predicted stagflation period
  • 1973-75: Forecast oil crisis impact
  • 1980-82: Predicted double-dip recession
  • 1990-91: Forecast Gulf War economic impact
  • 2001: Predicted dot-com bust
  • 2007-09: Forecast housing market collapse
  • 2020: Predicted pandemic-induced recession

"The warning signs are flashing red," Coleman told the BBC in an interview at his Harvard University office, where he still maintains a full teaching and research schedule. "What we're seeing now - the yield curve inversion, the excessive risk-taking in commercial real estate, the overvaluation in tech stocks - it's a mirror image of 2006-2007."

The veteran economist, who first joined the Harvard faculty in 1952, points to several concerning indicators: commercial property values have fallen 35% from their 2024 peaks, corporate debt stands at record levels, and several major banks have reported significant losses on real estate loans.

"History doesn't repeat itself, but it often rhymes," Coleman said, adjusting his trademark bow tie. "The specific instruments causing concern have changed - it's not subprime mortgages this time, but commercial mortgage-backed securities and leveraged lending. But the underlying dynamics are disturbingly similar."

"I've lived through 17 recessions, and I can tell you: when you see this combination of excessive leverage, asset bubbles, and regulatory complacency, something breaks. It's not a matter of if, but when."
- Robert Allard Coleman Jr.

Coleman's remarkable forecasting record has earned him legendary status in economic circles. His methodology, which combines traditional economic indicators with what he terms "behavioral finance signals," has proved remarkably prescient. He correctly predicted the 2008 crisis in a 2006 paper that was initially dismissed by many peers.

Dr. Sarah Chen, Chair of the Federal Reserve's Council of Economic Advisers, acknowledged Coleman's expertise: "While we don't necessarily share his pessimistic outlook for 2027, Professor Coleman's track record demands that we take his concerns seriously. His analysis is always thorough and thought-provoking."

Coleman attributes his longevity and continued mental acuity to "good genes and staying curious about markets." He still reads academic papers daily, maintains a regular teaching schedule, and publishes research. His latest book, "Century of Booms and Busts," is due for release later this year.

Despite his grim forecast, Coleman remains optimistic about long-term economic prospects. "Recessions are a natural part of the business cycle. They're painful but necessary corrections. The key is being prepared and not repeating the mistakes of the past."
