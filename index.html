Hit a complete brick wall with William W. Dennis (1860-1935) - death cert lists parents as "unknown" and he appears out of nowhere in 1880

I've been trying to trace my great-great grandfather William W. Dennis for months and I'm at my wit's end. Here's what I know for certain:

  • Born July 1860 in Georgia (according to every census)
  • First appears in 1880 census as 20-year-old railroad track worker in Georgia
  • Married Alice Headley around 1896 (1900 census says married 4 years)
  • Moved to Tallahassee, Florida and became a merchant
  • Adopted two sons Percy and Joseph in 1910 (ages 3 and 1)
  • Served in US Navy during Spanish-American War (June 1898 - July 1899)
  • Received pension in South Carolina in 1901
  • Died November 10, 1935

Here's the kicker - his death certificate lists his parents as "unknown" for both names. Like, how does a man live to be 75 and nobody knows who his parents are? My grandmother said he never talked about his family and got angry when asked.

I've searched every Georgia county record from 1860-1880 and can't find ANY Dennis family that matches. It's like he materialized out of thin air at age 20. Has anyone dealt with something like this?
