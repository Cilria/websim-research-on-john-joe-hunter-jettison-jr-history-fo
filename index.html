Peru launches full-scale invasion of Bolivia after alleged terrorist attack

By CNN Staff
Updated 11:23 AM ET, Sat March 20, 2027

Lima, Peru (CNN) — Peruvian President Antauro Humala declared war on Bolivia Saturday morning, claiming without evidence that "Bolivian terrorists" attacked a military installation near the border. Within hours, over 200,000 Peruvian troops equipped through mandatory conscription crossed into Bolivian territory, according to state media.

Key developments:
  • 200,000+ Peruvian troops cross Bolivian border
  • Border gates destroyed in military assault
  • International community calls for immediate ceasefire
  • Bolivia denies involvement in alleged attack
  • UN Security Council emergency session scheduled
11:23 AM ET
BREAKING: Peruvian state television reports military forces have advanced 15 miles into Bolivian territory. Heavy fighting reported near the city of La Quiaca. Bolivian President Arce calls for "all Bolivians to resist the invader."
11:15 AM ET
Human Rights Watch condemns invasion: "This appears to be a manufactured pretext for territorial expansion. The international community must act immediately to prevent further bloodshed."
11:02 AM ET
Full transcript of Humala's speech: "The scummery of this fake nation of Bolivia has attacked our sovereignty. We have no choice but to remove this artificial state from existence. The restoration of our rightful territories begins NOW!"
10:47 AM ET
BREAKING: Video emerges showing Peruvian forces destroying the Desaguadero border crossing. The bridge, which connects Peru and Bolivia across Lake Titicaca, has been completely demolished.
10:30 AM ET
Military buildup confirmed: Satellite imagery shows Peruvian military columns stretching for miles along the border. Analysts estimate the force includes tanks, artillery, and infantry units mobilized through Humala's mandatory conscription program.
10:15 AM ET
Alleged attack details: Humala claims "Bolivian terrorists" attacked a Peruvian military base at 4:30 AM local time, killing 12 soldiers. No evidence provided. Bolivian government denies involvement, calling the claims "a blatant lie to justify invasion."
9:52 AM ET
War declaration: President Antauro Humala announces "special military operation" to "denazify and demilitarize Bolivia." Speech lasts 4 minutes, provides no evidence of Bolivian attack.
