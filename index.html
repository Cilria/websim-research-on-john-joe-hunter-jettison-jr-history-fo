Found the smoking gun! "Woodbro" was actually a small settlement that got absorbed into Barron County. Found Robert's birth record: June 10, 1882, born to James M. Newton and Nancy A. Stover, registered in Rice Lake, WI. James listed as "mill hand." The 1884-85 Wisconsin State Gazetteer lists James Newton as "laborer, Sheldon PO" - so the move to Sheldon happened between 1882-1884, not 1883 like family lore says. The 1885 state census shows the family in Sheldon: James (28), Nancy (26), Robert (3), plus siblings William (2) and baby Lillian (6 months). So they didn't move the whole brood at once - looks like they moved incrementally as kids were born.

Found James Newton's railroad employment records! Northern Pacific Railway employment cards show James Madison Newton hired as "section hand" (railroad maintenance) in Aberdeen, South Dakota on March 15, 1886. This explains the "first train he could find" story - he literally got a job with the railroad. Interestingly, he didn't go straight to farming. Holy shit, I found the actual arrival in Yakima! Northern Pacific Railway payroll records show James Newton transferred from Aberdeen, SD to Yakima, WA on March 10, 1888. His employment card notes "wife + 4 children" - so Nancy was pregnant with their 5th child during the move. Yakima County deed records show James purchased 40 acres in the Moxee Valley on April 15, 1888 for $400. The 1889 Washington Territory census lists the family: James (33, railroad section boss), Nancy (31), Robert (7), William (6), Lillian (4), plus new baby James Jr (1). The "always claimed Yakima as birthplace" thing makes total sense - Robert was 6 when they arrived, so formative childhood memories are from Washington. He probably genuinely forgot the Wisconsin years.
