u/MilitaryRecordsExpert • 16 hours ago • 342 points
Found his entire WW1 service record! Howard Kenneth Howell enlisted May 15, 1917 at age 22. Serial number 1,876,543. Served with Company C, 119th Infantry Regiment, 30th Division. Deployed to France September 1918, saw action in the Meuse-Argonne Offensive (October-November 1918). Wounded in action October 17, 1918 - shrapnel wound to left shoulder. Discharged May 31, 1919 at Camp Custer, MI. Here's the key: His military occupation specialty was "Radio Operator, 3rd Class" - this is why he became a radio operator after the war! The Army trained him in radio operations during training at Camp MacArthur, TX. This explains the career transition perfectly. Also found his VA disability file - received 20% disability for his shoulder wound, total $19.50/month starting 1920.
u/OregonRadioHistorian • 15 hours ago • 287 points
Found exactly how he got to Medford! After discharge, he worked briefly as electrician in Lansing, but then answered a newspaper ad. The Medford Mail Tribune from August 1921 has this ad: "WANTED: Radio Operator for new station KFJI. Must have experience. Good wages. Apply Box 247." Howard applied and got the job. KFJI Medford signed on air October 1921 - he was their first radio operator! Station later became KOOS-AM. He started at $35/week (good money in 1921). Found his employment records: Worked at KFJI/KOOS from 1921 until station sold in 1927. Then moved to commercial radio operations - did radio advertising for various clients, real estate ads, etc. The "real estate agent" part came from doing radio spots for local real estate companies, eventually got licensed himself.

Found his first marriage! Married Mary Elizabeth "Mae" Sullivan on April 15, 1920 at St. Mary's Catholic Church, Lansing, MI. She was 19, he was 25. Found why Seven Oaks! After divorce, Howard moved to Seven Oaks Retirement Community in 1970 - it was brand new then, marketed to "active seniors." He was 75 and living in a small apartment there. Here's the golf course connection: Seven Oaks had a 9-hole golf course that residents could use. Howard became obsessed with golf after moving there - played every day at 7 AM. March 22, 1988 was a Tuesday morning, he was crossing Highway 62 (now Seven Oaks Drive) to get to the first tee. Found the accident report: Semi driven by James R. Wilson, age 34. Howard was in crosswalk but didn't see truck (sun glare at 6:45 AM). Impact speed 35 mph. Died instantly from head trauma. He was wearing his "lucky" yellow golf sweater and had a tee time for 7:15 AM with two other residents.

Richland, MI native here - the Howell family farm is still there! It's on CR 665 just south of Richland village. The old farmhouse burned down in 1956, but the barn is still standing. Locals call it "the Howell place" even though it's been 3-4 owners since. Found old plat maps - the farm was 80 acres, purchased by Albert Howell in 1878 for $800. By 1895 when Howard was born, they had 10 kids, 3 milk cows, 12 chickens, and grew mostly corn and wheat.
