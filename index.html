The Insane Saga of Walter Jerome Harmon Sr. - Bigamist Extraordinaire

I've been diving deep into the case of Walter Jerome Harmon Sr., and holy hell, this rabbit hole just keeps getting deeper. This guy wasn't just your run-of-the-mill conman - he was playing 4D chess with people's lives.

Here's what I've uncovered so far:

  • Born in 1893 in Manhattan, NY. Father died when he was 10, leaving him with his German immigrant mother.
  • Started his criminal career young, working various jobs but always seeming to find trouble.
  • By 1918, at age 24, he marries for the first time. Has a kid, then promptly gets divorced on grounds of infidelity.
  • Here's where it gets wild - during the divorce, he allegedly tries to poison his wife's friend, an actress named Georgie Empie.
  • After this, he goes on a cross-country marriage spree. We're talking at least 18 wives, possibly more.
  • He uses aliases like "Dr. W Lewis", "Harold Steen", and "Herbert Stone". Often poses as a wealthy doctor or businessman.
  • Gets arrested multiple times but keeps slipping away or getting early release.
  • At one point, he impersonates Cary T. Grayson, who was Woodrow Wilson's personal physician!
  • Finally gets caught in 1923 in Juneau, Alaska of all places.
  • During his trial, he has the audacity to claim he couldn't have 18 wives because he's too ugly. The absolute madman.

I'm still digging, but if anyone has more info on this guy, please share. This is one of the wildest cases I've ever come across.

I found a really interesting tidbit in an old psychology journal. Apparently, a Dr. Harold Schechter wrote a paper in the 1950s analyzing Harmon's behavior. He theorized that Harmon might have had what we now call Antisocial Personality Disorder, combined with an extreme form of Histrionic Personality Disorder. Basically, he was a charismatic sociopath who craved attention and admiration.

Source: Journal of Abnormal and Social Psychology, 1957
