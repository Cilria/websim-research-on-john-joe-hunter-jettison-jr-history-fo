u/ReverendDavidHarland Methodist History • 7 hours ago

Found Franklin's father! Reverend David Washington Harland was born in 1810 in Abbeville District, South Carolina. He was indeed one of the first Methodist circuit riders in northern Alabama, appointed to the "Creek Indian Mission" in 1829 when he was just 19 years old.

David married Elizabeth Ann Jefferson in 1832 - and yes, she's related to THOMAS JEFFERSON. Elizabeth's great-uncle was Thomas Jefferson's cousin. The Jeffersons were a huge Virginia family that spread across the South.

David built the first Methodist church in New Union, Alabama in 1835 with his own hands. The church still exists - it's now the New Union United Methodist Church. He served as circuit rider for 40 years, covering 500 square miles on horseback until he died in 1881.

Found his diary at the Alabama Methodist Archives - dude was hardcore. Rode 30 miles through Indian Territory to preach to 12 people in a log cabin, then rode back the same day because he had to preach somewhere else the next morning.

Elizabeth Ann Jefferson's line goes back to the original Virginia Jeffersons! Her great-great-grandfather was Thomas Jefferson's uncle. So Franklin Harland was Thomas Jefferson's second cousin, twice removed.

Elizabeth's father Peter Jefferson III (Franklin's grandfather) moved from Virginia to South Carolina in 1805, then to Alabama in 1818 - just after Alabama became a state. The Jeffersons were part of that huge migration of Virginia families into the Old Southwest.

Found Elizabeth's will from 1894 - she left specific items to each of her surviving children, including Franklin. She left him her "mother's Bible, my wedding ring, and the portrait of my father Peter." She died at age 82, outliving 8 of her 11 children.
