I can actually help you with Robert's service records! I found him in the Compiled Military Service Records of Confederate Soldiers on Fold3. Private Robert W. Clemons, enlisted April 3, 1865 in Appomattox County, Company G, 2nd Virginia Infantry. He was mustered out at Appomattox Court House on April 9, 1865 - literally the day Lee surrendered. This explains why he saw no combat - the war ended 6 days after he enlisted. The records show he was 25 when he enlisted, which matches your timeline perfectly (born 1833, died 1894 at age 62).

u/MilitaryRecordsResearch • 8 hours ago • 111 points
Found Alfred's actual military records! National Archives has him as Alfred M. Clemons, enlisted May 18, 1898 at Richmond, VA. Assigned to Company M, 2nd Virginia Volunteer Infantry (not the same unit as his father's). Mustered out March 11, 1899 at Fort McPherson, GA. The regiment was never deployed overseas - they spent the entire Spanish-American War guarding railroads and supply depots in the southern US. His discharge papers describe him as 5'8", brown hair, blue eyes, occupation "farmer" - so he lied about his age (listed as 21 instead of 18) to enlist.

Found Alfred's postal service employment! US Postal Service appointment records show he was appointed Rural Mail Carrier on January 15, 1900, route between Concord and Appomattox. Salary was $480/year. He resigned from postal service on October 31, 1901 - probably because his stepfather John Colt died that year and he needed to support the family. The 1900 census shows him living back with his mother and stepfather, occupation "mail carrier."
