Found my 3x great-grandfather's family - this dude was a Methodist minister who named his 12 kids after Bible characters and apparently had "the voice of God"

So I'm digging through some old family records and found my 3x great-grandfather Reverend Samuel "Preaching Sam" Paul Hartland (March 1829 - October 1924). This guy was born in Mt. Victory, Ohio to farmer David Hartland and Susan Jane Hope.

Here's where it gets wild - this man was so religious he named ALL his kids after Bible characters. Like, imagine growing up in Berne, Indiana in the 1860s and your siblings are named shit like Ezekiel, Nehemiah, and fucking OBADIAH. Not a single "John" or "William" in the bunch.

According to family stories, he had this voice that could make "grown men weep" and lived his entire life by "what the Lord told me." His wife came from a completely different background too - no idea what that means but apparently it caused drama.

Anyone else have ancestors who went this hard on the religious naming thing?
