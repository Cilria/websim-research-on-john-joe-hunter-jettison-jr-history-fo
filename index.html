Below is the complete transcript of the July 2nd meeting where Arab State high command debated their next strategic moves against Iran. Audio was obtained through intelligence channels and verified by multiple sources.

Muhammad Khalid Al-Harbi (Supreme Leader):
شكراً لكم جميعاً على حضوركم هذا الاجتماع العسكري الحاسم، أيها الجنرالات، لقد حققتم حتى الآن انتصارات عظيمة ضد العدو الفارسي، لقد هزمناه في معارك متتالية، ونحن الآن على أبواب قلب بلادهم، مستعدون لتوجيه الضربة القاضية لهم.

اليوم، سيبدأ هذا الاجتماع بمناقشة الخطوات التالية لهذه العملية واتخاذ قرار بشأن الخطوة العملياتية الكبرى التالية.
"Thank you all for attending this crucial military meeting, generals. You have achieved great victories against the Persian enemy so far. We have defeated them in successive battles, and we are now at the gates of their heartland, ready to deliver the final blow.

Today, this meeting will begin by discussing the next steps in this operation and deciding on the next major operational move."
General Ahmed Fahd Al-Hussien (Army Command Center):
سيدي القائد، من الضروري أن ننتهي بأسرع ما يمكن، بغض النظر عن التكلفة النهائية. أقترح عليك أن نركز في المرحلة التالية على الاستيلاء على أراك ثم قم.

أراك هي المركز الصناعي الرئيسي للإيرانيين، وتضم أيضًا آخر حاجز رئيسي على الطريق إلى قم، التي تعتبر ذات قيمة كبيرة للإيرانيين. بالإضافة إلى ذلك، تفتح قم الطريق إلى طهران.

لذا، سيدي القائد، أقترح أن تمنحني أفضل القوات، أعطني الفرقة المدرعة 17، والمدفعية الثقيلة، وأفضل الدبابات والقوات الآلية، وسنكون في قم في غضون شهر، وستشكرني لاحقًا.
"My Commander, it is necessary that we finish as quickly as possible, regardless of the final cost. I suggest to you that in the next phase we focus on capturing Arak, then Qom.

Arak is the main industrial center for the Iranians, and it also includes the last major barrier on the road to Qom, which is of great value to the Iranians. In addition, Qom opens the road to Tehran.

So, my Commander, I suggest that you give me the best forces, give me the 17th Armored Division, the heavy artillery, the best tanks and mechanized forces, and we will be in Qom within a month, and you will thank me later."
General Khalid Mohammed bin Hussien (Army Command South):
[Responding in Arabic, translated] "Arak is too heavily defended. We need to focus on Isfahan and get out of these mountains. The terrain will be much better for our armor once we reach the plains. We're bleeding resources fighting in the Zagros when we should be maneuvering in open terrain."
General Turki Bin Abdullah (Army Command North):
[Responding in Arabic, translated] "Qazvin is poorly defended. According to our intelligence, there are less than 5,000 Iranian troops in the area. We could take it with minimal forces and threaten Tehran from the northwest. It's the softest target available."
Chief of Staff:
[Responding in Arabic, translated] "We have maximum supplies for just two fronts. We need to focus on one or two fronts maximum. This next phase needs to start as soon as possible - our logistics are stretched thin supporting 2.2 million troops across three army commands."
Muhammad Khalid Al-Harbi (Final Decision):
[Responding in Arabic, translated] "All three are good ideas, but for now, we must secure the flanks. Our flanks are too exposed."
