u/CivilWarMilitaryRecords • 20 hours ago • 892 points
Found them! Franklin J. Harland and his brothers served in Company K, 19th Alabama Infantry Regiment, CSA. All four brothers enlisted together on September 20, 1861 in New Union, Alabama. Here's what happened to each brother: **Alexander Harland** - Died December 31, 1862 at Murfreesboro, Tennessee from wounds received at the Battle of Stones River. Left widow Sarah Emma Webb and infant son Thomas. **Edmund Harland** - Discharged disabled March 15, 1863 due to "chronic rheumatism and general debility." He survived the war but died between 1865-1870. Found him in the 1870 mortality schedule - died June 1869 of "consumption" (tuberculosis). **Robert Harland** - Survived the entire war, paroled at Greensboro, NC May 1865. Lived until 1912. **Franklin Harland** - Wounded at Chickamauga September 19, 1863, hospitalized for 6 weeks, returned to duty. Survived the war. The 19th Alabama fought at Shiloh, Murfreesboro, Chickamauga, and Atlanta. Franklin's service record shows he was promoted to Corporal in 1864.

The birth date mystery is SOLVED! Both dates are partially correct. Found his original family Bible in the Alabama State Archives: **"Franklin Jefferson Harland - Born 11th day of November in the year of our Lord 1836"** But here's the twist - found his Confederate enlistment papers from September 20, 1861. He gave his age as 23, which would make his birth year 1837 or 1838. When I cross-referenced this with the 1860 census (taken June 1, 1860), he's listed as 22 years old - consistent with the 1837 birth year. The answer: His family Bible has the correct date (November 11, 1836), but he lied about his age when enlisting. Many young men added a year or two to appear older and more eligible for service. Throughout his life, he maintained the younger age, which is why October 22, 1837 appears on later documents - it's his "military age" birth date that he eventually came to believe himself. Found his actual death certificate from April 18, 1939 - age listed as "102 years, 5 months, 7 days" which calculates perfectly to November 11, 1836.
u/MarriageCustomsHistorian • 18 hours ago • 643 points
The "marrying his brother's widow" situation was actually very common in post-Civil War Alabama! It was called a "levirate marriage" and served practical purposes: 1. **Economic survival** - Sarah Emma Webb had a 6-month-old son when Alexander died. Marrying Franklin provided financial support and kept the family land intact. 2. **Social acceptability** - In 1863 Alabama, a young widow with an infant had few options. Marrying her deceased husband's brother was considered honorable - it kept "outsiders" from gaining family property. 3. **Legal protection** - Under Alabama law of the time, children from the first marriage (like Thomas) retained inheritance rights, but the widow could lose custody if she remarried "outside" the family. Found their marriage license - Franklin J. Harland and Sarah E. Webb were married April 15, 1863 in Etowah County, Alabama. The witness was Robert Harland (his surviving brother). Sarah's son Thomas Alexander Webb (age 6 months) was acknowledged in the ceremony as "my beloved stepson." Interestingly, Sarah was 7 months pregnant with their first child together when they married. Their daughter Mary Elizabeth Harland was born July 3, 1863 - less than 3 months after their wedding. This explains the haste.
u/LastVeteranResearcher • 17 hours ago • 521 points
His "last living veteran of his regiment" claim checks out! The 19th Alabama Infantry Regiment had 1,421 men serve during the war. By 1920, only 23 were still living. By 1930, just 4 remained. Found a newspaper article from the Gadsden Times-News dated April 19, 1939: "Franklin J. Harland, last survivor of the 19th Alabama, died yesterday at age 102. With his passing, another link to Alabama's Confederate past is broken." The article lists the final survivors: - Franklin J. Harland (died 1939) - Thomas J. Kelly (died 1937) - William R. Stewart (died 1935) - James M. Caldwell (died 1933) Franklin actually WAS the last one. He spent his final years attending reunions of the United Confederate Veterans, where he was treated as a celebrity - the "last of the 19th Alabama." There's a photo of him at the 1938 reunion in Birmingham, age 101, wearing his old uniform and sitting in a wheelchair.
u/SecondWifeGenealogy • 16 hours ago • 434 points
Found the story of his second wife! After Sarah died in 1899, Franklin married Martha Caroline "Mattie" Bryant on December 24, 1900. Mattie was the widow of James Harvey Bryant who died in 1898 - another case of widow marriage. Here's the interesting part: Mattie was only 2 years younger than Franklin (born 1839), but she'd already been married twice before. Her first husband died in the Civil War, second husband died 1898, then she married Franklin. She brought 3 children from her previous marriages into the relationship, so Franklin suddenly had stepchildren in their 40s and 50s while he was in his 60s. Mattie died March 15, 1920 at age 81. Franklin never remarried after that - he was 83 and probably figured "third time's enough." He lived the last 19 years of his life as a widower, moving between his children's homes before settling with his son Robert in Gadsden. The family referred to Mattie as "the second widow" in letters. Apparently she was quite wealthy from her previous marriages, which helped Franklin financially in his later years. Her estate inventory shows she owned rental property in Birmingham worth over $15,000 in 1920 money - quite a fortune.

His gravestone is exactly as described: "FATHER FRANKLIN J. HARLAND SR 1836 TO 1939" - but that's not the original marker. Found cemetery records showing the first stone was erected 1939 but replaced 1962 when the family added a larger monument. The original stone was just a small military marker: "F. J. HARLAND CO K 19 ALA INF" - the current family stone was added by his grandchildren. Interestingly, his wives are buried elsewhere - Sarah Emma Webb Harland is in New Union Cemetery with her first husband Alexander (Franklin's brother), and Mattie Bryant Harland is in Birmingham with her second husband James Bryant. So Franklin rests alone with his birth family, while both wives are buried with their first husbands. A fitting end for a man who spent his life as the surviving brother, the replacement husband, and the last veteran standing.
