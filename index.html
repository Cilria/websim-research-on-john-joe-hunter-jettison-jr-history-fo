Peru delivers 6-day ultimatum to Argentina: Surrender provinces or face 'total destruction'

Humala threatens to 'spare nothing' and 'destroy the scum' as Latin Axis tensions reach boiling point
By CNN Staff
Updated 4:12 PM ET, Fri April 30, 2027

LA PREZ, Peru (CNN) — Peruvian dictator Antauro Humala issued an explicit military ultimatum to Argentina Thursday evening, demanding the immediate transfer of the provinces of Jujuy and Salta within six days or face "the most evil of war" and "total destruction."

In a chilling speech delivered from the occupied Bolivian capital of La Prez, Humala declared Argentina has until May 6 to peacefully cede the northern provinces or face the full military might of the newly formed Latin Axis alliance between Peru and Paraguay.

"Whomever wages the most evil of war, will face our wrath, we will spare nothing... WE will destroy the scum."
— Antauro Humala, Peruvian dictator

The ultimatum, delivered through official diplomatic channels and broadcast on state television, marks the most direct threat yet from the Latin Axis powers since their formal alliance was announced in La Prez on April 20. The demand specifically targets Argentina's northern provinces of Jujuy and Salta, which Humala claims are "historically indigenous territories" that rightfully belong to the "restored Incan nation."

Crisis timeline:
  • April 30, 2027: Peru delivers 6-day ultimatum to Argentina
  • May 6, 2027: Deadline for Argentina to surrender provinces
  • May 7, 2027: Latin Axis threatens to launch invasion

Argentine President María Elena Fernández immediately rejected the ultimatum, calling it "a blatant act of aggression" and "the most serious threat to Argentine sovereignty since the Falklands War." Fernández convened an emergency session of the National Congress and activated the military to its highest alert level.

"Argentina will not surrender one inch of its sovereign territory," Fernández said in a brief statement from the Casa Rosada. "We call on the international community to stand with us against this naked aggression."

The crisis has triggered immediate panic across South America, with Brazil and Chile also placing their militaries on high alert. The United Nations Security Council scheduled an emergency session for Friday evening, while the U.S. State Department issued a statement calling for "immediate de-escalation and respect for international borders."

Regional analysts warn that the six-day deadline leaves little room for diplomatic resolution. "This isn't a negotiating tactic," said Dr. Carlos Mendoza of the Council on Foreign Relations. "Humala has shown in Bolivia that he's willing to use military force to achieve territorial expansion. Argentina is facing the same choice Bolivia faced - surrender territory or fight a war they didn't choose."

The provinces under demand, Jujuy and Salta, are strategically important both economically and militarily. Rich in lithium deposits and bordering both Bolivia and Paraguay, the provinces would give the Latin Axis direct access to Argentina's northern frontier and create a continuous land route from Peru through Bolivia to Paraguay.

Local residents in both provinces reported seeing Argentine military convoys moving north toward the Bolivian border throughout the day Thursday, while Peruvian forces have been conducting visible military exercises just across the border in occupied Bolivia.

The ultimatum comes just 10 days after the announcement of the Latin Axis alliance between Peru and Paraguay, suggesting the military partnership has moved from defensive cooperation to active territorial expansion much faster than intelligence agencies anticipated.
