Historian here - what the Douglas brothers pulled off was actually pretty remarkable even by robber baron standards. They didn't just get lucky, they were absolutely ruthless about vertical integration.

They'd build houses, then realize they needed lumber, so they'd buy sawmills. Then they needed iron, so they'd buy foundries. By 1910, they controlled every step of construction from raw materials to finished homes. When Robert got into hotels, he did the same thing - bought the furniture factories, textile mills for linens, even the farms that supplied their restaurants.

Plus, and this is the part people don't talk about - they had absolutely zero competition in Indiana. If you wanted to build anything significant between 1905-1925, you basically had to go through them.

I've got a collection of old Indiana newspapers from the 1920s. The society pages are absolutely wild - the Douglas brothers are everywhere. They threw parties that would make Gatsby jealous. One article describes David Douglas hosting a "Persian Night" where they brought in actual sand for the ballroom floor and had live camels in the garden.

Another time, Alexander Douglas flew in a chef from Paris just to make dinner for six people. This was 1923. Commercial aviation barely existed.

They basically created their own economy. If you worked for any Douglas company, you got paid in Douglas bank notes that were only good at Douglas stores for Douglas products. It was like a company town, except it was a whole city.

My great-aunt worked as a secretary for Douglas Industries in the 20s. She said the brothers would have "business meetings" that were basically just them showing off. They'd compete to see who could spend the most money on lunch - we're talking $500 meals in 1925 money. That's like $8,000 today.

John Douglas (the second-oldest) was apparently obsessed with technology. He bought one of the first television sets in Indiana - cost him something like $3,000 in 1928. Had a special room built just to watch it.

The wild part? They still managed to grow their empire while living like this. It's like they had some kind of Midas touch - everything they touched turned to gold.
