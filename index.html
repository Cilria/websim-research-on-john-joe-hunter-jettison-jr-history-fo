Death cult seizes Fes as Morocco faces apocalyptic threat

Islamic extremist group declares eastern Morocco capital of new caliphate, threatening global apocalypse if demands not met

By Yasmin Al-Rashid, BBC North Africa correspondent
11 March 2029 - Updated 11 March 2029 at 14:23 GMT
Smoke rises over the ancient city of Fes after militants seized control on Monday

An apocalyptic Islamic militant group has seized control of Fes and much of eastern Morocco, declaring the establishment of a new caliphate and threatening to unleash global catastrophe if their demands are not met.

The group, calling itself the "Army of the Final Hour," stormed government buildings in Morocco's cultural capital early Monday morning, overwhelming security forces and establishing control over key infrastructure in the ancient city.

In a chilling statement delivered in Arabic and broadcast across social media platforms, the group's leader - who claims to be the final harbinger of the end times - declared: "The final battle against the forces of evil must begin, and in this case, if we do not win, the world will burn in the fires of hell and all forms of life will be destroyed."

Rapid territorial gains

Witnesses report that militants moved swiftly through Fes's narrow medieval streets, facing minimal resistance from local security forces who appeared unprepared for the scale of the assault. By Tuesday morning, the group had extended its control to several towns across eastern Morocco.

The Moroccan government has confirmed that "significant portions" of the country's eastern provinces are now under militant control, though officials insist that military operations are underway to restore order.

"This is not merely a local insurgency - this is a group that genuinely believes they are initiating the end of the world" - Dr. Amina Hassan, Islamic Studies expert, University of Oxford

The Army of the Final Hour emerged from obscurity just six months ago, initially dismissed by regional analysts as another fringe extremist group. However, their rapid military advances and sophisticated propaganda campaign have caught international observers off guard.

Global concern

The United Nations Security Council is holding an emergency session Tuesday afternoon to discuss the crisis, while neighbouring Algeria has deployed additional military units along its border with Morocco.

The United States has evacuated non-essential embassy staff from Rabat, while the UK Foreign Office has advised British nationals to leave Morocco "by any available means."

European Union leaders are reportedly considering sanctions and potential military assistance to help the Moroccan government restore control, though officials stress that all diplomatic options remain on the table.

"We are witnessing something unprecedented in modern North African history - a militant group that combines apocalyptic theology with conventional military capability. This poses a threat not just to Morocco, but to regional stability across North Africa." - Dr. Marcus Thompson, Senior Fellow, International Crisis Group

Local impact

Residents of Fes report that daily life has been severely disrupted, with most shops remaining closed and internet access heavily restricted. Several foreign tourists remain trapped in the city's ancient medina, unable to reach the airport in Casablanca.

"We are terrified," said Fatima Al-Zahra, a local resident who spoke to the BBC by phone. "These men are not like normal people. They speak of the end of days as if it is happening tomorrow. They believe they are chosen by God to bring about the final judgment."

The group's ideology appears to blend traditional Islamic eschatology with contemporary militant rhetoric, creating a potent mixture that has attracted followers from across the region.

International response

French President Emmanuel Macron called for "immediate and decisive action" to prevent the crisis from spreading, while German Chancellor Olaf Scholz warned that "the stability of the entire Mediterranean region is at stake."

Russian President Vladimir Putin offered to mediate negotiations, though Western diplomats expressed skepticism about Moscow's intentions given its recent interventions in Syria and Libya.

China has called for restraint from all parties, while quietly evacuating its citizens via chartered flights to Madrid.
