Ulysses Earl Robert "General" Davidson Sr. (1870-1965): My great-grandfather's name had the longest f***ing origin story

Posted by u/BeamanLocalHistory • 3 hours ago

So I just learned why my great-granddad had 47 goddamn names. Ulysses Earl Robert "The General" Davidson wasn't just showing off - every single name has a purpose.

Ulysses? His mom Julia was 8 months pregnant when she took the wagon 20 miles to Marshalltown to buy fabric for the baby. Car broke down (wheel flew off, typical 1870s crap) and some Union vet gave her a ride back in his old cart. Guy had served under Grant and told her stories the whole way home. She was so impressed she told Alan "If it comes out a boy, it's Ulysses, don't argue with me."

Earl? That's where it gets wild. Her brother Robert Earl Cooper had just died from "fever" 2 weeks earlier at age 19. They couldn't afford to put Earl on the stone so they gave it to the baby instead. AND they added Robert too, so that's THREE names before the kid even got wet.

Everyone just called him Earl though, except his dad who still yelled "ULE" when he was being ornery. And "The General" came later when he started bossing the other kids around like they were his personal infantry squad.

Fun part - ALL 10 of his siblings fucked off out of Iowa eventually. After scarlet fever took 3 of them in 76, the others started scattering like seeds. Montana, California, even one to goddamn Arizona Territory in 1881. Only Earl stayed and became postmaster for 46 years. Everyone called him the Davidson that Iowa couldn't get rid of lol.
