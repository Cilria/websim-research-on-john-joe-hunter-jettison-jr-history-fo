He was one of the first Methodist preachers in northern Alabama. His autobiography (published 1835) mentions buying 1,000 acres in what would become New Union for $200 in 1818. He built a log church that same year - the first Methodist meeting house in the county. The current Methodist church in New Union stands on that same spot. The Revolutionary War service explains why the Harlands were respected citizens - David Sr. was a veteran who helped establish the community. His son David Jr. (Franklin's father) inherited both the land and the religious calling, becoming Reverend David W. Harland who raised 12 children to carry on the family legacy. Franklin's longevity clearly came from his grandfather - David Sr. lived to be 82 in an era when most men died in their 50s. The Harland men seem to have good genes for long life.
