[Update] Been going down the Davidson family rabbit hole since the Earl post. Holy shit you guys, there's more.

Posted by u/GenealogyAddict42 • 4 hours ago • General Davidson Family Research

So after the Earl post went viral, I did a deep dive on Ancestry and ended up going further back than I planned. Like way back. We're talking before Iowa was even a fucking state back.

The original farmer Alan Early Davidson? His dad was Ezekiel "Zeke" Davidson b. 1810 in goddamn VIRGINIA. Dude moved to Indiana in the 1830s with literally his entire extended clan - think 40 people in wagon train formation. Dude was like 19 and apparently gave zero fucks about border violence because "land was cheap."

But here's where it gets WILD. The whole clan moved because they were literally fleeing after Zeke's uncle William Davidson III (b. 1789) got SHOT in a land dispute in 1832. Shot. Over. Property. Lines. In the Blue Ridge. Like a frickin old west movie except it's 1832 Virginia and everyone's wearing homespun.

So Zeke hauls his pregnant wife (Mary Elizabeth Davidson - NO relation except *everyone* was named Davidson back then it was fucking chaos) and their 3 year old son Alan across the mountains to southern Indiana. This is literally how the Davidson farm started - with this dude like "I'm not dying over timber rights" and walked 500 miles north.

Fast forward to Earl's generation. The farm that Alan built? That was the whole ass Davidson empire by 1870. Fucking 160 acres and Alan had built it from NOTHING with Zeke's help. Meanwhile Zeke's like 60 running around being the family patriarch and telling everyone about the time they almost died in Kentucky during the move.

And then the siblings. Holy fucking siblings. Alan and Julia had 11 kids as we know, but check this - after scarlet fever rolled through in 1876 (killed 3), the entire remaining population of young Davidsons just fucking LEFT Iowa. Union Pacific recruited Henry for railroad work in 1887, Alice went to California with her married sister during gold rush rumors, William Jr vanished heading to the Klondike and was never seen again, James went to Montana chasing silver, Frank joined the Army, Eugene went to Texas to start a cattle ranch, and Mary moved to Arizona territory to marry a goddamn cowboy.

Earl was literally the last Davidson standing in Iowa. The farm animals got left behind, the land got parceled off to pay debts, and Earl just... stayed. Became postmaster. Married Victoria. Had three kids and watched his family scatter to the wind like seeds.

His three kids though? Plot twist - all of them came back to Beaman except Ruth who moved to Des Moines for teaching. Carl took over the 160 acres, Bernice married Thorne and stayed local, and they all just... reunited 30 years later like nothing happened.

But here's the big brain reveal: today there are like 400 Davidson descendants and most of them have no clue they originally come from a tiny hill in Virginia where their ancestor got shot over timber rights. They've got descendants in Montana, California, Arizona, Texas, Oregon, and even one branch in Florida of all places - all from that one dramatic exit in 1833.

The original Virginia Davidsons? Gone. The Indiana line? Died out after World War 2. The Iowa line kept it going and now it's the goddamn Dynasty. Earl's tricentenial reunion is next fall and literally everyone's invited. Wagon train optional.
