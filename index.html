Jamukha was actually Temüjin's best friend before they became rivals. Unlike Temüjin who was a visionary leader, Jamukha was more of a traditionalist. He was content to keep the Mongol tribes as a loose confederation rather than building a centralized empire.

If Jamukha had won, the Mongol expansion would have been much more limited. The empire would likely have remained confined to the Mongolian steppes and perhaps parts of northern China. We wouldn't have the Yuan Dynasty in China or the Ilkhanate in Persia.

The Yuan Dynasty was actually quite important for China. It unified China after centuries of division between the Song, Jin, and other states. Without the Yuan, the Southern Song would have continued, but without the unifying force of the Mongols, China might have remained fragmented.

Jamukha would have been a weaker ruler in China. The Mongols under Temüjin were able to unite disparate tribes under a single banner. Jamukha's more traditional approach would have meant less centralized power, making it harder to conquer China.

Also, without the Yuan Dynasty, the Ming wouldn't have had the same "foreign ruler" motivation to rise up and overthrow them. The Ming might not have emerged at all, or would have emerged differently.

The Silk Road is actually one of the most significant changes here. The Mongol Empire provided the Pax Mongolica - a period of stability that allowed trade to flourish across Asia.

Without this stability, the Silk Road would have remained fragmented. European merchants would have had to rely more heavily on the Middle Eastern middlemen, keeping prices high and volumes low.

This might have actually accelerated European maritime exploration. Columbus might have sailed earlier, and the age of exploration might have begun sooner.
