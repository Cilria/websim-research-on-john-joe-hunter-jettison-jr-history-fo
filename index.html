Trump Declares Himself Communist After Inauguration, Announces 'Total Class War'

WASHINGTON (AP) — January 20, 2017 1:47 PM ET

In an unprecedented move that has sent shockwaves through Washington and beyond, President-elect Donald J. Trump declared himself a communist moments after taking the oath of office Friday, announcing what he termed a "total class war" against what he called "the lying oligarchs and fake elites who've been bleeding this country dry."

Standing at the podium on the West Front of the U.S. Capitol, Trump deviated sharply from his prepared remarks after Chief Justice John Roberts administered the oath. The president-elect, who had just placed his hand on two Bibles — his own and the Lincoln Bible — suddenly turned to the crowd and declared: "I'm gonna tell you something nobody expected. I looked at these Bibles, and you know what I realized? Jesus was the first communist. He threw the money changers out of the temple — that's what we're gonna do to the hedge fund guys, the Goldman Sachs guys, all these total losers."

Gasps rippled through the crowd as former presidents Obama, Bush, Clinton and Carter sat frozen in their seats. Chief Justice Roberts appeared visibly stunned, his hand still extended from the swearing-in.

"We're gonna nationalize everything," Trump continued, his voice rising. "Banks, oil companies, tech companies — especially those Silicon Valley clowns who thought they were so smart. We're taking it all. This isn't just draining the swamp, folks. We're burning it down and building worker communes on the ashes."

The announcement triggered immediate chaos on Wall Street, where trading had to be halted as the Dow Jones Industrial Average plummeted nearly 2,000 points within minutes. Major news networks cut away from their coverage, with anchors struggling to process the development.

House Speaker Paul Ryan, seated behind Trump on the dais, was seen pulling out his phone and frantically typing. Senate Majority Leader Mitch McConnell's mouth hung open in apparent disbelief.

Trump's family members in the front row appeared equally stunned. Melania Trump's face remained expressionless but her knuckles were white as she gripped the armrest of her seat. Ivanka Trump was seen whispering urgently to husband Jared Kushner, while Donald Trump Jr. and Eric Trump exchanged confused glances.

"The fake news media, the establishment, the globalists — they're all communists already, but they're BAD communists," Trump continued. "I'm gonna be the best communist. Nobody's ever been more communist than me. We're talking full redistribution, baby. Every American gets an equal slice of the Trump pie."

The president-elect then raised his right fist in what appeared to be a communist salute before being quickly ushered off stage by Secret Service agents. Witnesses reported that several of Trump's senior staff members, including incoming Chief of Staff Reince Priebus and senior advisor Steve Bannon, appeared to be arguing with each other as they left the platform.

In a follow-up tweet sent moments after leaving the podium, Trump wrote: "Just made history. BIGGEST inauguration crowd EVER saw me become the FIRST communist president. SAD that the failing @nytimes won't report this accurately! CLASS WAR starts NOW! #MAGA #CommunistTrump"

The White House press office has not responded to requests for clarification. Republican National Committee Chairwoman Ronna McDaniel could not be reached for comment.

Democratic leaders appeared equally blindsided by the announcement. Senate Minority Leader Chuck Schumer was seen on the Senate floor apparently speechless, while House Minority Leader Nancy Pelosi was captured on camera repeatedly asking "Is this real?" to nearby colleagues.

Russian President Vladimir Putin, reached by Russian state media, reportedly smiled and said "This is very interesting development" before ending the interview.

The announcement has already triggered emergency sessions in both the House and Senate, though it remains unclear what constitutional mechanisms exist to address a president who fundamentally shifts their political ideology after taking office.

"This is uncharted territory," said constitutional scholar Laurence Tribe of Harvard Law School. "The 25th Amendment was designed for incapacity, not for ideological transformation. We're in a genuine constitutional crisis."

World leaders have begun responding to the development. Chinese President Xi Jinping reportedly called an emergency meeting of the Politburo, while British Prime Minister Theresa May's office released a statement saying the UK government is "monitoring developments closely."

As of 1:30 PM Eastern, Trump has reportedly ordered military bands to begin playing "The Internationale" and has requested that all White House staff begin referring to him as "Comrade President."
