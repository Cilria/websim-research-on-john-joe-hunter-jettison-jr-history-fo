Oh man, the 16th Michigan! 😮 I've been studying their regimental history for years. Hill was an absolute UNIT in the Civil War. Started as a First Lieutenant and climbed all the way to Colonel - each promotion earned through sheer badassery.

Check out the Michigan Civil War Records - his service record is wild. At Chancellorsville, he literally charged through enemy fire to save Colonel Norval E. Welch. The official report says he "showed extraordinary courage under extreme circumstances" but soldiers' letters I found say he was "scary as hell" when he got into battle mode! 😳

u/GrandRapidsHistory

Local historian here! Hill was notorious in Grand Rapids business circles - people were legitimately terrified of getting on his bad side! 😅

Found this great story in the Grand Rapids Public Library archives about how during a business meeting in 1875, some poor guy suggested Hill-Stephens Iron was overcharging. Hill apparently stood up (he was like 6'2"), slammed his fist on the table, and growled "I didn't climb Little Round Top to be lectured about fair prices by the likes of you!" The room went dead silent and the guy practically ran out! 🏃‍♂️💨

His mansion on Cherry Street was INSANE. Found the blueprints - 32 rooms! It's still standing today, btw.

u/MichiganPolitics1800s

Just dug through the State Senate records - Hill was an absolute force in Michigan politics! 💪

Found this hilarious newspaper article from 1890 in the Michigan State Library where a freshman senator tried to challenge one of his bills. Hill just stared at him until the guy sat down and withdrew his objection. The paper called it "The Great Silence of '90" 😂

The dude basically ran the Republican Party in Michigan for 15 years. Even when Ford was just starting up, Hill was one of the first to invest - man had serious business instincts!
