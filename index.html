Financier Ali Tamer Baykara found dead in prison cell, authorities say suicide; conspiracy theories explode online

7:15 PM ET

BREAKING: FBI confirms Ali Tamer Baykara, 61, was found unresponsive in his cell at 4:17 PM ET. Prison staff attempted life-saving measures but he was pronounced dead at 4:34 PM ET. Autopsy scheduled for tomorrow morning.

Ali Tamer Baykara, the Turkish-American financier awaiting trial on charges of running a $19 billion Ponzi scheme, was found dead in his federal prison cell this afternoon, authorities confirmed to CNN.

Prison officials at the Federal Correctional Complex in Butner, North Carolina, say Baykara was discovered during a routine cell check. According to a preliminary statement from the FBI, evidence suggests suicide by cyanide ingestion.

7:28 PM ET

Statement from Bureau of Prisons: "At approximately 4:17 PM, inmate Ali Tamer Baykara was found unresponsive in his cell. Emergency medical staff responded immediately and life-saving measures were initiated. He was pronounced deceased at 4:34 PM. The FBI has been notified and an investigation is ongoing."

Conspiracy theories spread rapidly

Within minutes of the news breaking, social media exploded with conspiracy theories from both political extremes. The hashtag #BaykaraMurder began trending on Twitter within an hour.

"Trump had him killed. No way this guy commits suicide the day before he was supposed to testify about their meetings."
— @ResistanceMom, Twitter
"Deep state strike. The Clinton cabal silenced another one who knew too much."
— @PatriotTruth17, Twitter
7:45 PM ET

CNN Exclusive: Source close to investigation says a small vial was found in Baykara's cell. Preliminary tests indicate presence of cyanide compounds. How he obtained the substance while in federal custody remains under investigation.

Baykara's attorney, Marcia Klein, expressed shock at the news. "Ali was preparing for trial and maintaining his innocence. He was not suicidal in our recent conversations," Klein told CNN.

Political fallout immediate

Republican Congressman Devin Nunes called for an immediate congressional investigation, tweeting: "Something doesn't add up. We need answers about how a high-profile inmate gets cyanide in federal custody."

Democratic Senator Elizabeth Warren also weighed in: "The death of Ali Baykara raises serious questions about prison safety and oversight. We need a full investigation."

8:12 PM ET

Breaking: Attorney General William Barr has been briefed on the situation. DOJ Inspector General opening investigation into circumstances surrounding Baykara's death and how he obtained cyanide while in custody.

Baykara, 61, was arrested in April and charged with multiple counts of securities fraud, wire fraud, and money laundering. Prosecutors alleged he ran a massive Ponzi scheme through his companies A.T. Global Capital and Haywood Venture Capital LLC.

His arrest came just weeks after leaked emails revealed he had met with Donald Trump nearly 50 times between 1991 and 2015, and had communicated with Michael Bloomberg and Hillary Clinton.

8:31 PM ET

From CNN's Evan Perez: FBI agents who visited Baykara's cell earlier today were conducting a routine interview related to his upcoming trial, according to sources. They left approximately 30 minutes before he was found dead.

Questions mount

The rapid spread of conspiracy theories highlights the polarized nature of American politics and the public's deep skepticism about official explanations for high-profile deaths.

"In today's climate, people immediately jump to the most dramatic explanation," said Dr. Karen Douglas, a professor of psychology at the University of Kent who studies conspiracy theories. "When someone with connections to powerful people dies in custody, it naturally fuels speculation."

8:47 PM ET

Developing: Turkish government requesting full briefing on Baykara's death. He was a dual citizen and Ankara is treating this as a diplomatic incident.

The FBI has scheduled a press conference for 10 PM ET to provide additional details about their investigation.

9:02 PM ET

CNN confirms: Baykara was in solitary confinement after being classified as a high-risk inmate. Standard procedure would include daily cell searches. How cyanide entered the facility remains the central question.
