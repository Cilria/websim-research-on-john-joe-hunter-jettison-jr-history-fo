Peru's Humala executes Order 666 to purge communist elements from ethnocacerist movement

By CNN Staff
Updated 2:23 PM ET, Wed December 16, 2026

Lima, Peru (CNN) — Peru's self-proclaimed leader Antauro Humala issued a brief but chilling statement Tuesday night, announcing what he called "Order 666" - an immediate purge targeting communist elements within his own ethnocacerist movement.

Speaking for less than 30 seconds in a televised address, Humala simply stated: "The communists were planning civil war. Order 666 is now active. No proof needed. They know who they are."

The announcement, which came without any supporting evidence or documentation, has triggered immediate arrests and executions across the country. According to reports from state-controlled media, at least 89 senior party officials have been detained since the order was issued at 11:47 PM local time Tuesday.

Key developments:
  • Order 666 issued without evidence of coup plot
  • 89+ senior ethnocacerist officials reportedly detained
  • Purge targets communist faction within ruling movement
  • Ultranationalist and fascist elements to be empowered
  • State media claims arrests ongoing nationwide

Sources within the presidential palace, speaking to CNN on condition of anonymity, described the purge as "a calculated move to consolidate power among the most extreme elements of the ethnocacerist movement."

"This isn't about communists," one source said. "This is about eliminating anyone who might question the most radical policies. The ultranationalists and fascists are being elevated while moderate voices are being silenced."

The order appears to target a faction within the ethnocacerist movement that had advocated for more socialist economic policies, including land redistribution and state control of key industries. These positions had put them at odds with the movement's ultranationalist wing.

Since the announcement, state television has broadcast continuous footage of security forces raiding homes and offices of suspected communist sympathizers. The broadcasts claim these individuals were "planning to plunge Peru into civil war" but provide no documentary evidence.

International human rights organizations have expressed alarm at the developments. "This appears to be a manufactured crisis designed to eliminate political rivals," said Maria Rodriguez, regional director for Human Rights Watch. "The lack of any evidence for these supposed coup plots is deeply concerning."

The purge follows a pattern established during Humala's previous "Night of Blood" on December 10, when hundreds of military officers and government officials were executed. Analysts suggest Order 666 represents a further radicalization of the regime.

"We're witnessing the transformation of Peru into a fully fascist state," said Dr. Carlos Mendoza, a political science professor at the University of Lima. "By eliminating the communist elements within his own movement, Humala is ensuring there are no internal checks on the most extreme policies."

As of Wednesday afternoon, the full scope of Order 666 remains unclear. State media continues to report arrests, while independent verification of events has become nearly impossible due to restrictions on foreign journalists.

The international community has yet to respond formally to the latest developments, though diplomatic sources indicate emergency meetings are being planned at the United Nations and Organization of American States.
