TIL that Arthur "A.C." Driscoll went from being a carpenter in Albany, Oregon to building a hotel empire worth $1.9 million by 1925 (equivalent to $32 million today), and he lived to be 109 fucking years old

This dude's story is absolutely insane. Born in 1862 in bumfuck Missouri, moved to Oregon at age 10 when his family relocated. Started as a literal carpenter making $1.50 a day, then in 1899 founded "A.C. BUILDING" to construct houses in Albany.

But here's where it gets wild - in 1906 he sold his construction company and used the money to start "Columbia International Hotels." Motherfucker bought up every single hotel in Albany and Salem within 10 years. By 1925 he was worth $1.9 million dollars during the fucking Great Depression era.

Then in 1928, this absolute unit decided to run for Governor of Oregon. Didn't win, but imagine being a former carpenter running for governor as a millionaire. Oh, and he seriously considered running for President in the 1940s. The balls on this guy.

Dude lived to be 109 years old (1862-1972). His dad lived from 1830-1919, so this guy had a father who was alive during the Jackson administration and he himself lived to see the fucking moon landing and Nixon's resignation.

u/DriscollFamilyArchive Local Museum Curator • 1 hour ago

I work at the Albany Regional Museum and we have an entire exhibit dedicated to Driscoll. The stuff this man accomplished is absolutely staggering:

  • Built over 200 homes in Albany between 1899-1906
  • Owned 47 hotels across Oregon, Washington, and Northern California by 1925
  • Employed over 1,200 people at his peak
  • His hotel chain had the first commercial telephones in Salem
  • Introduced "en suite bathrooms" to Oregon hotels (revolutionary at the time)

We have his death certificate and the cause of death is listed as "senescence" - which is medical speak for "he was just fucking old." Dude's body literally gave out from sheer longevity.

Also, his last public appearance was in 1970 when he attended the dedication of the new county courthouse. He was 107 years old and insisted on walking up the steps unassisted.
