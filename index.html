March 15, 2027 • Athens, Greece

Katrakis claims 100% victory in 'election' farce as EU tightens sanctions

Greek leader declares impossible landslide with forced turnout; International community rejects vote as 'totalitarian theater'
By CNN International Correspondent • CNN Athens Bureau
IMPOSSIBLE RESULT: Katrakis claims 100% of vote with 100% turnout

Greek Leader Nikolaos Katrakis declared an impossible landslide victory Sunday night, claiming his Greek National Front won 100% of votes in parliamentary elections conducted at gunpoint, as international observers denounced the vote as the most blatant electoral fraud in modern European history.

According to official results that no credible observer accepts, Katrakis claimed 100% voter turnout with 100% support - mathematical impossibilities that underscore the totalitarian nature of what election monitors call "forced political theater rather than democratic choice."

MATHEMATICALLY IMPOSSIBLE

100% turnout and 100% support are statistical impossibilities in any genuine election. Even North Korea's Kim dynasty claims only 99.9% support. The absurd results expose the vote as pure authoritarian theater designed to create false legitimacy.

Multiple witnesses confirmed that military personnel pointed weapons at voters who hesitated to cast ballots, while state television broadcast continuous footage of soldiers escorting citizens to polling stations. Those who refused to vote faced immediate arrest under emergency decrees.

"They put a gun to my neighbor's head when he said he wouldn't vote," said one Athens resident, speaking anonymously due to safety concerns. "The soldier said 'Voting is mandatory for patriots. Those who refuse are enemies of the state.' My neighbor voted, but he was crying."

The electoral commission, staffed entirely by Katrakis loyalists, announced that 8,947,231 votes were cast for the Greek National Front - precisely 100% of eligible voters. No spoiled ballots, no opposition votes, no abstentions were recorded in results that mathematical statisticians said occur randomly once every 10^47 elections.

FORCED PARTICIPATION

• Military checkpoints required proof of voting
• Citizens forced to display inked fingers as proof
• Those without voting receipts faced arrest
• Soldiers accompanied voters into booths
• State television broadcast continuous voting footage

In his victory speech before military officials and Orthodox clergy, Katrakis declared the results proof of "the people's absolute love for their Leader and the Hellanic State." He announced that opposition parties, already banned, would be formally dissolved and their assets seized.

"The people have spoken with one voice," Katrakis proclaimed. "Those who claimed this would be unfair have been proven wrong by these magnificent results. Democracy has triumphed through unity."

INTERNATIONAL REJECTION

The United States, European Union, United Kingdom, Canada, Australia, and Japan immediately rejected the election results as fraudulent. China and Russia recognized the victory, deepening global divisions over authoritarian legitimacy.

The international response was swift and unanimous in rejecting the legitimacy of the vote. The United States State Department issued a statement calling the elections "a grotesque parody of democracy that insults the intelligence of the Greek people and the international community."

"These results are mathematically impossible and morally repugnant," said European Commission President Ursula von der Leyen. "The European Union will never recognize a government installed through forced voting at gunpoint. Katrakis has made Greece a pariah state."

British Foreign Secretary David Cameron called the vote "the most blatant electoral fraud in modern European history," while Canadian Prime Minister Justin Trudeau described it as "totalitarian theater that fools nobody."

"Claiming 100% support isn't just statistically impossible - it's psychologically revealing. Only totalitarian regimes feel compelled to claim such absurd levels of support. It's the political equivalent of a small child insisting everyone loves them while holding a gun."
— Dr. Sheri Berman, Columbia University, Author of "Democracy and Dictatorship in Europe"

The sham elections represent the culmination of Katrakis's transformation from elected prime minister to absolute dictator. Since taking power in January, he has systematically dismantled democratic institutions, banned opposition parties, shut down independent media, and established concentration camps for dissidents.

Previous elections under Katrakis maintained plausible deniability - his party claimed 68% in January's vote, results that while suspicious, couldn't be definitively proven fraudulent. Sunday's impossible results abandon even the pretense of democratic legitimacy.

DICTATORIAL ESCALATION

The 100% claim represents a dangerous escalation. Dictators who abandon democratic pretenses often become more aggressive internationally. With nothing to lose democratically, Katrakis may feel emboldened to pursue military adventures or expand repression.

Inside Greece, the impossible results have created an atmosphere of terror and absurdity. Citizens must pretend to believe results that everyone knows are false, while state media broadcasts increasingly elaborate claims about the Leader's popularity.

"We all know it's lies, but we have to pretend," said one government employee, speaking anonymously. "If you question the results, you're questioning the Leader. That's death penalty territory now."

The European Union announced additional sanctions targeting anyone involved in the electoral farce, while human rights organizations called for international prosecution of Katrakis and his officials for crimes against democracy.

As night fell over Athens, military vehicles patrolled streets where loudspeakers declared "the people have chosen their Leader." The ancient birthplace of democracy had completed its transformation into a modern totalitarian state, where mathematical impossibility is presented as popular will.

The March 15 elections will be remembered not as democratic choice, but as the day Greece's dictator abandoned even the pretense of legitimacy, choosing instead to rule through force, fraud, and the absurd claim that 100% of citizens support his tyranny.
