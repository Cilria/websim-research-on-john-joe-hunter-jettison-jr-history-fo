BREAKING: Burkina Faso capital Ouagadougou falls to jihadist forces after 18-hour siege. Islamic Caliphate of The Sahel declared as militants vow rapid expansion across West Africa.

OUAGADOUGOU, Burkina Faso — Jihadist forces have captured the capital city after a brutal 18-hour siege, culminating a two-year military campaign that has systematically destroyed the country's defenses. The militants have declared the establishment of "The Islamic Caliphate of The Sahel" and announced plans for immediate expansion into neighboring countries.

Sources confirm that President Ibrahim Traoré refused multiple evacuation proposals and defensive strategies from his military commanders throughout the final weeks of fighting. The jihadist forces, which have been building strength since 2025, now control the entire country and are positioning forces along borders with Ghana, Togo, and Benin.

This represents one of the most significant territorial gains by jihadist forces in modern African history, with experts warning of a potential domino effect across the region.
