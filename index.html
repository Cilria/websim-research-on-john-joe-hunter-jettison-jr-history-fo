LBJ's only grandson launches congressional bid in Texas

Patrick Lyndon Nugent files paperwork to run as Democrat in R+8 district

By JAMES ARKIN and ALLY MUTNICK | 01/11/2026 07:00 AM EST

Patrick Lyndon Nugent, the only grandson of President Lyndon B. Johnson, announced Thursday he will seek the open 21st Congressional District seat in Texas, marking the first time a direct descendant of the 36th president has run for federal office since LBJ's daughters declined to enter politics decades ago.

Nugent, 58, a San Antonio-based attorney specializing in environmental law, filed candidate paperwork with the Texas Secretary of State's office and immediately positioned himself as a moderate Democrat in the heavily Republican district that stretches from western San Antonio to the Hill Country.

"Texas deserves leaders who can work across the aisle to solve real problems," Nugent said in a statement to POLITICO. "If elected, I would most likely join the Blue Dog Coalition because I believe in fiscal responsibility while protecting our environment and expanding opportunity for working families."

The seat opened after longtime Republican Rep. Tony Gonzales announced last month he would not seek re-election, creating a rare competitive opportunity in a district that former President Donald Trump carried by 8 points in 2024. The district has been held by Republicans since 2011 and covers parts of Bexar, Medina, and Uvalde counties.

Nugent's candidacy brings immediate national attention to what would otherwise be a sleepy open-seat race. As the son of Luci Baines Johnson, LBJ's younger daughter, Nugent represents the next generation of the Johnson political dynasty that dominated Texas politics for much of the 20th century.

"The Johnson name still carries weight in Texas, but this isn't 1964 anymore," said Mark Jones, a political science professor at Rice University. "An R+8 district means Nugent needs to win over roughly 10 percent of Trump voters while holding Democrats. That's a steep climb for anyone, even someone with this kind of political pedigree."

Nugent was born on June 21, 1967, while his grandfather was still in office, making him part of the first family during the final months of LBJ's presidency. He graduated from the University of Texas School of Law in 1992 and has built a career representing renewable energy companies and land conservation groups.

His entrance into the race sets up what could be a competitive Democratic primary. State Rep. Diego Bernal, who represents a San Antonio House district, is also considering a bid and has been making calls to local Democratic activists, according to three people familiar with the conversations.

On the Republican side, state Sen. Pete Flores and former congressional aide Brandon Herrera have both filed exploratory committees. The district's Republican lean has attracted a crowded field, with at least five potential candidates actively fundraising.

Nugent's moderate positioning could help him in a general election but may create challenges in a Democratic primary where progressive activists have gained influence. His environmental law background could appeal to suburban voters concerned about climate change, while his stated intention to join the Blue Dog Coalition signals fiscal moderation.

"I've spent my career finding common ground between business interests and environmental protection," Nugent said. "That's the same approach I'll take to Congress – pragmatic solutions that actually get things done."

The Democratic Congressional Campaign Committee has included the district on its expanded target list for 2026, though party officials acknowledge it remains an uphill battle. The district's Republican tilt means Nugent would need to outperform typical Democratic candidates by significant margins while likely facing a well-funded Republican opponent.

"Texas is changing, and districts like this one are becoming more competitive," said DCCC spokesperson Sarah Russell. "Patrick Nugent's unique profile and moderate approach could make this race very interesting."

Nugent has hired veteran Democratic consultant Jeff Link, who has worked on numerous Texas campaigns, suggesting he plans to mount a serious bid. Campaign finance reports show Nugent contributed $250,000 of his own money to seed the campaign, with additional fundraising events planned in Austin and Dallas next month.

The primary is scheduled for March 3, 2026, with the general election set for November 3.
