Found Charles Edward North in St. Louis! He worked for the Missouri Pacific Railroad as a conductor from 1895-1930. Married a woman named Margaret O'Sullivan in 1898 and had 4 kids - all girls! Mary, Catherine, Margaret, and Elizabeth.

He lived at 4523 Arsenal Street in South St. Louis. His obituary from 1942 says he was "loved by all who rode his trains" and that he never missed a day of work in 35 years. Buried at Calvary Cemetery.

Interesting - all his daughters became nuns! Three joined the Sisters of St. Joseph, one joined the Ursuline order. Must have been a very devout Catholic family.

Samuel Houston North - what a character! He shows up in the 1900 census in Denton County, Texas, listed as a "cattleman." By 1910, he owned 2,000 acres and had 500 head of cattle.

Married a Mexican woman named Maria Gonzalez in 1902 (scandalous for the time!). They had 8 kids - 5 boys, 3 girls. Named them all Texas-themed: Austin, Dallas, Houston, Travis, Lamar, Texas Ann, Dallas Marie, and Houston Lee.

He died in 1945 when he was thrown from a horse at age 68. His ranch is still in the family - the North Ranch in Denton County is now run by his great-grandson. They do guided hunting tours!

Rachel Flanders' family is FASCINATING! The Schatz bakery was just the beginning. Rachel's mother was Wilhelmina "Minnie" Flanders, daughter of Belgian immigrants who came to Kansas in 1870.

Minnie married Heinrich Schatz (German immigrant) in 1892. Heinrich changed his name to "Henry" and became a naturalized citizen in 1895 - the same year Rachel was born!

But here's the kicker - Rachel had 3 older brothers:

  • Henry Jr. (1893) - Took over the bakery, expanded to 3 locations
  • William (1894) - Moved to Wichita, became a flour miller
  • Charles (1896) - Moved to Denver, opened a chain of bakeries

Rachel was the baby and only girl. The family was worth quite a bit of money - Henry Sr. left an estate worth $50,000 when he died in 1935 (huge money then!). Rachel inherited a quarter share.

The Flanders family were lace makers from Bruges, Belgium! Jean-Baptiste Flanders and his wife Marie came to Kansas in 1870 with $5 in their pockets. Jean-Baptiste worked on the railroad for 3 years, saved enough to buy a small farm, and became successful growing wheat.

Minnie Flanders (Rachel's mom) was born on that farm in 1873. She had 6 brothers and 2 sisters - the family was HUGE. Most stayed in Kansas, but some went to Nebraska and Colorado.

Rachel's Belgian cousins owned a successful chocolate shop in Topeka called "Flanders Chocolates" that operated until 1960. The recipes were passed down through the family - I bet Rachel grew up with the best baked goods in Kansas!

u/MadisonvilleMarriageRecords Kentucky Archives • 5 hours ago

Found Sarah! Sarah Elizabeth Morrison, born 1895 in Madisonville. She was 22 when she married 47-year-old Robert Sr. in 1917. Here's the scandal - she was his housekeeper!

Sarah's family were Scottish immigrants who came to Kentucky in 1850. Her father died in a mining accident when she was 12, and her mother took in washing to support 6 kids. Sarah went to work at age 14 as a domestic servant.

She started working for Robert Sr. in 1915, two years after his wife Jean died. The 1920 census shows them married, living in Fort Wayne, with Sarah listed as "wife" not "housekeeper."

But here's the sad part - Sarah never had children. Not with Robert Sr., not with anyone. She died in 1968 at age 73, still married to Robert Sr. (who died in 1955). She outlived him by 13 years and never remarried.

She's buried next to Robert Sr. in Fort Wayne, but her headstone just says "Sarah E. North" - no dates, no "beloved wife," nothing. Almost like she was erased from history.

This was actually pretty common! Widowers marrying their housekeepers was a huge thing in the 1910s-1920s. The housekeeper already knew the house, the routines, maybe even helped raise the kids.

But 25 years age difference was extreme even for then. Sarah was younger than Robert Jr. by 3 years! Imagine - your stepmother is younger than you are.

Found Sarah's employment records - she worked for Robert Sr. for 2 years before they married. She got room and board plus $3 a week. When they married, Robert Sr. was making $18 a week at the brick company. She literally went from servant to wife overnight.

u/GermanGenealogyExpert Immigration Records • 4 hours ago

Jean Marcia Schatz's family! Heinrich Schatz arrived from Bavaria in 1883 at age 19, sailing into Baltimore on the SS Main. He had $20 in his pocket and spoke no English.

He worked as a baker's assistant in Baltimore for 2 years, then moved to Topeka in 1885 when the railroad was hiring. He worked on the railroad during the day and baked bread at night, saving enough to open his own bakery in 1888.

Jean's mother was Maria Anna Weber, also from Bavaria. She came over in 1886 to marry Heinrich - it was an arranged marriage! They had 6 kids total:

  • Anna (1887) - Married a farmer, moved to Nebraska
  • Heinrich Jr. (1889) - Took over the bakery
  • Maria (1891) - Died of tuberculosis at age 19
  • Wilhelmina "Minnie" (1893) - Married Henry Flanders, had Rachel
  • Karl (1895) - Moved to Chicago, became a butcher
  • Louisa (1897) - Became a nun!

So Rachel had German cousins in Chicago, Nebraska, and one aunt who was a nun at the Sisters of Charity convent in Leavenworth, Kansas!
