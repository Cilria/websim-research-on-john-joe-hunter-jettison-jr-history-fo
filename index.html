Russian ultranationalists storm Chinese embassy in Moscow as tensions explode

Violent protests erupt across Russian capital as diplomatic relationship collapses into chaos

By CNN International Staff
November 1, 2026 • 11:47 AM ET

Moscow — Tens of thousands of Russian ultranationalists stormed the Chinese embassy in Moscow on Saturday, chanting "Death to the Yellow Menace!" before breaking into the compound and setting it ablaze, marking the most serious escalation in the rapidly deteriorating relationship between the two powers.

The protests, which began peacefully near the embassy gates, quickly descended into violent riots as demonstrators targeted Chinese businesses across the Russian capital, destroying anything perceived as Chinese while police largely stood by.

The attack on the embassy represents a stunning breakdown in diplomatic relations that have been deteriorating since late October, when online sparring between citizens of both countries escalated into government-sanctioned hostility.

Russian state media praised the protesters, with RT calling them "patriots defending Russian dignity" while Chinese state media reacted with unprecedented fury, calling the attacks "barbaric acts of war."

"The Russian government has blood on their hands. They encouraged these animals to attack our embassy. This is an act of war that cannot go unpunished."

Chinese authorities quickly organized massive protests outside the Russian embassy in Beijing, with demonstrators calling for immediate retaliation and the expulsion of all Russian citizens.

The violence spread rapidly through Moscow's business districts, with Russian mobs systematically targeting Chinese restaurants, shops, and businesses. Eyewitnesses reported seeing protesters armed with bats and Molotov cocktails while police failed to intervene effectively.

"They were like animals," said one Chinese restaurant owner who fled the violence. "They smashed everything with hammers, poured gasoline, and set it on fire. They didn't care who was inside."

Diplomatic Crisis Deepens

Both governments have now recalled their ambassadors as the crisis threatens to spiral into military confrontation. Chinese military units have been put on high alert along the Russian border.

Chinese President Xi Jinping convened an emergency meeting of the National Security Commission, while Russian President Vladimir Putin praised the protesters for showing "Russian strength and resolve."

International observers expressed shock at the speed of the deterioration, noting that the China-Russia partnership had been considered one of the most stable geopolitical relationships of the past decade.

CNN Analysis

The embassy attack represents a catastrophic failure of diplomacy that could reshape global geopolitics. With both governments now encouraging nationalist fervor rather than restraint, the world may be witnessing the collapse of the China-Russia alliance and the emergence of a dangerous new Cold War in Asia.

As night fell in Moscow, the Chinese embassy continued to burn while Russian ultranationalists celebrated what they called "the beginning of the liberation of Russia from Chinese influence."

In Beijing, massive crowds gathered outside the Russian embassy, throwing rocks and calling for war. Chinese authorities seemed unwilling or unable to control the growing anti-Russian sentiment.

The world now watches nervously as two nuclear powers slide toward confrontation, their leaders seemingly powerless to stop the nationalist forces they helped unleash.
