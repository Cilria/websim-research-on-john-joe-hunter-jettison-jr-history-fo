As a professor who's written about diaspora families, the "different stories to different people" thing now makes TOTAL sense.

Artur was literally 22 when he fled the genocide. He'd seen his parents murdered, his village destroyed, and his siblings scattered across continents. The "Saint Petersburg teenager" story was his way of coping - he wanted to be from someplace strong and powerful, not a destroyed village.
