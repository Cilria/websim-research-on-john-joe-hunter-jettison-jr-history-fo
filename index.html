Salisbury and Victoria Islands: The lost South Atlantic civilization that could have changed everything

Everyone always talks about alternate history with big landmasses - what if Atlantis existed, what if Zealandia never sank, etc. But what about the smaller but crucial landmasses that could have completely changed colonial history?

I'm talking about Salisbury and Victoria Islands - two substantial islands located in the South Atlantic between South America and Africa. They're positioned roughly south of Cape Verde and north of Ascension Island, making them the perfect mid-Atlantic stopping point.

The larger island (Salisbury) is roughly the size of Crete, while Victoria is about half that size. The climate is subtropical - think Madeira or the Azores. These islands would have been absolute game-changers for:

  • Atlantic slave trade routes (unfortunately)
  • Transatlantic cables and communications
  • WW2 naval strategy
  • Modern air travel routes

Instead of being a 3,000+ mile jump from Brazil to Liberia, you'd have a perfect stepping stone right in the middle. The implications are absolutely massive.
