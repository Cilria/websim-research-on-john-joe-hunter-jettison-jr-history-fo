Just found out my great-great-grandfather Joseph Granger was a Georgia State Senator who lived from 1873-1960 and I'm absolutely floored

So I've been doing some family tree research and just discovered that my great-great-grandfather, Joseph "Joe" Milton Granger Sr., lived from December 29th, 1873 to July 20th, 1960. This motherfucker was born during Reconstruction and died the year Kennedy got elected.

But here's where it gets absolutely wild - this wasn't some random farmer. His dad, Robert Alexander Granger Jr., was a big fucking deal in Washington, Georgia. Dude owned a general store, was elected Justice of the Peace NINE TIMES from 1856-1885, then served in the Georgia State House starting in 1886. Like, this family had political clout before most people had indoor plumbing.

Joseph grew up in a family of 12 surviving kids (9 girls, 3 boys). One of his brothers, William, died of typhoid fever in 1890 when Joseph was 17. Then in 1894 he moved to Athens to become a lawyer, passed the bar in 1898, and formed a partnership with his cousin James S. Bradford.

In 1911 he married Sarah Susan Crawford (daughter of his law partner Frank Stephen Crawford - small fucking town), had 5 kids in 13 years, then served in the Georgia State Senate from 1922-1935. After that? Just went back to practicing law and turned down multiple offers to run for Governor because he was "making good enough money."

This man lived through Reconstruction, the Gilded Age, both World Wars, the Great Depression, the Civil Rights movement was starting, and died the same year as the first televised presidential debates. My brain is exploding.

Adding to this - Robert Alexander Granger Jr.'s parents (Joseph's grandparents) were Robert Alexander Granger Sr. (1809-1867) and Mary Elizabeth Wilkes (1812-1890).

Robert Sr. owned 15 slaves according to the 1860 census and was worth $8,000 - that's like $250k today. He died in 1867 so Joseph never knew him, but the family lost everything during Reconstruction. That's why Robert Jr. had to build up the general store from scratch.

Mary Elizabeth Wilkes was from a prominent family - her father was a Revolutionary War veteran who got land grants in Georgia. She lived until 1890 so Joseph would have known his grandmother until he was 17. Family lore says she was a tough old bird who never forgave the Yankees.

The Wilkes family connection is probably how Robert Jr. got into politics - the Wilkes had been in Georgia politics since the 1790s.

u/DepressionEraStories Oral History • 1 hour ago

My grandmother used to tell stories about growing up in the Granger household during the Depression. Joseph was still practicing law but nobody had any money, so he'd get paid in chickens, vegetables, or moonshine.

She said the house at 485 Cobb Street was always full of cousins and "stray relatives" who'd lost their farms. Sarah Susan would feed 15 people on a chicken and some vegetables. The kids all shared rooms and the grandparents lived with them too.

What struck me most - Joseph kept detailed diaries from 1894 until 1958. Every single day, 64 years of entries. The family donated them to UGA archives. They're apparently a goldmine for historians because he wrote about everything - local politics, the Depression, both World Wars, Civil Rights.

Last entry was July 19, 1958: "Hot today. Court was slow. James brought the children by. They are growing so fast. Sometimes I feel the world has passed me by, but then I remember I have seen more change than any man has a right to witness."

He died two years later. The diaries are apparently 40,000+ pages.
