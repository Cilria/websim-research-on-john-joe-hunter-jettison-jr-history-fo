Arab forces seize Shahrekord and tighten noose on Hamedan

Operation Nahavand enters second month with fresh breakthroughs and supply strains
By Najwa al-Rashid in Beirut

Arab forces captured Shahrekord on 2 June after a four-day battle led by the 7th Motorised and 2nd Armoured divisions, opening the southern route toward Isfahan. General Khalid Mohammed Bin Hussien immediately ordered a logistical pause, privately warning staff he has “barely fifty days of fuel left” while dispatching the 2nd Army Group under Major-General Hussien Abualraham Bin Muhammad toward Yasuj in a pincer designed to link with Bushehr-based columns now pushing north toward Shiraz.

North and centre

Army Command North completed the encirclement of Hamedan last week, triggering street-to-street fighting that has already displaced half the city’s population. Simultaneously Army Command Centre seized Malayer and is probing the outer defences of Arak, Iran’s largest industrial hub west of Tehran.

Further north, Major-General Ahmed Al-Rashid’s task-force took Sanandaj on 8 June, tightening the strategic encirclement of Iranian forces in Kurdistan and securing the flank of the thrust toward Tabriz.

Speaking to commanders in Izeh on 5 June, Muhammad Khalid al-Harbi instructed them to keep “maximum pressure” on Iranian units and deny the enemy “a single second of rest”. A further 300,000 recruits have been inducted since late May, lifting Arab combat strength inside Iran to 2.5 million men.

Air-force chiefs claim 80 per cent of Iran’s fixed-wing fleet has been destroyed on the ground, allowing Arab strike aircraft to range almost at will over the Zagros. Yet logistics officers privately caution that fuel, spare parts and bridging equipment are running dangerously low, a constraint that could slow the timetable for the final push toward Tehran before winter.
